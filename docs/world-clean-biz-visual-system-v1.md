# World Clean Biz Visual System V1.1

World Clean Biz is not a consumer media site, a product review site, or an AI content site.

Its positioning is:

```text
Global Cleaning Industry Intelligence Platform
```

Images must support:

```text
Identification
Judgment
Evidence
```

They should not be used merely for:

```text
Decoration
Technology atmosphere
AI art style
```

## Image Priority

Use article images in this order:

```text
Level 1: Real brand logos
Level 2: Real founder / CEO photos
Level 3: Real product photos
Level 4: Real exhibition / event photos
Level 5: WCB-generated article data visuals
Level 6: Industry landscape infographics
Level 7: AI-generated images
```

Rule:

```text
Use real assets whenever possible.
Use AI-generated images only as a fallback.
```

### Template E: Data Visual


```text

```

Data-visual images should be generated from the data already used in the article.

Do not use annual report, investor presentation, prospectus, SEC, or IR screenshots as the default final image. Use those sources only to verify:

```text

Date accuracy
Company identity
Source label
Context
```

Layout:

```text
Title
Chart
Key Insight
Key Numbers
Source / unit label
```

The key insight should be data-specific, not slogan-like.

Prefer:

```text

```

Avoid:

```text

```

Visual role:

```text
Body evidence image
Standalone data card
Reusable Template B module
Social / newsletter excerpt
```

Template E should feel closer to CB Insights, Visual Capitalist, and Bloomberg Intelligence than to an Excel chart.

## Cover Quality Rule

Priority order:

```text
1. Real logo
2. Real founder photo
3. Real product photo
4. Real industry data
5. Real event photography
```

Only use AI-generated visuals if no real asset exists.

The cover must communicate within 3 seconds:

```text
Who?
What company?
What product?
What is the key insight?
```

If the image looks like a company brochure, a marketing flyer, or a landing-page hero, revise it toward a business media cover.

## Cover System V2 Refinement

When a cover is already close to Bloomberg / Fortune / CB Insights style, do not redesign from scratch. Improve information hierarchy and editorial quality.

### Hierarchy

The intended reading order is:

```text
Logo
Company / brand name
Category label
Headline
Analysis summary
Key data
Product
CEO / founder
World Clean Biz branding
Research issue number
```

Brand recognition should happen before headline recognition.

### Headline

If the headline dominates the page, reduce headline size by 20-30%.

The headline should carry the argument, but the company identity should be immediately visible first.

For brand-analysis covers, prefer a two-layer title when useful:

```text
MAYTRONICS

The Next iRobot?
```

The company is the subject. The headline is the question or argument.

### Logo

Increase logo presence when needed. The reader should identify the company before reading the headline.

### Product

For product disruption and category-change stories, the product must be a primary visual anchor.

Target visual balance:

```text
Product: 40%
CEO: 30%
Text: 20%
Data / labels: 10%
```

Make the product larger than the CEO when the article is about product disruption or category change. It may partially break layout boundaries. Add realistic shadow or ground contact so it does not look pasted or floating.

### Evidence Labels

Avoid generic marketing labels such as:

```text
Global Pool Cleaning Leader
Strategic Signal
```

Prefer concrete evidence:

```text
Market Leader Since 1983
$500M+ Revenue
Largest Installed Base
40+ Years Market Leadership
Global Pool Category Leader
```

Use real numbers whenever possible. Numbers are stronger than adjectives.

If an exact number is not verified, use a defensible time-based or source-based statement, such as:

```text
Dolphin Platform Since 1983
40+ Years In Pool Robotics
```

Use data anchors such as:

```text
40+ Years
Global Leader
$500M+ Revenue
Since 1983
```

Only use financial numbers after source verification.

### Industry Intelligence Layer

Add research-publication cues where appropriate:

```text
POOL ROBOTICS
INDUSTRY INTELLIGENCE
ISSUE NOTE 2026
SPECIAL REPORT
```

The cover should feel like a professional industry intelligence report, not a corporate brochure, marketing flyer, investor pitch deck, startup landing page, or AI-generated poster.

### CEO / Founder Label

The CEO or founder label should identify the person, not dominate the cover.

Keep the person name smaller than the company name and headline unless the cover is explicitly a founder profile.

### Research Number

Every final cover should include a small research identifier when possible:

```text
WCB-2026-006
Pool Robotics
Brand Analysis
```

This gives the image a research-institution feel and helps a large article archive look systematic.

## Body Image System

Each article should use:

```text
1 cover image
3-6 evidence blocks
```

Avoid:

```text
Decorative images
Images without information value
```

Every image must answer:

```text
What information does the reader gain from this image?
```

### Evidence Block 1: People

Use for:

```text
CEO
Founder
Key executive
```

Format:

```text
Portrait
Name
Title
```

### Evidence Block 2: Product

Use for:

```text
Product analysis
Technology analysis
Category analysis
```

Format:

```text
Real product photo
Caption
```

### Evidence Block 3: Data

Use for:

```text

```

## Prohibited Image Types

Do not use:

```text
Generic AI robot
Blue neon robot
Future sci-fi robot
Random factory render
Random 3D illustration
Decorative AI image
Image without information value
```

## Final Standard

After seeing an image, readers should immediately understand:

```text
Which company
Which product
Which founder
What judgment
What evidence
```

They should not think:

```text
This image looks good, but I do not know what it is saying.
```

This standard applies to future World Clean Biz articles, blog pages, reports, and industry research visuals.
