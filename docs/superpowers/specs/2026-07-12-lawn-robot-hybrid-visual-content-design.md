# Lawn Robot Product + Decision Hybrid Visual Content Design

## Objective

Reduce the amount of continuously visible text on the robotic lawn mower sourcing landing page while preserving its strongest industry-opportunity judgments. Product imagery creates desire; diagrams and comparison tools communicate the argument quickly; detailed reasoning remains available through progressive disclosure.

## Approved direction

Use the Product + Decision Hybrid approach.

- Retain the existing Precision Industrial Editorial visual system.
- Keep the product selector as the page's emotional and commercial center.
- Add a six-platform opportunity landscape.
- Add a platform-to-channel fit matrix.
- Convert repeated decision copy into a compact evidence flow.
- Keep ten detailed industry FAQs collapsed by default.
- Reduce continuously visible body copy by approximately 40–50% without removing substantive buyer guidance.

## Evidence standard

All charts are qualitative editorial judgment, not measured market data.

- Label visual assessments as `World Clean Biz editorial assessment`.
- Use language such as `Strong fit`, `Conditional fit`, `Focused fit`, `Entry position`, `Premium position` and `Professional use`.
- Do not use numeric scores, percentages, market size, growth rates, rankings or implied statistical precision without verified source data.
- Include a concise note explaining that final product and supplier decisions require target-market evidence.

## Module design

### 1. Hero

Keep the approved opportunity-led Hero and existing product scene. Limit the supporting paragraph to one concise commercial argument. The Hero continues to establish that buyers should choose the market opportunity before choosing the factory.

### 2. Buyer decision strip

Retain the Enter, Expand, Differentiate and De-risk buyer positions. Keep them as short ruled statements with no additional explanatory paragraphs.

### 3. Product selector

Keep the six-model interaction, product IDs, main-image-to-thumbnail hierarchy and CTA tracking.

For the selected model, show:

- large product image;
- one short `Market opportunity` statement;
- compact lawn, market and channel tags;
- one `Why it can win` statement;
- one `Critical proof points` statement;
- product-specific CTA and custom-brief CTA.

No additional long product paragraphs are added.

### 4. Six-platform opportunity landscape

Add a two-axis qualitative map containing all six platforms.

- Horizontal axis: `Accessible entry` to `Premium product and service value`.
- Vertical axis: `Broad residential adoption` to `Specialist and professional use`.
- Plot RM-01 through RM-06 as labeled product-platform markers.
- Use accessible colors, direct labels and a visible editorial-assessment note.
- Provide a short accessible text summary for screen readers.

The landscape replaces repeated prose explaining where each platform competes.

### 5. Platform-to-channel fit matrix

Add a comparison matrix covering:

- Retail
- Ecommerce
- Specialist dealer
- Private label
- Professional/project sales

Rows represent the six product platforms. Cells use qualitative labels:

- `Strong fit`
- `Conditional fit`

Use a dash for channels that are not a natural primary route. Do not use dots that could be interpreted as a numeric score without a legend.

The matrix replaces repeated channel-fit explanations across the page.

### 6. Market-success conditions

Retain the three conditions that determine whether an opportunity can scale:

- clear customer problem;
- repeatable field performance;
- channel economics that survive returns and service.

Reduce each to a headline plus one short supporting sentence. Use simple technical visual cues or cropped product-detail imagery only when an existing approved image supports the point. Do not generate decorative pseudo-data charts.

### 7. Industry judgment profile

Keep Denny's real photograph and approved identity facts. Reduce the visible copy to one concise judgment statement explaining why a convincing platform can still fail commercially. Preserve the section as human evidence, not a generic biography.

### 8. Evidence flow

Combine the existing buyer outcomes, process and decision framework into a single four-stage visual flow:

1. `Opportunity` — Is the customer problem valuable?
2. `Platform` — Does field performance prove the promise?
3. `Supplier` — Can capability survive scale?
4. `Economics` — Does margin survive after the sale?

Each stage contains one short question and up to three evidence labels. The flow communicates a go/no-go sequence rather than a service process.

### 9. Industry FAQ

Keep all ten approved industry-opportunity questions. All details remain closed on initial page load.

- No answer text is continuously visible until the user opens a question.
- Preserve keyboard and screen-reader behavior.
- Keep answers detailed enough for serious buyers.
- Do not reintroduce questions about company identity, contact, forms, factory-name disclosure or generic collaboration workflow.

### 10. Final CTA

Keep the opportunity-to-product-brief message. Use a short paragraph requesting the target customer, lawn environment, channel, price position and reason to win.

## Image strategy

- Use the six existing robotic mower concept images in the selector and opportunity modules.
- Use Denny's existing real event photograph in the industry-judgment section.
- Do not add generic stock photography.
- Do not create images that imply a specific verified factory model, certification or supplier relationship.
- Any future real product replacements must preserve the current product ID and aspect-ratio system.

## Responsive behavior

### Desktop

- Opportunity landscape renders as a full-width two-axis map.
- Channel-fit matrix remains a table-like comparison surface.
- Evidence flow renders as four connected stages.

### Mobile

- Product selector keeps one large image and horizontally scrollable thumbnails.
- Opportunity landscape becomes a vertically stacked labeled map or ordered platform list without horizontal overflow.
- Channel matrix becomes horizontally scrollable with a visible `Swipe to compare` hint; the first column remains understandable without relying on hover.
- Evidence flow stacks vertically in decision order.
- FAQ remains collapsed.

## Scope and constraints

- Modify only the lawn robot landing page, lawn product selector and lawn-specific styles/data.
- Preserve page routing, SEO metadata, product IDs, image paths, form behavior and GA4 parameters.
- Preserve the pool robot page.
- Preserve unrelated pages, shared navigation, footer and articles.
- Do not add third-party charting dependencies; use semantic HTML and CSS for these qualitative visualizations.

## Validation

- Six platforms appear in the opportunity landscape and channel matrix.
- Every visual assessment includes an editorial-assessment label or legend.
- No numeric scores or unsupported market statistics are introduced.
- All ten FAQ items are closed on initial load and open individually.
- RM-01 through RM-06 selection and product-specific CTA tracking remain functional.
- Desktop and mobile have no horizontal page overflow.
- The sourcing verification script and production build pass.
- The pool robot page contains none of the lawn-only visual modules.
