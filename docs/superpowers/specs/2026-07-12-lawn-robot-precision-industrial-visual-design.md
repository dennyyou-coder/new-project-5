# Lawn Robot Precision Industrial Editorial Visual Design

## Objective

Polish the robotic lawn mower sourcing landing page into an international B2B industrial editorial experience that makes product selection, buyer risk, expert judgment and the final inquiry path feel credible and commercially focused.

## Approved direction

- Deep navy, mineral gray, cold white and restrained electric blue.
- Strong editorial hierarchy with thin rules, compact radii and minimal shadow.
- The product selector is the page's strongest visual moment: a dominant main image, compact model rail and structured product facts.
- Audience, risks, deliverables, process and decision framework use numbered editorial structures instead of repeated floating cards.
- Denny's section reads as an editorial expert profile, not a testimonial card.
- FAQ uses crisp ruled disclosures; the final CTA closes with a strong dark band.
- Mobile uses tighter spacing and fewer oversized card surfaces.

## Constraints

- Do not change English copy, section order, product data, form behavior or GA4 tracking.
- Do not generate new imagery; use the existing mower images and Denny photograph.
- Scope the visual system to `/sourcing/lawn-robots` only. The pool robot page must remain unchanged.
- Preserve the approved main-image-to-thumbnail hierarchy and responsive product selection behavior.

## Validation

- Automated source guard confirms the lawn-only page class and visual tokens.
- Existing sourcing SEO and funnel verification passes.
- Production build passes.
- Desktop and mobile browser QA cover the hero, selector, lower-page sections, model switching and FAQ disclosure.
- Pool robot page is checked for visual leakage.
