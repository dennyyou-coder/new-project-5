## Commercial Robotic Vacuums

Commercial robotic vacuums can leverage the underlying algorithm logic from domestic models, making the overall transition not particularly challenging for companies like Roborock. Switching over would be relatively quick. The primary issue with this product line is its limited market size; achieving even a few hundred million in annual sales is already considered impressive. Additionally, the sales channels required for commercial products are entirely different from those of domestic models, necessitating the rebuilding of teams and networks.

## Analysis of Roborock's Prospects in Cordless Vacuum Cleaner Market

From a product perspective, the craftsmanship of Roborock’s cordless vacuum cleaner is top-notch, with significant investment in mold costs (estimated at 6-7 million RMB per model). The company also dares to explore new technologies, being the first to use soft-pack lithium batteries. Overall, Roborock's research and development capabilities in cordless handheld vacuums are leading among most companies.

Can Roborock replicate its success in robot vacuum cleaners with these two cordless models?

My personal answer is likely no.

The products themselves, quality, and features are all up to par. The market potential is substantial (the cordless vacuum cleaner market might even be larger than the robot vacuum cleaner market). What then hinders Roborock's development?

The biggest challenge lies in external factors. When Roborock first launched its LiDAR navigation robot vacuum, similar high-end products from iRobot and Ecovacs were priced around 3,000 RMB. By offering a model at just 1,599 RMB under the Mi brand, Roborock quickly captured market attention.

However, the current cordless market has changed. Although Dyson's top-tier models still retail for over 5,000 RMB, most high-end brushless motor products are priced between 1,299 to 1,599 RMB. Introducing a model at 2,599 RMB would struggle to gain market acceptance.

Moreover, the market environment is constantly evolving. In this period, rapid feedback and timely product launches that suit the current market conditions are crucial. Failure to establish a foothold in such a dynamic market could make future development challenging.

If Roborock cannot launch several key models within the 1,299-1,599 RMB price range, it will likely struggle in the cordless vacuum cleaner market.

Alternatively, if Roborock aims to focus on the high-end market and quietly establish itself as a premium brand, that could be another viable strategy.
