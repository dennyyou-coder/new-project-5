### Can the Small Appliance Industry Realize a "One Power" Battery Sharing Ecosystem?


#### 

The "One Power" (universal battery) model has been successfully validated in the power tool sector—single battery packs can support dozens of products, allowing consumers to save 30-50% on purchase costs. For brands, this increases customer stickiness.

TTI and Greenworks have attempted to transplant this model into the home appliance sector but with limited success. The core challenge lies in the strong mobility requirements for power tools versus the predominantly fixed-use scenarios for traditional appliances.

The prerequisite for a universal battery is electrification. Currently, only 15% of small appliances are electrified, primarily focusing on cleaning devices (vacuum cleaners, floor washers, robotic vacuums) and personal care products (electric toothbrushes, hair removal devices). Only some kitchen appliances have undergone partial electrification.

From a product type perspective, small appliances can be categorized into two major groups:

1. **Electrical Appliances** (motor-driven): Devices like vacuum cleaners, which are highly mobile, will likely complete their electrification within 3-5 years. Less mobile electrical appliances such as fans, coffee makers, and air purifiers have some demand but overall do not provide strong incentives for electrification.

2. **Heating Appliances** (heat-generating devices): These are constrained by high energy consumption and require advancements in both heating technology and battery technology to enable electrification. Even if these technologies improve, most heating appliances lack mobility requirements, making their electrification unlikely.

#### 

1. The path toward full electrification of small appliances is long, with cleaning devices likely to achieve it sooner.
2. If heat issues are resolved, appliances like rice cookers, air fryers, and ovens may not have sufficient motivation for electrification due to minimal mobility needs.
3. Assuming true electrification, what form would the batteries take? Given that the design of small appliances is a key selling point, battery pack designs will also be important. However, from a cost perspective, cylindrical lithium-ion cells are likely to dominate.
4. Within 5 years, focusing on centralized battery packs is unlikely to avoid significant losses. When might it be a viable opportunity? Watching major players like Midea, Xinbao, Shark, and SEB for their moves could provide insights.

# Lithium-Ionization of Small Appliances: A Long Road Ahead

During the pandemic, camping scenarios briefly propelled a surge in lithium-ion small appliances; however, with the increase in electric vehicles and the use of high-capacity portable power stations (such as Eppu and Electric Two), 78% of users prefer carrying a combination of portable power stations and traditional electrical appliances.

## 1. The Path to Full Lithium-Ionization for Cleaning Appliances

The full lithium-ionization of small appliances is still a long way off, with cleaning appliances potentially leading the charge. It's possible that brands like Dreame, Ecovacs/Tineco, and Roborock might first unify their battery packs internally within cleaning appliances; however, these battery packs are unlikely to be compatible with other household appliances.

## 2. Limited Lithium-Ionization for Heat-Generating Appliances

If the heat issue is resolved in the future, electric rice cookers/air fryers/toasters, which have no significant mobility needs, may not see much motivation for lithium-ionization due to their basic lack of portability requirements.

## 3. What Would Lithium Batteries Look Like?

Assuming full lithium-ionization were to occur, what would the batteries look like? Given that the design of small appliances is a key selling point, battery pack designs will also be crucial. However, from a cost perspective, I believe cylindrical lithium-ion cells are most likely, with different specifications based on power requirements.

## 4. The "Universal Battery" Is Not in Sight for Five Years

Within five years, the concept of a universal battery is unlikely to materialize; otherwise, it would likely result in significant financial losses. If such a universal battery were feasible later, the biggest beneficiaries would be major players like Xiaomi and Midea. If they remain inactive, it's best to keep your wallet safe.
