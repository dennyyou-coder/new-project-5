A few days ago I went to Suzhou and Nanjing on a business trip and took the opportunity to visit the AWE exhibition in Shanghai. I wanted to see if any major manufacturers had released new products.


**Midea**

The wealthy Midea, a company with a market value of thousands of billions, did not skimp on expenses and rented the entire W1 hall. The cost of the booth alone is at least several million yuan.


From the perspective of cleaning appliances, they primarily promoted two dust mite removers, one robot vacuum, and one cordless vacuum cleaner.

**Leck Electrical**

Leading industry player Leck continues to focus on promoting its MagicClean series, launching a new product, the Leck MagicClean M12. It is evident that significant investment has been made in the promotional video for the MagicClean M12. However, the same old question remains: Currently, only Leck and Vorwerk are stillstill insisting on upright stick-style lithium cordless vacuums in the entire AWE exhibition hall. Is this direction correct? If it is not correct, the more effort invested, the greater the potential cost.

**Ecovacs**

Ecovacs has released a robot vacuum equipped with "eyes," claiming it can recognize various objects. At the demonstration, a shoe, a sock, and a power cord were placed on the table. When I held my phone in front of the camera, the recognized object was "Shoe." An Ecovacs representative informed me that currently, only shoes, socks, and power cords can be identified.

![What AWE Reveals About China’s Cleaning Appliance Industry image 7](/images/insights/awe-china-cleaning-appliance-signals-image-007.jpg)

Tek, a brand under Ecovacs Group, has renamed itself Tineco and simultaneously launched an allegedly artificial intelligence-powered cordless vacuum cleaner.

![What AWE Reveals About China’s Cleaning Appliance Industry image 8](/images/insights/awe-china-cleaning-appliance-signals-image-008.jpg)

I checked, this product seems to feature a dust sensor that automatically adjusts the suction power. While this isn't a completely new function, its use in cordless vacuum cleaners appears to be a first. Personally, I believe that this dust sensor will become a standard component in vacuums in the future.

Roborock has released some new products, including a new cyclonic vacuum cleaner that stands out in terms of design and craftsmanship. Roborock's release of this product seems to be aimed primarily at international markets.

**Vorwerk**

Vorwerk also showcased its products at the AWE exhibition. It is evident that the trend towards cordless vacuum cleaners has significantly impacted Vorwerk. The wired models, which previously held an advantage, are no longer on display. Currently, only one upright wireless model and one robot vacuum are being exhibited.

Inquired about the prices, they indeed remain very high-end. The cordless vacuum cleaner costs over 7000 yuan, and the robot vacuum (originally a Neato model) also costs over 7000 yuan.....

Dyson did not anticipate that the wireless trend it initiated would first affect these direct sales companies.

**Shark**

Shark also set up a very upscale exhibition booth, where all of Shark's models from North America were brought in. From the perspective of product transplantation, Shark's speed is significantly faster than other foreign companies. The biggest issue currently might be localizing products for the North American market, as vacuum cleaners there tend to be overly muscular. If Shark could develop a product specifically for the Chinese market, it would pose a significant threat to numerous second- and third-tier brands in the Chinese market.

**Sauber**

In recent years, Sauber has also seen significant growth in its non-core businesses. Previously, some offline channel data showed that it was already among the leaders in certain periods. SEB Group has been rapidly expanding in Europe, capturing market share from Electrolux and Philips. Similarly, Sauber has leveraged its channel advantages to gain a substantial presence in the domestic market.

**Sharp**

Sharp has released a lightweight product with the entire machine weighing only 1.5KG (the main unit weighs 1.1KG). The weight of current handheld vacuum cleaners is a concern, and light weighting is also a direction.

**Huawei**

Huawei launched the Hi Link ecosystem at this exhibition. From an industrial design perspective, the products in Huawei's ecosystem are already on par with those from Xiaomi's ecosystem. If the products from these parties were placed side by side, it would be almost impossible to distinguish them. If the industrial design advantages of enterprises in Xiaomi's ecosystem are caught up by traditional enterprises, then where does the advantage of Xiaomi's ecosystem lie?
