## Private label starts before factory comparison

Many buyers start private label sourcing by asking for supplier lists. That can help, but it is rarely the best first step. A better starting point is understanding product positioning, target channel, feature expectations, compliance requirements, and after-sales responsibility.

## Key questions

- Is the buyer looking for OEM customization or ODM speed?
- Which target market and price tier matter most?
- What level of packaging, testing, and documentation is required?
- Who handles warranty, spare parts, and product support?
- Does the supplier match the buyer's channel and volume expectations?

## Supplier fit matters

A strong supplier for one buyer may not be suitable for another. Some factories are better for stable volume. Others are better for flexible ODM projects, component specialization, or category depth.

## Better sourcing intelligence

World Clean Biz treats sourcing as an information process first. The goal is to help buyers understand the decision context before choosing who to contact.
