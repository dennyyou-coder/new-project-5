A typical American middle-class family’s swimming pool did not require much technological imagination in the past.

Pool maintenance workers would regularly visit, and professional stores sold equipment. Cleaning robots with cables slowly crawled along the pool floor. This industry was large but quiet. Unlike smartphones,robot vacuum or electric vehicles, it did not constantly generate new product launches, nor did it often feature prominently in the financing stories of Chinese startups.

Now, this slow business has been disrupted.

Wireless technology is making pool robots more akin to consumer electronics. Users can order them on Amazon and see products at stores like Costco, Walmart, or Home Depot; they might also be inspired by a short video. The capabilities that Chinese companies are familiar with—rapid iteration, supply chain compression, online sales, and price point coverage—are suddenly impacting this old industry.

Thus, the previously quiet table is now crowded. Traditional pool equipment companies are defending their channels and service networks, while new Chinese brands bring consumer electronics strategies. Cleaning robot and outdoor robotic companies view pools as part of a backyard robotics ecosystem. They all produce pool robots but bet on different futures.

The complexity of this change lies in the fact that the companies at the table do not come from the same system. Traditional pool equipment companies guard their channels and service networks, Chinese new brands bring consumer electronics approaches, and cleaning robot and outdoor robotic companies see pools as part of a backyard robotics ecosystem. They all produce pool robots but bet on different futures.

![Article image 2](/images/insights/pool-robotics-new-competitive-table-image-002.jpg)

**Maytronics / Dolphin are the Old Kings.** In the pool robot industry, Dolphin has long been an inescapable name. Many users first encountered pool robots through Dolphin. Maytronics also built a deep foundation in professional channels and service networks through this brand. However, the days of the old kings have become less comfortable. In 2025, Maytronics' revenue was approximately RMB 3.4 billion (ILS 1.406 billion). More strikingly, the gross margin for private pool robots dropped from 36.5% in 2024 to 23.9% in 2025. Behind these numbers, there is an increasing number of competitors. Aiper, Beatbot, and WYBOT have already launched attacks from different directions, capturing a portion of the market: Aiper entered through wireless technology and retail channels, Beatbot focused on high-end smart products, while WYBOT relied on manufacturing and cost advantages. Meanwhile, Roborock, Ecovacs, Tineco, Narwal, Dreame, MOVA, Aiper, Beatbot, Maytronics, SharkNinja, iRobot, Dyson, Deerma, Uwant, Anker, Xiaomi, Husqvarna, Positec, and other robot companies have either released or are developing similar products. Their financial, product, and supply chain capabilities are not weak. The pressure on Maytronics has evolved from a single new brand challenge to multiple players entering the market simultaneously. It now has introduced new management with background in supply chain, finance, and global operations, and is emphasizing inventory, cash flow, and returning to core business. The old kings remain, but they must prove their defensive capabilities in a more crowded landscape.

**Fluidra is the Channel Empire.** While users may not remember Fluidra's name, they likely use its products such as pumps, filters, heaters, disinfection equipment, pool covers, cleaning accessories, and commercial pool solutions. In 2025, Fluidra's sales were approximately RMB 17.21 billion (EUR 2.184 billion). Its true value lies in the long-term relationships it has formed with dealers, pool service providers, and existing pool users. The ongoing business of pools is maintenance, repairs, and renovations. In 2025, Fluidra invested approximately RMB 680 million (USD 100 million) to acquire a 27% stake in Aiper. This move resembles how traditional channel empires integrate new engines: Fluidra has the channels, while Aiper brings wireless robots and consumer brand speed.

**Aiper / Aiper Intelligent is a wireless challenger.**

Dolphin represents the old order, while Aiper brings in new play: wireless, one-click start-up, online sales, large retail channels, and rapid iteration. Fluidra disclosed that Aiper's 2024 revenue was approximately RMB 13.3 billion (USD 195 million), a year-on-year increase of 75%. According to the Aiper website, the company has entered over 57 countries and regions with more than 10,000 retail stores. Its product range has expanded from pool bottom cleaning to include wall, line, and surface cleaning. From shipment numbers and market presence, Aiper is already leading many new entrants and is considered Maytronics' strongest competitor. Founder Wang Yang's approach is focused: find a sufficiently niche scenario with the potential for global dominance, then invest resources accordingly. In public interviews, he refers to European and American pool specialty stores as "trust nodes." This statement is worth noting because pool robots ultimately need to enter service and trust networks. Aiper now has an early advantage, funding support, and both online and offline channel foundations. Over the next year, it will be a critical window period for consolidating its leading position. While many new entrants are still busy with lawn mowers or other segments, Aiper needs to quickly turn shipment leadership into brand awareness, channel trust, and industry headship. Its challenge lies here: as sales pick up, issues such as battery life, water resistance, safety, and after-sales service will be magnified. The Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) issued recalls for Aiper-related products in 2023 and 2025, which will long-term test the brand's credibility.

**Beatbot is an upscale startup company.**

Beatbot Innovation initially targeted the high-end market rather than entering through a low-price volume approach. According to Denny’s industry information, Beatbot generated sales of approximately RMB 5 billion in its first year and made significant noise in the high-end pool robot market. This success story highlights stronger pumps, more complex motion control, smarter mapping navigation, and packaging the pool robot as an advanced tech product.

Founder Wang Shengle is a former co-founder of Zhiwei, known in the industry as "Da Le." He is young, direct, and willing to take big risks, having also experienced significant pressure: early Beatbot products were priced high and struggled to sell, forming over RMB 100 million worth of inventory. Later, it was able to break through with independent station sales. Capital has also backed him; in 2025, Beatbot completed approximately RMB 1 billion in financing and plans to invest about RMB 500 million over the next two years to enter lawn mowers.

Beatbot's biggest advantage now is that it has established a foothold in the high-end pool robot market and secured substantial funding support. The major players entering the space have not yet made pool robots their primary focus, giving Beatbot about one year of window time. It needs to leverage this period to turn its high-end noise into more stable channels, product reputation, and scale.

Once the lawn mower market share becomes clearer next year, more robot companies will reallocate resources, increasing pressure on pool robots. For Beatbot, the high-end market is now open; the next step is to prove that it can convert short-term leadership into a long-term position.

**WYBOT is a Manufacturer Stepping into the Spotlight.** Unlike Aiper and Beatbot, which have strong consumer brand presence, WYBOT appears more as a hard-hitting player in the pool robot supply chain. From 2023 to 2025, WYBOT's revenue grew from RMB 378 million to RMB 812 million, and its profit increased from RMB 60.85 million to RMB 100 million; the company has already entered markets in over 60 countries and regions. According toChina Insights Consultancy (Zhoshizhi Consulting), WYBOT is ranked among the global top three and China's number one supplier of pool cleaning robots based on its own product shipment volume and GMV for 2024. Its highlights lie in manufacturing, engineering, cost control, and production capacity. The company has a family enterprise flavor with Fu Guilan serving as chairman and general manager, and Yu Qian as vice-chairman and technical director. On April 2026, WYBOT resubmitted its IPO application to the Hong Kong Stock Exchange. For this company, listing is just aintermediate milestone; the bigger challenge lies in transforming from a manufacturer into a global brand.

Aiper, Beatbot, and WYBOT represent three paths taken by Chinese challengers: branding for volume sales, high-end financing, and manufacturing transitioning to branding. Each of them is challenging the old order but faces different challenges. Aiper needs to build trust with professional channels, Beatbot needs to validate its ability to scale at a premium price point, while WYBOT must transition from supply chain capabilities to brand capabilities.

Larger variables come from cleaning robot and outdoor robot companies. They enter the market with funding, algorithms, supply chains, and experience in category expansion, but pool robots may not follow the logic of floor sweepers or lawn mowers.

**Zhiyi is an offensive giant.** Zhiyi's size has entered the discussion at a 30 billion yuan level, with expansions in floor sweepers, mopping machines, vacuum cleaners, hairdryers, and overseas business. Its entry into pool robots is not surprising. At CES 2026, Zhiyi launched the Zircon2 series of pool robots, emphasizing intelligent perception, autonomous recharging, edge cleaning, and full-pool cleaning. What truly makes competitors nervous about Zhiyi is its consistent offensive strategy: fundraising, marketing, supply chain management, product definition, and multi-category advancement simultaneously. It has completed approximately 3.6 billion yuan in financing with a valuation of around 17 billion yuan; the Zhiyi CVC has also successfully raised the first phase of a 10 billion yuan industrial fund in Shaoxing. Founder Yu Hao's style is aggressive, expanding Zhiyi's boundaries from cleaning appliances to smartphones, cars, major household appliances, and more. Pool robots are not yet Zhiyi's main battlefield; once it decides to invest continuously, the competitive intensity of this category will increase.

**Ecovacs serves as a test case for established service robot companies.** In 2024, Ecovacs reported total revenues of 16.542 billion yuan and was one of the earliest companies to emerge in China's service robotics industry. With funding, brand recognition, distribution channels, and after-sales systems, its interest in pool robots is unsurprising. In 2024, Ecovacs invested 25 million yuan in ZhiOrange Power, laying out a strategy for pool cleaning robots; subsequently, ZhiOrange Technology / ZhiOrange Power announced its closure. This case more neutrally illustrates that complex categories like pool robots are difficult to secure through external investments alone. If a company truly wants to compete long-term in this sector, it will ultimately need to take the field and solidify product definition, waterproof structures, battery safety, supply chains, overseas distribution channels, after-sales maintenance, and other critical aspects.

Ecovacs founder Qian Dongqi has transitioned from vacuum cleaner trade and manufacturing to service robotics; recently, he has taken on a more significant role in home robot business. Ecovacs possesses organizational resources and long-term experience with robots. What is truly worth observing now is whether it will turn this external layout into an ongoing internal investment project.

**Roborock is a stable performer.** Roborock has already achieved industry leadership in the floor sweeper market, being one of the strongest companies in China's cleaning robot sector in terms of product definition and operational efficiency. In 2024, its revenue was 11.945 billion yuan; for the first three quarters of 2025, it generated 12.066 billion yuan in revenue, a year-over-year increase of 72.22%. Its characteristic is a restrained pace, typically entering markets only after thoroughly understanding product opportunities and then using relatively restrained SKU offerings and stable product capabilities. When applied to pool robots, Roborock certainly warrants attention, but its entry may not be particularly early. Industry confirmation shows that Roborock has developed pool robots, with the earliest possible launch date being 2027. Meanwhile, Roborock's pace in the garden robot market is already slightly behind, and it now needs to get its lawn mower product line up and running; therefore, the priority for pool robots may be lower. For Roborock, the issue is not capability but resource prioritization. After Aiper, Beatbot, WYBOT, and other players have accelerated their positioning, whether it will allocate sufficient resources to this relatively niche market with heavier after-sales needs remains to be observed.

**Ninebot's Wildland Continent is a player in the backyard ecosystem.** Ninebot Company reported sales of 14.196 billion yuan in 2024, with robot business revenue amounting to 895 million yuan. Current public statements from Wildland Continent primarily focus on smart lawn mowing robots; users have confirmed that Ninebot has developed pool cleaning robots, which may be launched soon. Founder Wang Ye once mentioned that Ninebot started as a robotics company and later entered consumer hardware through balance vehicles and electric two-wheelers. CEO Ren Guanjiao of Wildland Continent exudes stability, more like a long-term-oriented hardware company: pushing products, channels, supply chains, and organizational structures at their own pace. According to Denny's records, the 1 millionth machine from Wildland Continent has already been produced.

For Wildland Continent, pool cleaning robots are a product line that cannot be abandoned because of the natural coexistence of lawns, pools, and garden maintenance in American backyards. However, with the booming lawn mowing robot market currently at the forefront, this is where their immediate focus lies. Whether Wildland Continent can become the leader in the lawn mowing robot market will determine its position in the backyard robotics ecosystem, even whether it can truly stand firm as a company. Pool cleaning robots could serve as an extension of the ecosystem, but for now, the hardest battle remains with lawn mowing robots.

**Mammotion is an aggressive product-oriented startup.** Mammotion has already entered the pool cleaning robot market from outdoor robotics. Reliable public sales figures are not available; this report does not use unverified numbers. However, in the lawn mowing robot market, industry players generally consider Mammotion as a leading startup. In terms of pool cleaning robots, Mammotion has launched SPINO E1 and introduced SPINO S1 Pro, attempting to integrate pools into their outdoor robotics lineup. Founder Wei Jidong is a former DJI product manager, with high evaluations from industry friends regarding his product capabilities. Mammotion also exhibits strong characteristics of a product-oriented founder: quick decision-making, bold bets, and strong control over product definitions. Over the past few years, Mammotion's aggressive approach has enabled it to achieve results in the lawn mowing robot market. It dares to quickly launch new products and seize global market positions ahead of time. Pool cleaning robots are attractive for Mammotion because lawns, gardens, and pools naturally belong to the same American backyard; however, its primary task over the next year will be to consolidate its position as a leading player in lawn mowing robots. Pool cleaning robots can serve as an extension of their product portfolio, but lawn mowing robots remain the main battlefield that Mammotion must defend.

As Chinese companies continue to flood into this space, traditional pool equipment manufacturers are not standing still. They may not be the best at creating consumer brand awareness, but they control professional channels, dealer relationships, and system sales capabilities.

**Hayward is a traditional equipment gatekeeper.** In 2025, Hayward's net sales are approximately RMB 7.63 billion (USD 1.122 billion). Its product range includes pumps, filters, heaters, sanitizers, automation, lighting, and cleaning equipment; robotic pool cleaners are just part of its comprehensive pool product portfolio. Hayward already offers robotic cleaning products such as AquaVac and TigerShark, including the AquaVac 250Li cordless and AquaVac 650 Wi-Fi models. As a publicly traded company on the US stock market, Hayward has the ability to continue investing and acquiring; its corporate style is more traditional in terms of pool equipment and professional management systems, focusing heavily on channels, distributors, and system sales. Hayward's strengths lie in its complete range of pool equipment and specialized distribution channels, while its weaknesses are lower consumer brand awareness and slower wirelessization compared to new players like Aiper and Beatbot. It remains to be seen whether Hayward will continue competing with its own product lines or seek partnerships, investments, or acquisitions to bolster its wireless robot capabilities.

**BWT / Aquabot is a player in the professional market.** The overall sales figures for BWT are not currently using unverified data, but BWT Robotics / Aquabot has a clear presence in robotic pool cleaning systems: public information indicates that it has over 30 years of experience as an OEM in this field and a customer network spanning more than 25 countries. Unlike Aiper or Beatbot, which rely on consumer brand recognition to drive sales, BWT is more akin to a professional equipment company, emphasizing engineering expertise, commercial scenarios, and reliability. Its product line covers both residential and commercial pools, including models such as F1, Essential, Aquabot, BATbrown cordless, Neptune, and ULTRAMAX commercial. If the consumer market continues to favor lower-cost wireless machines, BWT will need to focus on maintaining its presence in service-intensive and reliability-focused professional markets.

Pools robots appear as a standalone category on the surface but are likely to be integrated into a larger landscape of yard robots in the long term.

In the past, companies like Maytronics could grow independently in the relatively specialized and closed market for pool cleaning. However, this environment is unlikely to persist. Pools robots, lawn mowing robots, and yard cleaning robots are gradually being placed in the same backyard scenario within European and American households to compete. What users will purchase is no longer a single device but an entire suite of backyard automation solutions.

This change will impact the ultimate size of companies in the industry.

If the paths of lawn mowing robots and pool robots converge, leading companies may have the opportunity to achieve billion-dollar sales. By that stage, the market will not accommodate many brands. Whether it is Maytronics as an old king or new forces like Aiper, Beatbot, WYBOT, Mammotion, Ninebot, Roborock, and Xiaomi, only 3-4 companies are likely to survive.

The time window is also narrowing.

It can be anticipated that the direct competition between pool robots and lawn mowing robots will begin in 2027 and gradually yield results by 2028. For players in the pool robot industry, there may be only about a year left before the market consolidates.

During this period, Aiper must turn its lead in shipments into an industry leadership position; Beatbot must convert its high-end buzz into stable scale; WYBOT must prove that manufacturers can truly step forward; Maytronics must defend its professional channels and brand awareness; Wildland Continent, Mammotion, Roborock, and Xiamu, these companies from the yard and cleaning robot sector, will need to decide on the priority of pool robots in their product portfolios.

The brilliance of many players is just the first half.

What truly decides the ranking going forward will be whether companies can stabilize product reliability, channel order, global after-sales service, and capital efficiency within the backyard robot race that encompasses both pools and lawns. This industry will ultimately shift from a battle for pool robots to a consolidation war among leading yard robot companies.
