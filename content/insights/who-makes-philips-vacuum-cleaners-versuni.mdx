**Philips-branded vacuum cleaners and floor-care appliances are developed, marketed and supported by Versuni under a brand license from Royal Philips.** Hillhouse acquired the former Philips Domestic Appliances business in 2021, and that independent business was renamed Versuni in 2023. Royal Philips still owns the PHILIPS trademark; it does not directly own Versuni.

That answers the company question, but not every manufacturing question. Versuni has its own factories and also works with global product and component suppliers. The legal manufacturer, production site and country of origin can therefore differ by model. The seller, importer and warranty provider can also change by market.

For distributors, procurement teams and compliance professionals, the correct answer is a responsibility map—not simply “Philips” or “Versuni.”

## Who Makes Philips Vacuum Cleaners?

At the product-business level, the answer is **Versuni**.

Versuni publicly describes floor care as one of its main product groups. Its official company materials use the Philips SpeedPro Max vacuum cleaner and Philips AquaTrio cordless wet-and-dry vacuum as examples of products developed by the business. A 2025 announcement for the Philips OneUp electric mop provides a more recent example of the Philips floor-care brand continuing within Versuni's portfolio.

The wording still needs care. “Made by Versuni” can mean that Versuni controls product development, commercialization and support. It does not prove that every unit comes from a factory legally owned by Versuni.

Versuni's [2023 Modern Slavery Statement](https://assets.versuni.com/Versuni_Modern_Slavery_Statement_2023_PDF_1fc90275f5.pdf) said the company had manufacturing sites in Brazil, Italy, Romania, India and China, while also sourcing products and components from a global supplier network. That is evidence of a real manufacturing and supply-chain footprint. It is not a model-by-model origin list for Philips vacuum cleaners.

For a specific vacuum, wet-and-dry cleaner or electric mop, the safest formulation is:

> Versuni is the licensed product company behind the Philips domestic-appliances and floor-care business. The exact legal manufacturer and factory depend on the model and production records.

## Who Owns Philips Domestic Appliances?

Hillhouse acquired the Domestic Appliances business from Royal Philips.

On March 25, 2021, Royal Philips announced an agreement to sell the business to Hillhouse Capital at an enterprise value of approximately €3.7 billion. The companies also agreed to a 15-year brand license with an estimated net present value of approximately €0.7 billion, bringing the announced total transaction value to approximately €4.4 billion. Philips' [official transaction briefing](https://www.philips.com/c-dam/corporate/newscenter/global/standard/resources/healthcare/2021/da/philips-media-briefing-transcript-of-introduction-by-ceo-frans-van-houten.pdf) identified Philips, Saeco, Gaggia and Walita among the licensed domestic-appliance brands.

Royal Philips' [2023 Annual Report](https://www.results.philips.com/publications/ar23/downloads/files/en/PhilipsFullAnnualReport2023-English.pdf) says the sale was completed on September 1, 2021. The final accounting consideration included closing adjustments, so it should not be confused with the values announced when the agreement was signed.

The acquired company initially continued as Philips Domestic Appliances. On February 17, 2023, it announced the new corporate name **Versuni**. The [Versuni name-change announcement](https://www.versuni.com/newsroom/philips-domestic-appliances-becomes-versuni/) states that the change followed its separation from Royal Philips and sale to Hillhouse, while the Philips consumer brand would continue under license.

Hillhouse also described Versuni in a [March 2025 company profile](https://www.hillhouseinvestment.com/2025/02/21/veronique-pauwels-cultivating-versunis-legacy-of-innovation/) as the global household-appliances company it acquired from Royal Philips in 2021. This is the latest official ownership statement reviewed for this article.

The sequence is therefore:

| Date | Event | Meaning today |
|---|---|---|
| March 25, 2021 | Royal Philips agreed to sell Domestic Appliances to Hillhouse | Hillhouse acquired the product business, not the PHILIPS trademark |
| September 1, 2021 | Sale completed | Domestic Appliances became independent from Royal Philips |
| February 17, 2023 | Philips Domestic Appliances announced the Versuni name | The company identity changed; licensed Philips products continued |
| March 2025 | Hillhouse publicly described its 2021 Versuni acquisition | Latest official public ownership confirmation reviewed for this article |

## Does Royal Philips Still Own the Philips Name?

Yes. Royal Philips still owns the PHILIPS trademark.

For example, the Philips Home [UK terms of use](https://www.home-appliances.philips/gb/en/legal/terms-of-use) identify Koninklijke Philips N.V. as the owner of the registered PHILIPS trademark while identifying Versuni Nederland B.V. as the company offering the site. Similar legal separation appears across regional Philips Home pages.

This is a trademark-license structure:

1. Royal Philips owns the PHILIPS mark.
2. Versuni has the right to use that mark for the licensed domestic-appliances business.
3. Hillhouse owns the acquired operating business, according to the latest official public record reviewed.
4. Local Versuni companies or distributors may handle sales, service and warranties.

A 15-year license is not permanent trademark ownership. It is also not enough to calculate a precise public expiration date, because the complete agreement—including its commencement, renewal and other contractual provisions—is not publicly available in the sources reviewed.

This distinction is commercially important. A distributor should not describe Versuni or Hillhouse as the owner of the global PHILIPS trademark. It should also avoid naming Royal Philips as the direct supplier of a Versuni floor-care product unless the contract and invoice actually say so.

## Is Versuni Part of Philips?

No. Versuni is not part of the Royal Philips corporate group.

The company became independent after the September 2021 divestment. It retained access to the Philips brand through a license, which explains why a Philips vacuum cleaner can be a Versuni product even though the front of the machine carries the PHILIPS name.

This is similar to other appliance structures in which the familiar brand and the operating company are different. World Clean Biz has explained comparable—but legally distinct—arrangements in [Bosch appliances and BSH](https://worldcleanbiz.com/blog/who-owns-bosch-appliances-bsh-siemens-brands) and in the [AEG and Electrolux relationship](https://worldcleanbiz.com/blog/who-owns-aeg-electrolux-relationship).

The comparison should not be stretched too far. Each trademark license, acquisition and regional entity map has its own scope. The Philips relationship must be verified from its own transaction and legal documents.

## Which Philips Products Belong to Versuni?

Versuni is a domestic-appliances company. Its published portfolio spans kitchen appliances, coffee, climate care, garment care and floor care. Philips-branded vacuum cleaners, wet-and-dry floor cleaners and electric mops fall within that floor-care scope.

But the Philips name also appears on products that remain inside Royal Philips.

Royal Philips' [2025 Annual Report](https://www.results.philips.com/publications/ar25/downloads/files/en/PhilipsFullAnnualReport2025-English.pdf) describes its current Personal Health business as three units:

- Personal Care, including shavers, groomers, hair-care and hair-removal devices;
- Oral Healthcare, including Sonicare products;
- Mother and Child Care, including Avent products.

Royal Philips also continues to operate major health-technology businesses in diagnosis, treatment, connected care and monitoring.

The correct rule is therefore not “all Philips consumer products are Versuni.” The correct rule is **identify the category first**. A Philips vacuum cleaner may sit in the Versuni domestic-appliances system, while a Philips shaver or Sonicare toothbrush sits in Royal Philips' Personal Health system.

## Where Are Philips Vacuum Cleaners Made?

There is no reliable single-country answer for the entire brand.

Versuni's 2023 statement listed company manufacturing sites in five countries and described a global product and component supplier network. It did not allocate every category or model to a particular plant. A corporate manufacturing footprint cannot establish that a specific Philips vacuum was made in China, India, Romania, Italy or Brazil.

Country-of-origin claims also require more than a head-office address. Versuni is headquartered in Amsterdam, but a Dutch headquarters does not make every product Dutch-made. Hillhouse's investment origin likewise does not determine the customs origin of a product.

For each model, procurement teams should verify:

- the rating label and retail packaging;
- the manufacturer named in the manual or declaration of conformity;
- the importer or responsible person in the destination market;
- regulatory certificates and recall notices;
- customs and origin documentation;
- the factory audit, purchase order and batch traceability records.

This model-level approach also applies to other premium appliance groups. World Clean Biz's analyses of [Miele's ownership and manufacturing network](https://worldcleanbiz.com/blog/who-owns-miele-family-manufacturing-network) and [Dyson's ownership and manufacturing structure](https://worldcleanbiz.com/blog/who-owns-dyson-james-dyson-singapore-manufacturing) show why headquarters, ownership and factory location need separate evidence.

## Who Sells and Warrants Philips Floor-Care Products?

The responsible entity varies by market and channel.

| Market example | Entity named in an official document | What the document establishes |
|---|---|---|
| Netherlands | Versuni Netherlands B.V. | Seller for the local Philips Home online shop |
| Spain and Italy | Versuni Netherlands B.V. | Seller named in the national online-store terms |
| Germany/Austria site | Versuni Germany GmbH | Regional site publisher; official imprint identifies Versuni Holding B.V. as shareholder |
| Hungary | Versuni Hungary Kft. | Contracting party for Philips and Saeco domestic-appliance online purchases |
| United States | Versuni USA Corporation | Seller and warranty-provider example for Philips-branded home appliances |
| Chile | Versuni Chile S.A. | Local warranty entity for covered categories and SKUs |

These examples demonstrate variation; they are not a complete global entity list. A retailer may be the invoice seller, a separate Versuni or distributor entity may be the importer, and another company may provide the local warranty.

Warranty scope also matters for B2B buyers. The current [U.S. Versuni warranty](https://us.home-appliances.philips/pages/warranty-information), for example, excludes professional use beyond normal domestic use. A cleaning contractor, hotel or commercial facility should therefore confirm the permitted use case instead of assuming that a consumer floor-care warranty applies in a business environment.

## What Procurement and Compliance Teams Should Verify

Before approving a Philips floor-care supplier or making an ownership claim, verify:

1. **Product category:** Is the product domestic floor care, Royal Philips Personal Health equipment or another licensed Philips category?
2. **Target country:** Which legal and regulatory market applies?
3. **Trademark authorization:** Does the seller's authority cover the product class, country and channel?
4. **Contracting seller:** Which legal entity appears in the quotation, terms, invoice and payment record?
5. **Importer and responsible person:** Which company appears on the label and local compliance documents?
6. **Manufacturer of record:** Which entity is named in the manual, declaration, certificate or recall file?
7. **Factory:** Does the audit cover the exact plant, production line, model and batch—not simply the Versuni group?
8. **Country of origin:** Do customs and origin records support the marketing claim?
9. **Warranty and intended use:** Who pays for repair, replacement, recall and spare parts, and is commercial use covered?
10. **Data responsibility:** If the product connects to an app or account, which Versuni entity or affiliate controls customer and device data?

For a distributor, these checks determine more than wording. They affect authorization, channel conflict, import compliance, product liability, recall execution, spare-parts planning and warranty cost.
