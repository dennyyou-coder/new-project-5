Yes, you did not misread. Here I am talking about hosting an IFA exhibition, not participating in one.
![Article image 2](/images/insights/why-china-needs-a-cleaning-appliance-ifa-image-02.jpg)
With China's growing strength, it is only a matter of time before Chinese brands become world-class brands. When that happens, foreigners from all over the globe will gather in Suzhou and Shanghai to seek business opportunities. I want to host a Chinese version of an IFA exhibition so that Chinese brands can release their new products on their home turf.
Some people might say, "Denny, you must be out of your mind. Your little company can barely get a booth at an IFA exhibition; how do you think you can host one?" You have no idea how difficult it is.
Recently, at the IFA exhibition, various giants in cleaning appliances showcased their impressive black technologies, almost making the IFA exhibition a showcase for Chinese brands.
Midea boldly rented out an entire exhibition hall and treated the event as its private press conference.
Zhiyi took up nearly 1,000 square meters of space and launched products such as stair-climbing robots, lawn mowers, and full-scenario solutions.
MOVA also launched products like stair-climbing robots and lawn mowers, as well as various other cutting-edge technologies.

Roborock, a subsidiary ofRoborock, has placed large advertisements outside the exhibition hall. In addition to their cleaning appliances, they have also launched three lawn mowing robots at once.

Anker's Eufy has also released a stair-climbing robot, which is quite advanced. It is said that it will officially hit the market in 2026.

In contrast, other exhibitors from other countries have not shown much activity. The venues originally occupied by Samsung and LG are now taken over by Chinese giants. Without the efforts of Chinese brands, the IFA exhibition would be rather uninteresting.

I have also attended several IFA exhibitions in the past. What is different about us compared to these giants is that as an OEM manufacturer, we do not exhibit in brand halls. The organizers probably find our OEM manufacturers not impressive enough and want to make money from them by setting up a Chinese Pavilion in a remote location where all these Chinese manufacturers are grouped together. This Chinese Pavilion has very low attendance; over the three-day period, it is estimated that only 20-30 business cards will be exchanged.

The value of IFA lies in its being the largest appliance exhibition in Europe. Due to convenient transportation, many buyers from neighboring countries come here. For Chinese brands entering the European market, IFA is an excellent platform for promotion and direct communication with buyers, facilitating quick cooperation.

Apart from customers in the European market, many professionals from other countries also come here to seek opportunities. The same applies to the CES exhibition in the United States.

However, distance is not the only consideration; it is commercial opportunities that matter. Every year, foreigners from all over the world come to Guangzhou for the Canton Fair to find suitable business opportunities.

As I mentioned earlier, the actual effect of IFA was mediocre. Other international exhibitions had even worse results. In 2023, we participated in ten global exhibitions; one time, at an exhibition in Italy, the result was extremely poor. Over three days, only five to six business cards were exchanged, and there has been no response after follow-up contact.

With nearly twenty years of experience, I have attended over a hundred professional exhibitions worldwide. My feeling is that most organizers are not professional; they do not understand the industry and do not consider the needs of exhibitors. Their sole focus is on selling exhibition spaces. Once the spaces are sold, they no longer care about the exhibitors' well-being. Such exhibitions will see exhibitors come only once and never return. Without exhibitors, buyers are even less likely to attend. Therefore, some exhibitions that have been held for many years still suffer from very poor quality.

So,

What kind of exhibition will I create?
How can I solve problems?
What benefits can I bring to everyone?

I will organize a specialized exhibition for cleaning appliances, including household cleaning (vacuum cleaners, floor scrubbers, robotic vacuums), outdoor cleaning (pool cleaners, lawn mowers), commercial/medical cleaning, and automotive cleaning. The exhibitors will include brand owners, whole machine OEMs, component suppliers, and solution providers. The exact date has not been set yet; it is tentatively scheduled for March to May 2026, lasting three days.

For exhibitors, the primary goal is to seek commercial cooperation opportunities. Different exhibitors have distinct target audiences:
- For brand owner exhibitors, their main purpose in participating is to promote their products and attract more distributors and channel partners. I will connect them with various live streamers, procurement teams from major e-commerce retailers, and distributors.
- For OEM whole machine factories, they aim to engage with customers from both domestic and international brands. I will invite buyers and R&D personnel from major domestic brands, as well as some foreign clients who have purchasing offices in China. However, it may take time for overseas clients to adapt to Chinese exhibitions beyond the Canton Fair.
- For component suppliers, their clients are procurement and R&D teams of brand owners and whole machine factories. I will invite a large number of industry professionals to visit and organize specific procurement matching sessions.

For professional visitors attending the exhibition:
- Buyers and R&D personnel hope to see the latest products from industry giants, find new products and better suppliers, and gain access to new supply chain technologies that can be used in their own products.
If they could meet most of the suppliers in one day, all buyers and R&D personnel would be willing to attend such an exhibition.

In addition to regular exhibitions, I will also host industry forums. I will invite influential experts for presentations; our previous events in Suzhou and Shenzhen each had over 500 participants, indicating that our content is highly valuable. Simultaneously, we will hold targeted procurement sessions for brand owners to facilitate more efficient connections between supply chains and brands.

This is my initial plan. Of course, there are still many details to be finalized. Regardless, we will definitely be more professional and valuable than current exhibition organizers.

I have briefed this matter with a few close friends and industry leaders, and some of them have already confirmed their participation. The CEO of Zhiyun Technology, Mr. Yu, has also expressed strong support. Some industry leaders raised some questions that I need to address in person.

I am very grateful for the support of these friends and industry leaders. They supported me without any information, and I will not let them down. Once I decide to do something, it must be better than what others can offer.
My future transformation direction is clear: transitioning from product development to serving the entire industry. Besides this exhibition, I also run a product company that could generate several million in sales this year. The future direction of this company is to help factories in the cleaning appliance industry sell their products to global customers. Currently, product sales are no longer about making huge profits through information gaps but rather by leveraging technological and service differences.

Additionally, I may also handle sourcing for some domestic and international clients in the cleaning appliance sector, from product development, communication, sample making, inspection to delivery. Due to our relationship with the factories, we might be able to help clients secure better prices. In this complex environment of cleaning appliances, finding the most suitable products for clients and helping them make a profit is our goal.

No matter what, my future will have countless directions. If anyone has any ideas, feel free to communicate with me.
