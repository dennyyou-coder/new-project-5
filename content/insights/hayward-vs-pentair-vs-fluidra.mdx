**Hayward, Pentair and Fluidra are three independent publicly traded companies, not three brands owned by one pool-equipment group.** Hayward is a focused pool-equipment platform listed as Hayward Holdings, Inc. Pentair Pool is one operating segment within the broader water company Pentair plc. Fluidra, S.A. is a multi-brand pool and wellness group whose portfolio includes AstralPool, Zodiac, Jandy and Polaris.

The practical difference is not captured by a single “best brand” answer. A distributor, pool professional or commercial buyer needs to compare the company scope, brands available in the target market, route to market, installed-product support and the legal entity responsible for the exact product.

## Three Different Ownership Structures

### Hayward: an independent focused company

Hayward Holdings [trades on the New York Stock Exchange under HAYW](https://investor.hayward.com/resources/investor-faqs/default.aspx). Its public parent is not Pentair, Fluidra or Maytronics. The company designs and manufactures pool equipment across pumps, filters, heaters, sanitization, lighting, automation, water features and cleaners.

Hayward is the most concentrated of the three comparison targets at the corporate level. Its [2025 Form 10-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1834622/000183462226000008/hayw-20251231.htm) reported $1.1222 billion in company sales, with $959.2 million from North America and $163.0 million from Europe and the rest of the world.

For the ownership chain, see [Who Owns Hayward Pool Products?](/blog/who-owns-hayward-pool-products). That page explains the public parent, operating companies and product-level responsibility. This comparison page focuses on how the Hayward platform differs from Pentair and Fluidra.

### Pentair: Pool inside a diversified water company

Pentair plc [is incorporated in Ireland and trades on the New York Stock Exchange under PNR](https://investors.pentair.com/ir-resources/investor-faqs). Pentair reported $4.1760 billion in consolidated 2025 sales, but that number covers more than swimming pools.

The comparable operating unit is Pentair’s Pool segment. It designs and sells residential and commercial pool equipment, including pumps, filters, heaters, lights, controls, sanitizers, automatic cleaners and maintenance products. The [2025 annual report](https://investors.pentair.com/static-files/47275fb3-6d2b-41e3-a33a-545c38f36e64) reported $1.5588 billion in segment sales.

Pentair therefore combines a recognizable whole-pool platform with the resources and reporting structure of a broader water company. A buyer should not use total Pentair group revenue as if it were pool-equipment revenue.

### Fluidra: a listed multi-brand pool group

Fluidra, S.A. [trades under FDR on the Madrid and Barcelona stock exchanges](https://www.fluidra.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/3.-English_2025-Integrated-Anual-Report-ENG.pdf). It reported €2.184 billion in 2025 group sales and [describes a footprint](https://www.fluidra.com/press-room/company-statements/latest-communications/) spanning more than 47 countries, more than 36 factories and more than 128 sales branches.

Fluidra’s structure is brand-led and regional. AstralPool, Zodiac, Jandy and Polaris are prominent anchors, while other brands cover construction systems, covers, components, water treatment, testing and adjacent pool categories.

The [Fluidra brand guide](/blog/what-brands-does-fluidra-own) owns the detailed portfolio map. The key comparison here is structural: Fluidra can approach a market through different brands, subsidiaries and channel models rather than one universal global catalog.

## Brand and Product Architecture

Corporate ownership and customer-facing brand architecture are not the same thing.

Hayward generally presents the Hayward name as the master platform, supported by product-family marks such as OmniLogic, AquaRite, ColorLogic and AquaVac. The structure makes the public company and the primary equipment brand relatively easy to connect, although regional entities and product documents still matter.

Pentair presents a broad corporate water identity and a defined Pool segment. Within that segment, Pentair Pool is the main equipment identity, with Kreepy Krauly, Pleatco and Sta-Rite named as primary brands in the 2025 annual report. The customer sees a pool platform, while investors see one segment inside a larger company.

Fluidra uses the broadest portfolio architecture of the three. Jandy can serve as a North American whole-pool equipment platform. AstralPool has a wide international professional range. Polaris is strongly associated with automatic pool cleaning, while Zodiac spans equipment and cleaning in several regions. Brand availability and responsibility therefore need country-level checks.

The practical question is not which company owns more names. It is whether the target brand gives the distributor or buyer a coherent range, an authorized commercial route and a support system for the products actually being purchased.

## Distribution Is Not the Same at All Three

Hayward’s [2025 filing](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1834622/000183462226000008/hayw-20251231.htm) describes a channel shaped by distributors, retailers, builders and service professionals. Management estimated that about 80% of US residential pool equipment moved through distributors in 2025, with about 15% sold directly to retailers and 5% to builders. In Europe, it estimated about 77% through distributors and 23% through direct sales.

Those figures reveal the importance of trade relationships, but they are regional industry estimates. They do not mean that every Hayward product follows the same route.

Pentair’s Pool customers include wholesale and retail distributors in residential and commercial markets, as well as end users. Its annual report also states that one Pool customer represented approximately 18% of Pentair’s consolidated 2025 sales. The filing does not name that customer in the cited section, so a buyer should not guess the identity.

Fluidra combines broad distribution with regional subsidiaries and sales branches. Its brand architecture lets the group serve professional, project, retail and consumer routes under different names. The same breadth increases the need to verify which entity controls the price list, inventory, dealer appointment, technical support and warranty in the target territory.

| Channel question | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Which regional company signs the agreement? | The listed parent rarely signs every local sale or distributor contract |
| Which brands and categories are included? | A dealer appointment for one brand may not cover sister brands |
| Is the route wholesale, professional, retail or direct? | Pricing, product access and service obligations can differ |
| Who stocks parts and performs diagnosis? | Sales reach does not prove repair capability |
| Who approves warranty claims? | The seller, distributor and warrantor may be different entities |

## Where Pool Robotics Fits

All three platforms participate in automatic or robotic pool cleaning, but the strategic position is different.

### Hayward: robotics within the main equipment brand

Hayward identifies AquaVac among its brands and includes cleaners in its equipment portfolio. Robotics sits inside the broader Hayward system rather than under a separate listed parent or a large external brand portfolio.

The commercial advantage is potential alignment with the rest of a Hayward equipment relationship. The due-diligence question is whether the local dealer supports the exact cleaner, parts and warranty process—not whether the dealer sells Hayward pumps or automation.

### Pentair: Prowler within the Pool segment

Pentair’s [official US robotic-cleaner page](https://www.pentair.com/en-us/pool-spa/products/cleaners/robotic-pool-cleaners.html) lists the Prowler family. The line sits beside the Pool segment’s larger equipment and connected-product offering.

Product status still needs SKU-level verification. One Pentair page, for example, marks the Prowler 930W as discontinued even while the category page remains useful for identifying the family. A corporate comparison cannot substitute for a current catalog, dealer quotation and service confirmation.

### Fluidra: owned cleaner brands plus an Aiper alliance

Fluidra owns Polaris and Zodiac within its group portfolio. Polaris has a strong automatic-cleaner identity, while regional Zodiac ranges also include pool-cleaning products. The ownership and operating chain for one major brand is covered in [Who Owns Polaris Pool Cleaners?](/blog/who-owns-polaris-pool-cleaners-fluidra-zodiac).

Fluidra also [completed an initial 27% investment in Aiper](https://www.fluidra.com/press-room/press-release/fluidra-delivers-strong-2025-results-advancing-its-strategic-priorities/) in 2025. That gives Fluidra a significant minority position and a strategic alliance in cordless robotics; it does not make Aiper a wholly owned Fluidra brand. The current distinction is explained in [Who Owns Aiper?](/blog/who-owns-aiper-fluidra-stake).

This makes Fluidra’s robotics position broader but more relationship-dependent. A buyer must distinguish wholly owned brands, regional product companies and a minority-owned strategic partner.

## Scale Without a Misleading Ranking

The latest full-year figures are useful only when their scopes remain visible.

| Disclosure | 2025 sales | Scope |
|---|---:|---|
| Hayward | US$1.1222bn | Consolidated Hayward group |
| Pentair Pool | US$1.5588bn | Pentair’s Pool reportable segment |
| Pentair total | US$4.1760bn | Diversified Pentair group; not a pool-only comparison |
| Fluidra | €2.184bn | Consolidated Fluidra group |

This is not a ranking table. Fluidra reports in euros, Pentair’s most relevant number is a segment, and Hayward’s group includes a smaller non-pool activity. Revenue also does not measure dealer quality, installed base, robot share, product reliability or local service.

For a distributor, a smaller relevant network can be more valuable than a larger group with the wrong territory or channel. For a buyer, the decisive facts are product fit, current availability, support and total installed responsibility.

## Responsibility Map for One Purchase

A public parent proves group control. It does not complete the transaction chain.

For any Hayward, Pentair or Fluidra-group purchase, record:

1. the public parent and operating group;
2. the customer-facing brand;
3. the exact model and regional suffix;
4. the legal manufacturer on the product documents;
5. the importer or responsible economic operator;
6. the contract seller and invoice issuer;
7. the installer or commissioning provider, if required;
8. the parts and repair route;
9. the named warrantor and claim authority;
10. the software, account and connected-service operator.

Several roles can belong to one company. They should still be written down separately because a channel change, cross-border sale or discontinued SKU can split the chain.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are Hayward, Pentair and Fluidra owned by the same company?

No. Hayward Holdings, Pentair plc and Fluidra, S.A. are separate publicly traded companies.

### Is Pentair only a pool-equipment company?

No. Pool is one reportable segment within Pentair. The group also reports Flow and Water Solutions segments.

### Does Fluidra own Hayward or Pentair?

No. Fluidra owns a portfolio that includes AstralPool, Zodiac, Jandy and Polaris, but Hayward and Pentair are independent public companies.

### Which company has the largest pool business?

The available 2025 figures do not support a simple ranking without qualifications. Hayward and Fluidra report group figures in different currencies, while Pentair reports a distinct Pool segment. The business boundaries are not identical.

### Which company is strongest in robotic pool cleaners?

That depends on market, product type and channel. Hayward has AquaVac, Pentair has Prowler, and Fluidra owns Polaris and Zodiac while holding 27% of Aiper. Portfolio position does not prove current SKU availability, performance or local support.

### Can one distributor sell every brand within a group?

Do not assume so. Distributor rights can be limited by brand, category, territory and channel. Verify the written appointment and current price list.

### Does buying from an authorized seller guarantee local repair?

Not automatically. Confirm the workshop, parts source, turnaround, freight responsibility and warranty authority for the exact product.

## Final Decision

Hayward, Pentair and Fluidra represent three different ways to organize a pool-equipment platform: a focused public company, a pool segment inside a diversified water group, and a global multi-brand pool company.

The right comparison moves from the public parent down to the transaction. Identify the brand, regional entity, SKU, commercial channel, service route and warrantor. That chain determines whether the platform can deliver in one market; the company name alone does not.
