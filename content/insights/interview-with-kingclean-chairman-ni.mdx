## Q&A on the Journey of Innovation and Growth

### Q: In 10 years, it seems that Kingclean faced no significant obstacles and experienced very smooth growth. What do you attribute this to?

**A:** Leading innovation.

### Q: Which decisions did you make particularly well during your journey? Why didn't some other factories like Ningbo Fuda or Shanghai Lele succeed?

**A:** I believe most companies today are following others rather than considering what users and customers need, then innovating. They simply follow what others are doing, which means they will always be a step behind. In China, I was the first to reduce motor costs from 125 yuan to 80 yuan, increasing performance and lifespan by 50%. I also introduced the first cyclonic high-volume vacuum cleaner, the first handheld high-power vacuum cleaner, and the first upright pole cyclonic vacuum cleaner. By creating many industry-first products, we achieved high sales volumes, which led others to follow us, thereby continuously reducing profits until they could no longer make a profit. My approach was through innovation and research development, focusing on understanding what users and buyers need.

### Q: What are the key factors that have contributed to Kingclean's success?

**A:** The first is innovation. I focused on improving performance and solving user pain points. The second is quality. Products with low repair rates sell better, leading customers to rely on us because our products generate profits. At the time, we proposed a concept: producing European-quality products at Chinese costs. Additionally, we emphasized industrial design as crucial, ensuring that designs are modern and future-oriented.

### Q: What challenges did Kingclean face over the years?

**A:** I don't think we faced any particularly difficult times. The development of Kingclean, including my investments in other areas, has not been so challenging that it felt like the sky was falling. While the broader economic environment is tough, everyone faces similar challenges. For instance, during the 2008 financial crisis and the economic downturn from 2011 to 2012, as well as recent fluctuations in real estate and investment industries, we managed to navigate these turbulent times without significant difficulties. Even when the pandemic hit, it initially affected our sales but did not have a substantial impact overall. Our profits remained stable, and we are now seeing growth again.

### Q: How do you maintain a balance between meeting user needs and ensuring customer profitability?

**A:** We aim to create products that users want and that customers can profit from. For example, by increasing the retail price by $10 while only adding $1 in cost, both we and our customers benefit. This approach ensures sustainable growth through continuous innovation and quality improvements. If we were focused solely on costs, lower prices would lead to lower margins, making it unsustainable for long-term development.

### Q: How do you see the future of your company?

**A:** The challenges are real, but they affect everyone equally. We must continue to innovate, focus on quality, and ensure that our products meet user needs while providing a profit margin for customers. By doing so, we can maintain sustainable growth in an increasingly competitive market.

**A:** Live streaming is essentially a form of traffic marketing rather than value marketing. Chinese consumers tend to follow trends, buying products not just for their low prices but also because they see others purchasing them on social media or through influencers.
