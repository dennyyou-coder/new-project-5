STIHL's Global R&D Center Lands in Suzhou, Accelerating Its Push into Smart Garden Equipment.

On December 31, 2025, STIHL Group, the German giant in global garden machinery, officially announced that its global electric machinery R&D center had signed to locate in Suzhou Industrial Park. The center is planned to officially begin operations in the second quarter of 2026 and will focus on developing next-generation smart robotic mowers and high-end lithium-battery garden tools.

This move marks a major upgrade in STIHL's China strategy: from heavy manufacturing in Qingdao and sales logistics in Taicang to core technology R&D in Suzhou. Choosing Suzhou, known as the “capital of robot vacuums,” is intended to use the local mature talent reserves in AI algorithms, visual navigation and RTK positioning to make up for its shortcomings in intelligent software.

STIHL's layout is seen as a direct counterattack against Husqvarna and Segway Navimow. It means this century-old company that started with gasoline engines has finally decided to make boundary-wire-free robotic mowers a core battlefield for the future, trying to move its R&D brain forward to the Yangtze River Delta in China, the region with the fastest global supply-chain response, and reshape its leadership in the electric era.

Ryobi Previews Five Key 2026 New Products, Deepening Multi-Voltage Platform Layout.

Ryobi recently disclosed several new products to be launched in 2026. Although the brand released more than 75 new products in the past year, these five tools are considered annual highlights because of their unique designs and performance upgrades, covering the 18V ONE+, 40V and USB Lithium battery platforms.

The 18V one-handed reciprocating saw is lightweight for one-handed operation and narrow spaces, with 3,000 strokes per minute, a 5/8-inch stroke length and quick blade change. Some retailers have launched kits with batteries for around USD 80, while bare-tool versions are expected in late January or February 2026.

The 40V AWD self-propelled lawn mower is the industry's first battery-powered all-wheel-drive mower, suitable for complex terrain or slopes, with a 21-inch multi-blade system, a mowing command-center display and Turbo mode for tall grass.

The 18V 4-1/2-inch circular cutoff saw addresses the weak power of current 3-inch cutoff saws with a larger, more powerful one-handed tool. It has a maximum cutting depth of 1-9/16 inches, can complete around 300 cuts per charge, and suits wood, drywall and flooring materials.

The USB Lithium pruning shears continue expanding Ryobi's home-tool series, using a 2 Ah USB-C rechargeable battery and completing more than 450 cuts per charge to replace manual pruning shears and reduce fatigue.

The 18V attachment-capable string trimmer is expected in the first quarter of 2026 at USD 159. It is 89% quieter than comparable gas products and supports multiple attachment heads.

These launches show Ryobi's deep focus on segmented scenarios, from one-handed lightweight tools for DIY users to high-end AWD mowers for large yards, while using unified battery interfaces to increase user stickiness.

Lowe's Removes Flex Tools at Scale, Potentially Ending Cooperation.

According to industry media ToolGuyd on December 19, 2025, U.S. home-improvement retail giant Lowe's removed most Flex 24V cordless power tools and accessories from its online store. Only a few bundles remain marked as in-store only, widely seen as a clear signal that Flex may be exiting Lowe's U.S. channel.

Online clearing: Lowe's website has almost cleared Flex's main power-tool listings, though some Flex Stack Pack toolbox pages remain. Channel change: Flex originally entered the U.S. market as a Lowe's exclusive brand. Although it has expanded to Amazon and other channels, losing support from its main physical retailer raises concerns about future growth and innovation. Potential replacement: the market speculates Bosch's 18V cordless power-tool series may fill the shelf space left by Flex at Lowe's.

This move sparked industry discussion about the U.S. development momentum of brands under parent company Chervon, including Skil and Ego. Some industry comments believe Lowe's strategy adjustment reflects further consolidation of the power-tool market toward a few dominant brands such as Milwaukee, DeWalt and Ryobi, while also deepening the industry stereotype that Lowe's is a graveyard for tool brands.

DeWalt Launches New Five-Flute Countersink Drill Bit Series.

On January 2, 2026, DeWalt released a new drilling accessory series: five-flute 82-degree countersink drill bits. The series is designed for metal, wood and plastic, aiming to reduce vibration and provide smoother drilling through a multi-flute design.

Design features include five-flute geometry to avoid excessive vibration and round shanks compatible with handheld drills and drill presses. Sizes include 1/4 inch, 3/8 inch, 1/2 inch, 5/8 inch and 3/4 inch. Consumers can buy single bits or a five-piece set, model DWAMDB5SETCS. At the time of writing, the five-piece set was about USD 25 on Amazon and USD 50 at Acme Tools. Single bits, using 1/2 inch as an example, were priced between USD 13 and USD 17.

Some observers noted that such multi-flute countersink bits are common among construction-tool brands, and some users guessed the product may be a rebranded Irwin product. Although the official claim emphasizes vibration reduction, some professional users commented that compared with multi-flute designs, single-flute or zero-flute designs usually perform better in preventing chatter, especially in handheld operation.
