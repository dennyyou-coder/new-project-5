Germany's STIHL global R&D center settles in Suzhou, accelerating its entry into the smart garden track
On December 31, 2025, STIHL, the German giant in the global garden machinery industry, officially announced that its global electric machinery R&D center has signed a contract to settle in Suzhou Industrial Park (SIP). The center is scheduled to be officially put into operation in the second quarter of 2026 and is committed to developing the next generation of intelligent lawn mowing robots and high-end lithium battery garden tools.
[Picture https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/sz_mmbiz_jpg/8yYcykH76A54XRI8S34kee6SKHYUOIice3KJaOtGblzZd]
This move marks a major upgrade of Steele's strategy in China: from "heavy manufacturing" in Qingdao and "sales logistics" in Taicang to "core technology research and development" in Suzhou. Choosing Suzhou, which is known as the "robot vacuum capital", aims to make use of the local mature AI algorithm, visual navigation (VSLAM)** and RTK positioning talent pool to make up for its shortcomings in intelligent software.
Steele's layout is seen as a direct counterattack to Husqvarna (Husqvarna) and Nine (Segway). This means that this century-old store, which started with a fuel engine, has finally made up its mind to regard "borderless lawn mowing robots" as its core battlefield in the future, trying to reshape its leadership in the electric era by moving its research and development brains forward to China's Yangtze River Delta region, where the global supply chain is the fastest responsive.

Lowe's removes Flex tools on a large scale, and cooperation between the two parties may end
