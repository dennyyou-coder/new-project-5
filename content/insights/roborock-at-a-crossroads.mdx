In May 2021, investors suddenly noticed that the stock of a company listed on the Growth Enterprise Market exceeded 1000 yuan, reaching its peak close to 1500 yuan, nearly matching the share price of Moutai Group, the domestic leader in liquor. For a time, Roborock became an object of curiosity for many. As people's understanding of Roborock grew, it began to be referred to with titles such as "Mortar of Cleaning" and "Machine Mortar."

![Article image 1](/images/insights/roborock-at-a-crossroads-image-001.jpg)

By 2022, Roborock's stock price had dropped to over 80 yuan, with a market capitalization of over 5 billion yuan, lagging behind its old rival Ecovacs' valuation of over 7 billion yuan. If we only look at the market capitalization, it seems that both companies are not far apart. However, if we conduct a deeper analysis, we may find that the gap between them has become significantly larger, and from the perspective of future development, this gap is likely to widen further.

**Let's take a look at the changes in sales for both companies over the past few years:**

In 2017, Roborock's revenue was 1.119 billion yuan, while Ecovacs' was 4.55 billion yuan.

In 2018, Roborock's revenue increased to 3.051 billion yuan, and Ecovacs' reached 5.694 billion yuan.

In 2019, Roborock's revenue grew to 4.205 billion yuan, while Ecovacs' was 5.312 billion yuan.

In 2020, Roborock's revenue was 4.53 billion yuan, and Ecovacs' reached 7.234 billion yuan.

In the third quarter of 2021, Roborock's revenue was 3.827 billion yuan, while Ecovacs' was 8.244 billion yuan.

One can see that Roborock experienced very rapid growth in recent years. However, in 2020, Ecovacs' sales suddenly surged, significantly widening the gap between the two companies. What happened then?

Roborock was founded by Chang Jing, a former Baidu product manager, and received investment from Xiaomi. From its inception, Roborock quickly became an industry leader due to its superior technical capabilities and high-quality products. The early development of Roborock can be attributed to several reasons:

1. **External Environment:** At the time, iRobot's prices were extremely expensive, with even basic random navigation machines costing 2-3000 yuan. Meanwhile, domestic companies were struggling, mainly producing non-intelligent random navigation and gyroscope navigation products. The laser modules at that time were also very expensive, with LiDAR navigation products priced around 4000 yuan. Roborock leveraged Xiaomi's strong supply chain to obtain high-quality laser modules at a good price, launching its first LiDAR navigation product for just 1500 yuan. With Xiaomi's powerful sales channels, Roborock achieved significant success in its first year.

2. **Technical Strength:** In the minds of many, Roborock is known as a company that focuses on technology and has relatively weak marketing capabilities. During the early stages of the company, Roborock had few market or sales personnel; instead, most team members were researchers and engineers. Early in the company's development, Roborock dominated the navigation algorithms across the industry.

3. **Xiaomi Support:** Without Xiaomi's support, it would have been impossible for Roborock to obtain such inexpensive LiDAR navigation modules. Additionally, without Xiaomi's early impressive sales capabilities, a marketing-focused company like Roborock likely wouldn't have sold as many units in its early stages.

4. **Craftsmanship Spirit:** During the initial release of Roborock and Xiaomi's robot vacuums, the public perception of robotic vacuums was that they were toys with poor quality. Roborock and Xiaomi jointly raised the industry standard to a higher level.

**These are the reasons for Roborock's success, but as the saying goes, 'where you succeed is where you may also fail.'**

As Roborock became one of the leaders in the industry, external environments, competitors, and market landscapes underwent significant changes. The turning point occurred in 2020.

**Now, let us revisit the advantages that made Roborock successful early on, and we find that many of these advantages no longer exist.**

1. External Environment. The market leaders are no longer iRobot but Ecovacs, Xiaomi, Roborock, and Narwal. These companies' research and development capabilities and product iteration execution power are not as strong as those of the early iRobot. The prices of products in the market have long entered a red ocean phase.

2. Technological Strength. By 2020 and even earlier, the industry had already completed the use of LiDAR navigation technology, with significant decreases in the cost of LiDAR navigation modules. Third-party manufacturers such as 1M-Robot and LD Robot already possessed mature LiDAR navigation technologies comparable to those of Roborock. Ecovacs also early on laid out its self-researched LiDAR navigation algorithms. Now, Roborock does not have many leading advantages over other competitors.

3. Xiaomi's Support. Considering their own development, Roborock no longer manufactures the Mi-branded vacuum cleaners for Xiaomi but focuses mainly on product development under the Roborock brand. This has both benefits and drawbacks.

4. Craftsmanship Spirit. Being meticulous about details is beneficial for products. However, sometimes being overly focused on unnecessary details can miss out on major industry trends. Products like Narwal's self-cleaning robot vacuum and Anker's hard floor washer have significant quality issues from a product perspective but are still accepted by the market; what matters more is making the product first.

The reason why Ecovacs significantly outperformed Roborock starting in 2020 was primarily due to the explosive growth of its sub-brand, Tineco's hard floor washers. Leveraging the pandemic, live streaming, and other short video trends, Tineco's hard floor washers dominated all channels and became synonymous with the floor cleaning industry.

We have conducted an in-depth analysis of ST Robotics' product line, which shows that the competitive pressure it faces is significant:

**Robot Vacuums:**

1. Home robot vacuums: Roborock's robot vacuums are considered high-end products in this category. However, being at the premium end does not solely depend on price; innovation and functionality of the product itself are also crucial. In 2020, Narwal emerged as a formidable competitor, surpassing Roborock in the high-end segment with single-product sales nearing RMB 2 billion, leading the industry towards self-cleaning capabilities.

In September 2021, Ecovacs released the full-featured robot vacuum X1, reigniting an arms race within the industry. Although Roborock did release similar products after Narwal and Ecovacs' launches, its role has shifted from being a market leader to one of the followers.

While Roborock still leads in the robot vacuum segment, currently, no significant innovations are coming from Roborock itself.

At the same time, the number of competitors in this industry is increasing. Narwal, valued at RMB 30 billion, Dreame, valued at RMB 17 billion, and new players such as Beetle, along with increasingly competitive players in the robot vacuum market, are all challenging Roborock's leadership position.

2. Commercial robot vacuums: The commercial segment is too small for both Ecovacs and Roborock to be considered significant at this time.

3. Wet Mop Machines: Tineco currently dominates the wet mop machine industry with a 60% market share in China. Although Roborock does have a product called U10, its sales volume is significantly lower compared to Tineco's offerings. While U10 has some innovations, the industry is constantly evolving, and Tineco's iteration speed far exceeds that of other players. It can be anticipated that for now, Roborock will struggle to make it into the top few in the wet mop machine market.

![Article image 3](/images/insights/roborock-at-a-crossroads-image-003.jpg)

4. Handheld Vacuum Cleaners. They can basically be ignored.

**One could say that from a product perspective, Roborock's innovative capabilities have significantly declined.**

Additionally, in terms of marketing and channel layout, Roborock remains underperforming.

**One could say that no matter which aspect is considered, the situation Roborock Technology now faces has become more challenging than it was in previous years. If previously Roborock relied on product quality and technology to offset its marketing disadvantages, it no longer possesses this advantage at the product level.**

In 2021, Loke Intelligent Technology Co., Ltd. was established in Shanghai, with Tencent and Sequoia Capital leading a one billion dollar investment by year-end. The rumors that Roborock Technology would be involved in automobile manufacturing finally came to an end. However, instead of the company itself, Chang Jing, the individual founder, became the main actor. It is said that Loke Intelligent's approach to car manufacturing will focus on off-road vehicles similar to Mercedes-Benz G-Class and follow a range-extended route akin to Li Auto. A significant portion of Chang Jing's energy would be redirected from Roborock Technology to this new venture.

![Article image 4](/images/insights/roborock-at-a-crossroads-image-004.jpg)

Just for Roborock, when facing intense competition from numerous strong rivals in the robot vacuum industry, would it be a good thing if the founder were to leave the company and shift to another sector? Many years later, as the competitive landscape stabilizes, future observers might look back and realize that 2022 was a crossroads for the robot vacuum industry, with only one victor left to reflect on the events of the year.

Roborock stood at the historical crossroads in 2022.
