A Chinese swimming pool robot company, how it leverages its alliance with Fluidra to challenge the established order of Maytronics.

![Article image 1](/images/insights/aiper-fluidra-pool-robotics-alliance-image-001.jpg)

*The alliance between Yuanding and Fluidra is not just an equity investment but also a capability complementation between wireless pool robots and professional pool channels.*

In 2019, when Yang Wang pressed Yuanding Intelligent's resources into the pool robot market, pool cleaning in Europe and America was still a slow business. Cables dragged through the water, machines moved along random paths, and users relied on professional stores and manual maintenance. The market was large but unlike consumer electronics, which rapidly changed with each generation.

Aiper’s choice was to make this professional equipment more akin to consumer electronics: wireless, one-button start-up, online sales, retail channels, and rapid iteration.

This was not a niche opportunity for a substitute product. The original pool robot leader was Maytronics. In 2025, Maytronics' revenue was 140.6 million Israeli new shekels, estimated at approximately RMB 3.4 billion based on the exchange rate as of May 29, 2026; in 2024, it was approximately RMB 3.93 billion. For Aiper, the real target was not to become a wireless pool robot brand but to compete for the next generation's industry leadership by wireless, online, and redefining the rules with China’s supply chain.

Six years later, it allied with the global pool equipment group Fluidra. In December 2025, Fluidra completed its first phase transaction, investing approximately $100 million to acquire a 27% stake in Aiper, estimated at RMB 680 million based on the exchange rate as of May 30, 2026. The deal also included a second phase: if Aiper's future revenue exceeds $370 million and it achieves an EBITDA margin of about 15%, Fluidra plans to further increase its stake to a majority.

This was more of an industrial alliance than just a minority equity investment. Aiper gained funding, professional channels, and pool service systems; Fluidra obtained wireless pool robots, consumer brands, and rapid iteration capabilities.

Aiper needed this ally. In 2024, as the market for pool robots began to scale, Aiper's sales grew by 75%. But with scaling came pressure: more brands were selling on Amazon, intensifying online price competition; entry into professional channels was not easy; competitors like Xingmian Innovation started raising funds; and cleaning robot giants such as Zhiwei, Ecovacs, and STONE were observing or entering this category.

For Yuanding to become the world leader, it needed more than just sales. It required additional funding to support R&D, inventory, channel fees, and global after-sales services; it also needed a stronger offline partner to enter professional pool channels rather than competing only on Amazon and large retail channels.

Fluidra’s foundation was precisely what Aiper struggled to establish alone. In 2025, Fluidra's revenue was 2.184 billion euros, estimated at approximately RMB 17.21 billion; Adjusted EBITDA was 501 million euros, estimated at approximately RMB 3.95 billion. Its product range included pumps, filters, heaters, disinfection equipment, pool covers, IoT, cleaning accessories, robots, and commercial pool solutions. The Pool & Wellness business accounted for 98% of total revenue.

More importantly, it is about the channels. According to Fluidra's annual report, pool demand is divided into three parts: new residential pools account for approximately 30%, maintenance, repair, and renovation of residential pools account for around 60%, and commercial pools account for about 10%. This means that its core business is not a one-time sale of equipment but long-term embedding in the pool maintenance network.

Fluidra also needs Aiper. Wireless pool robots are more akin to consumer electronics, where users compare prices online, watch videos, read reviews, and purchase directly from large retail channels. This market demands lighter products, faster iterations, and stronger online presence.

From the interview transcript, Aiper is valued for a set of capabilities that have already been proven: product and technology, omnichannel sales, brand localization, overseas supply chain, global service, and international organizational structure. TechNode's interview provides a more direct explanation: after evaluating Chinese manufacturers, Fluidra found two key points in favor of Aiper—willingness to share technical details and the ability to customize its supply chain for different channels.

These points are crucial. The wireless pool robot needs to enter multiple channels such as Amazon, Walmart, Home Depot, Costco, as well as specialized pool stores. Different channels have varying price ranges, after-sales requirements, and product configurations. Aiper can supply a $500-$800 product for Walmart, approximately RMB 3,400-5,400, separately from the higher-end models priced over $1,000 for pool specialty stores, which are around RMB 6,800 and above. For a channel-focused company like Fluidra, this is more valuable than relying on a single blockbuster product.

![Article image 2](/images/insights/aiper-fluidra-pool-robotics-alliance-image-002.jpg)

*Aiper's product line extends from pool bottom cleaning to wall, waterline, and surface cleaning.*

Competition is no longer coming from just one direction. Xingmiao Innovation is pushing Beatbot with financing and high-end products, completing a new round of financing worth approximately 1 billion yuan in 2025; Wanyuan Technology has shifted from manufacturing to its own brand, WYBOT; Zhiyue, STONES, and Ecovacs are standing on the sidelines with their funding, algorithms, and overseas channels. For Yuanding, the window period is getting shorter.

In this competitive environment, Fluidra needs Aiper's speed, while Aiper needs Fluidra's foundation.

The transaction structure reflects this cautious approach. Fluidra did not directly hold a controlling stake but initially acquired 27%. According to the annual report financial notes, Aiper's initial equity investment cost was €85.396 million, approximately RMB 670 million, with goodwill of €74.42 million, or about RMB 590 million. This means that the value of this alliance lies not in the book net assets but in Aiper's growth, brand, channels, and technological capabilities.

The basis for this arrangement is Yuanding's growth over the past few years. Fluidra disclosed that Aiper's sales in 2024 were approximately $195 million, or RMB 1.33 billion, a year-on-year increase of 75%. Backed by this growth rate, Aiper's sales in 2023 are estimated to have been around $111 million, or RMB 760 million. In a Forbes interview republished by Sohu, it is mentioned that Aiper aims for over one million units sold and entry into more than 7,000 stores in Europe and America in 2024.

In 2025, according to Aiper's official website, the company covered over 57 countries and regions, entered more than 10,000 retail stores, had over 3 million global users, and became the global leader by manufacturer sales volume. Fluidra did not separately disclose Aiper's 2025 sales but expects a compound annual growth rate of nearly 40% for Aiper from 2023 to 2027, with sales exceeding $400 million, or RMB 2.72 billion, in 2027.

![Article image 3](/images/insights/aiper-fluidra-pool-robotics-alliance-image-003.jpg)

*Aiper's Growth Path: By 2024, Aiper aims for approximately RMB 1.33 billion in sales, with a target of surpassing RMB 2.72 billion by 2027.*

These figures explain why both parties are willing to form an alliance: Yuanding needs Fluidra to continue amplifying its growth, while Fluidra seeks to incorporate Aiper's growth into its network of pool equipment.

However, Aiper still needs to prove itself. The category of wireless pool robots is characterized by high unit prices, strong seasonality, and significant post-sale pressure. In 2023, the U.S. CPSC recalled approximately 22,000 units of Aiper Elite Pro GS100 due to battery overheating and short-circuit risks leading to burn and fire hazards. In 2025, the CPSC issued another recall for the Aiper Seagull Pro, citing risks of melting, smoking, or catching fire during charging.

In an interview with KrASIA, Yang Wang responded to the 2023 recall by stating that the batch had a defect rate of 0.049%, but the company chose to recall all units and claimed that the third-generation product addressed these issues through potting technology. This response highlights that pool robots are continuously exposed to water, electricity, sunlight, high temperatures, and humidity, making safety redundancies and quality control critical for brand credibility.

Yuanding's alliance with Fluidra does not signify a stable competitive landscape in the pool robot industry; it merely enters a higher intensity level of competition.

![Article image 4](/images/insights/aiper-fluidra-pool-robotics-alliance-image-004.jpg)

*Fluidra gave Aiper a larger entry ticket, but it faces a reshuffled competitive landscape.*

On one side is Maytronics. Even under pressure in 2025, Dolphin remains one of the most important names in global pool robot order. Aiper can complement its offline channels and professional services through Fluidra; similarly, Maytronics might seek partners to address wireless technology, consumer channels, and product iteration speed.

On the other side are Chinese competitors. Xingmai Innovation entered from the high-end market; if it continues to penetrate more volume-driven price segments, it will directly collide with Aiper's core business. Wang Yuan Technology has moved from the depths of the supply chain to the doorstep of capital markets. As a former supplier and established manufacturer, its manufacturing experience, cost control, and product engineering capabilities represent another form of pressure.

Further out, Zhiyue, Ecovacs, STONE Robotics, Ninebot Continental, and Kuma Power are also looking at outdoor family robots. They may not yet have made pool robots their primary battleground, but they all possess the necessary funds, algorithms, supply chains, overseas channels, and brand organization capabilities.

Like Wang Yang, they are targeting not just a small business in a niche category, but the top spot in the world of outdoor family robots.

Fluidra gave Aiper a larger entry ticket, but it did not secure its victory. The competition in the pool robot industry may not only involve product substitution; it could also be reshaped through equity stakes, channel partnerships, supply chain collaborations, and capital alliances.
