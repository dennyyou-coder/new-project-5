## Early Battery Technologies in Cleaning Appliances

The earliest vacuum cleaners used nickel-cadmium (NiCd) batteries. NiCd batteries are characterized by their small capacity, long charging times, and short usage duration.

Early NiCd battery-powered vacuums were known for their "charge 10 hours, use 5 minutes" reputation. These batteries exhibited a memory effect; if not fully discharged, they would not release all of their stored energy during subsequent uses. Additionally, due to environmental concerns, the European Union banned the use of NiCd batteries in power tools and small household appliances starting January 1, 2016.

## The Rise of Lithium-Ion Batteries in the Market

After the advent of lithium-ion batteries (Lithium-ion battery), they gradually replaced nickel-metal hydride (NiMH) batteries as the market's dominant choice. Compared to NiMH batteries, lithium-ion batteries offer higher energy density—1.5 to 2 times that of NiMH batteries—and lack the memory effect and have a lower self-discharge rate. These batteries function by relying on the movement of lithium ions between the positive and negative electrodes. Lithium-ion batteries use an embedded lithium compound as one of their electrode materials. Currently, common cathode materials used in lithium-ion batteries include lithium cobalt oxide (LiCoO2), lithium manganese oxide (LiMn2O4), lithium nickel oxide (LiNiO2), and lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4).

Lithium-ion batteries come in various forms based on their packaging. Generally, they are categorized into cylindrical, prismatic, and pouch (polymer) lithium-ion batteries.

## Square Lithium-Ion Batteries

Square lithium-ion batteries typically refer to those housed in aluminum or steel cases. These batteries have a relatively simple structure and higher energy density compared to cylindrical cells.

For single cylindrical 18650 cells (1.75AH), the energy density ratio can reach up to 215Wh/kg. For square cells with a capacity of 50AH, the energy density ratio can be as high as 205Wh/kg. The system grouping efficiency for 18650 cells is around 60%, while that for square cells is about 70%. This means that the system energy density ratio for 18650 batteries is approximately 129Wh/kg, whereas it is around 143Wh/kg for square batteries.

Additionally, due to their larger individual cell size, square batteries exhibit better consistency in performance under the same power conditions compared to more numerous cylindrical cells. However, because of the high degree of customization, there are currently thousands of different models on the market, making it difficult to standardize production processes.

## Soft Pack Lithium-Ion Batteries (Polymer Lithium-Ion Batteries)

Soft pack lithium-ion batteries, also known as polymer lithium-ion batteries, are a type of rechargeable battery that wraps a polymer shell around liquid lithium-ion cells. The soft packaging typically consists of three layers: an outer barrier layer (usually made from BOPA or PET for external protection), a barrier layer (the middle aluminum foil layer), and an inner functional layer with high barrier properties.

## Why Do We Need BMS?

Q: Because the capacity, position, and working conditions of individual battery cells are not entirely consistent. Without a unified battery management system (BMS), it's easy for one cell to be overcharged or discharged, leading to potential failures in the entire battery pack. The purpose of a BMS is to monitor and manage each cell.

### Functions of BMS in Electric Vehicles

> In electric vehicles, BMS can perform the following functions:
>
1. Monitor the voltage and current status of each battery cell, allowing high-voltage cells to discharge while low-voltage cells continue charging, thereby maintaining balance within the entire battery pack and slowing down overall degradation.
2. Estimate the state of charge (SOC) of the battery pack based on monitored parameters such as voltage and current, ensuring that SOC remains within a reasonable range.
3. Monitor the temperature of different parts of the battery pack and control cooling through an integrated thermal management system to keep temperatures within optimal working ranges.
4. Detect any leakage issues in the battery pack; immediately alert if abnormalities are detected.
5. Communicate with other vehicle systems in real-time, providing current battery status parameters.
6. Record usage history data for each cell for offline analysis.

## Current Trends in the Power Tool Industry

Currently, lithium-ion battery products are beginning to platformize within the power tool industry. For instance, TTI's lithium-ion batteries primarily fall into two platforms: 12V and 18V.

From a product perspective, a common battery platform reduces development costs for individual devices and significantly cuts back on BMS (Battery Management System) expenses.

On the sales front, a unified battery platform allows different power tools to share compatible batteries. This means that when consumers purchase new power tools, they will prioritize those with battery packs that match their existing products at home. From this standpoint, larger companies are likely to become even more dominant, while smaller firms may struggle to pose significant threats.
