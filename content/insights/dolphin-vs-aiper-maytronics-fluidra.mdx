**Dolphin is generally the stronger fit for buyers who prioritize an established manufacturer, a broad residential and commercial range, authorized dealers, repair capability and parts support. Aiper is generally the stronger fit for channels seeking a cordless-first residential lineup, consumer-electronics-style product development and broad digital or mass-retail distribution.**

That is a procurement answer, not a laboratory verdict. Dolphin and Aiper each sell multiple models across different price and feature levels. Pool shape, surface, debris, cleaning frequency and local support can change the outcome. A fair comparison begins with the exact SKU and ends with the seller, service route and written warranty.

The corporate structures are also different. Dolphin is a Maytronics brand, and Maytronics reports that it develops and manufactures Dolphin robotic pool cleaners. Aiper operates through a multi-entity group and an external manufacturing network. Fluidra owns 27% of Aiper Inc. and shares control with W&D United, but Fluidra does not own Aiper outright.

## One Search Query, Two Different Decisions

### For a pool owner

Start with the exact model and pool. Aiper is the more natural starting point for a cordless-first residential search. Dolphin offers both cordless Liberty products and a wider corded range, with local dealer support available in many markets. Pool shape, surface, filtration, cleaning coverage, lifting weight, price and the nearest service route should decide the final purchase.

### For a distributor, dealer or pool service company

Start with the operating system behind the product. Dolphin offers broader residential and commercial coverage, established parts and a visible professional service structure. Aiper offers a focused cordless story, fast product cycles and access to major digital, home-improvement and pool retailers. Margin after returns, replacement inventory, warranty freight, channel conflict and parts availability matter more than a headline specification.

## Who Makes Dolphin and Aiper Pool Cleaners?

**Maytronics makes Dolphin robotic pool cleaners.** The company’s [official timeline](https://www.maytronics.com/corporate/maytronics-timeline.html) traces the brand to 1983, and its [2025 annual report](https://brand.maytronics.com/asset/a6e748f3-5756-4108-9775-5f7401c1dc37/2025-Annual-Report-HEB.pdf) describes the development and manufacture of residential and commercial Dolphin robots as Maytronics businesses.

Dolphin is therefore more than a label placed on an unrelated manufacturer’s product. Maytronics owns the core brand, develops the machines and operates the manufacturing system behind them. Regional subsidiaries and dealers may handle sales or service, but those commercial layers do not replace Maytronics as the group manufacturer.

The Aiper answer needs more layers. Aiper’s group develops and markets its pool robots, and Shenzhen Aiper Intelligent appears in trademarks, product documents and regulatory records. Official transaction materials also show that the company uses external manufacturing partners. A [Spanish competition review](https://www.cnmc.es/sites/default/files/6510726.pdf) referred to two OEMs in China and one in India, while Fluidra’s earlier transaction announcement referred to manufacturing partners in China and Vietnam.

Those disclosures may reflect different dates, definitions or suppliers. They support a multi-country manufacturing network, not one permanent factory answer for every Aiper product. A distributor should obtain the model label, declaration, manual, country-of-origin record and supplier documents for the exact batch.

World Clean Biz’s detailed guides explain the two structures separately: [Who Makes Dolphin Pool Cleaners?](https://worldcleanbiz.com/blog/who-makes-dolphin-pool-cleaners-maytronics) and [Who Owns Aiper?](https://worldcleanbiz.com/blog/who-owns-aiper-fluidra-stake).

## Is Dolphin Simply the Corded Brand and Aiper the Cordless Brand?

No. That description is outdated.

Aiper built its identity around cordless robotic pool cleaning. Its [current U.S. catalog](https://aiper.com/us/home) presents Scuba, Seagull and related battery-powered cleaners alongside EcoSurfer skimmers, HydroComm devices and IrriSense yard products. Cordless design is central to the brand’s product and marketing system.

Dolphin still has a large installed base and current range of corded robots, but Maytronics also sells the battery-powered Liberty family. The official [Dolphin Liberty 600 page](https://maytronics.com/en-us/store/residential-pools/cordless-cleaners/dolphin-liberty-600/99998600-US.html) lists a cordless robot with app functions, multi-layer filtration and a three-year U.S. product warranty. At the same time, products such as the [Dolphin Premium 60](https://www.maytronics.com/en-us/store/residential-pools/dolphin-premium-60/99996612-PR6.html) retain the plug-in architecture, app control and weekly scheduling associated with higher-end corded Dolphin models.

The useful distinction is product-system depth.

- Aiper is **cordless-first** across its consumer pool-cleaning identity.
- Dolphin supports **corded and cordless** routes under one established brand.
- Dolphin also maintains a [commercial Wave range](https://www.maytronics.com/en-us/robots-commercial-pools.html) for larger and more intensively used pools.
- Aiper’s public U.S. catalog is organized mainly around residential pool and smart-yard use.

This difference affects assortment planning. A retailer built around easy-to-ship consumer technology may value a coherent cordless story. A pool professional serving residential, hospitality, school or municipal customers may need more product classes, service tools and commercial references.

It does not prove that every Dolphin is more durable or every Aiper is easier to use. Those are model-level claims requiring comparable testing and service data.

## How Do the Company Structures Differ?

Maytronics is an Israeli public company. Kibbutz Yizre’el is identified as its controlling shareholder, while other investors own the publicly traded shares. The controlling shareholder, listed manufacturer, Dolphin brand and regional Maytronics subsidiaries are connected but remain distinct legal and commercial layers.

Aiper Inc. is a private Cayman Islands group company. In December 2025, Fluidra completed an approximately $100 million investment through newly issued shares and obtained a [27% interest in Aiper](https://www.fluidra.com/press-room/press-release/fluidra-completes-an-initial-27-investment-in-aiper/). Spanish competition authorities classify the completed transaction as joint control by Fluidra Commercial and W&D United.

Fluidra’s involvement matters. It brings a global pool-equipment group, professional channels and a planned path toward a future majority interest if specified financial conditions are met. It does not turn Aiper into a wholly owned Fluidra subsidiary today. A Fluidra shareholding also does not make Fluidra the automatic invoice seller, importer or warranty provider for every Aiper machine.

For procurement, the practical question is not only “Who owns the brand?” It is “Which entity signs this agreement, issues the invoice, owns the regional inventory, imports the product and accepts the warranty obligation?”

## Which Brand Has the Stronger Channel and Service System?

Dolphin’s main structural advantage is the depth of the Maytronics pool-industry network.

Maytronics sells through professional dealers, mass-market accounts and online channels. Its U.S. and Canadian [store locator](https://www.maytronics.com/en-us/store-locator/) distinguishes certified service, warranty processing, commercial resellers, showrooms, trained technicians and certified parts. That classification lets a buyer check whether a nearby business merely sells a Dolphin or can also diagnose and repair it.

The network still requires transaction-level verification. A retailer may sell an authorized product without repairing it. A channel-exclusive model may have a different name, color, accessory pack or feature set. Direct-website terms do not automatically apply to a dealer invoice. Parts compatibility should be checked by product number rather than appearance.

Aiper uses a more consumer-facing channel architecture. Its U.S. [authorized sales list](https://aiper.com/us/authorized-sales-channels) includes the official store and large digital, home-improvement and pool retailers. The company also provides centralized support, repair requests, warranty processing and proof-of-purchase workflows through its website.

That model can offer broad reach and simpler consumer discovery. It can also move more responsibility into remote troubleshooting, shipping and centralized claim handling. The actual experience depends on the country, retailer and model. A professional buyer should ask where a failed unit goes, who pays freight, whether local repair is available, what replacement stock is held and how long parts remain orderable.

The right comparison is therefore not “dealers versus the internet.” Both brands use multiple channels. The difference is how much of the service system is visible and executable near the end customer.

## How Do Dolphin and Aiper Warranties Compare?

Neither brand has one worldwide warranty duration.

Maytronics’ [global warranty FAQ](https://www.maytronics.com/global/support/faq/?question=how-long-is-the-warranty-of-my-dolphin) says Dolphin warranties typically range from 24 to 36 months and directs owners to the exact product page. Some regional or model pages list shorter coverage, so “typically” is not a brand-wide minimum. Its registration system asks for the serial number and purchase invoice, and it states that coverage applies in the country where the cleaner was purchased.

Aiper’s [U.S. refund and warranty policy](https://aiper.com/us/refund-and-warranty), updated in April 2026, lists product periods from 12 to 36 months. It also gives shorter periods for certain accessories and requires valid proof of purchase from the official site or an authorized channel. The company’s authorized-seller page says diverted or unauthorized sales are not covered.

This creates four recurring risks for distributors and cross-border buyers:

1. **Wrong warranty assumption:** a flagship model’s three-year coverage is copied to an entry model with a shorter term.
2. **Gray-market inventory:** the product is genuine but was bought outside the authorized channel or intended country.
3. **Seller mismatch:** the customer expects the brand website to honor return terms that only apply to direct purchases.
4. **Freight and downtime:** the written warranty exists, but the repair route requires international or long-distance shipping.

Warranty length is only one cost variable. Buyers should compare exclusions, batteries, chargers, wear parts, labor, freight, replacement policy, turnaround time and parts availability.

## What Should a Distributor or Procurement Team Verify?

### Corporate and territory

- The legal brand owner in the target country and product class.
- The entity signing the distribution or supply agreement.
- Territory, channel and exclusivity rights.
- Whether Fluidra’s Aiper relationship changes any local agreement—or is irrelevant to it.

### Product and manufacturing

- Exact model number, product number, EAN/UPC and revision.
- Legal manufacturer named in the manual, label and declaration.
- Physical country of origin for the shipment.
- Factory audit, quality documentation and traceability where required.
- Battery transport, charger, plug, radio and chemical-exposure compliance.

### Channel and after-sales

- Authorized-seller status on the purchase date.
- Invoice seller, importer of record and local responsible party.
- Warranty duration and exclusions for the exact model.
- Battery, charger, filter, brush, motor, track and power-supply coverage.
- Repair location, freight allocation, turnaround target and replacement policy.
- Spare-parts prices, minimum order, stocking plan and years of availability.

### Commercial decision

- Net landed cost rather than promotional retail price.
- Margin after returns, warranty freight and replacement inventory.
- Channel conflict between direct stores, marketplaces, mass retail and dealers.
- Product-life-cycle protection when a new generation launches.
- Sell-through, return rate and service data for the intended market—not global marketing totals.

## Can Brand Marketing Settle the Dolphin vs Aiper Question?

No. Both companies publish comparisons and performance claims designed to support their products.

Maytronics maintains an official Dolphin-versus-Aiper page. It is useful evidence that the search question matters and that Maytronics positions its dealer, filtration and service system as competitive advantages. It is still content produced by one side of the comparison.

Aiper promotes cordless design, navigation, suction, sensors and claimed category leadership. Those pages accurately describe the company’s product strategy, but superlatives such as “first,” “best” or “strongest” require matching definitions and independent testing before WCB can adopt them as conclusions.

For buyers, a controlled test should use the same pool, debris load, cleaning area, water condition, cycle definition and maintenance standard. The test should record missed areas, fine-particle capture, retrieval weight, cleaning time, recharge or power use, filter maintenance, fault rate and support response. Specification sheets alone cannot supply that evidence.

## FAQ

### Is Aiper better than Dolphin?

Aiper can be the better fit for a buyer who wants a cordless-first residential product and is comfortable with its authorized retail and centralized support model. Dolphin can be the better fit when dealer repair, commercial options, parts access and an established manufacturer matter more. Exact models and local support determine the practical result.

### Is Dolphin better than Aiper?

Not in every use case. Dolphin has a broader product and service system, but that does not prove that every Dolphin model outperforms every Aiper. Match the pool, product class, price, seller and warranty before choosing.

### Who owns Dolphin?

Dolphin is a Maytronics brand. Maytronics is publicly traded in Israel and controlled by Kibbutz Yizre’el. The kibbutz is the controlling shareholder, not the product manufacturer or sole owner of all public shares.

### Is Aiper owned by Fluidra?

Fluidra owns 27% of Aiper Inc. and shares control with W&D United after the December 2025 transaction. Fluidra does not currently own Aiper outright, and the announced path to a future majority stake has not been reported as completed.

### Are Dolphin pool cleaners cordless?

Some are. Maytronics sells cordless Dolphin Liberty models as well as corded residential products and commercial Wave robots. “Dolphin equals corded” is no longer an accurate category description.

### Are all Aiper pool cleaners cordless?

Cordless pool cleaning is central to Aiper’s current brand and product range. Buyers should still verify the power and charging architecture of the exact model and any accessory or pool-monitoring device in the package.

### Are Dolphin and Aiper made in the same country?

Maytronics reports that it develops and manufactures Dolphin robots, with core production in Israel. Aiper’s disclosed manufacturing network includes multiple Asian OEMs and countries. The exact country of origin must be checked on each model and shipment.

### Which brand has the better warranty?

There is no brand-wide answer. Both use model- and country-specific terms. Dolphin’s global FAQ says coverage is typically 24–36 months, although some regional/model pages are shorter; Aiper’s current U.S. policy lists 12–36 months depending on the product. Authorization, proof of purchase, exclusions, freight and repair access can matter as much as the stated duration.

### Can I import a Dolphin or Aiper from another country and keep the warranty?

Do not assume so. Maytronics states that warranty coverage applies in the purchase country, while Aiper restricts its warranty by country of receipt and authorized channel. Cross-border buyers should obtain written confirmation before purchase.

## Final Answer

The most accurate Dolphin vs Aiper conclusion is a comparison of operating systems.

Dolphin is backed by Maytronics’ integrated brand, manufacturing, residential, commercial, dealer, parts and service structure. Aiper is a cordless-first smart-yard brand with a multi-OEM supply network, broad consumer retail presence and a strategic relationship with Fluidra, which owns 27% and shares control with W&D United.

Dolphin is the safer starting point for many professional, commercial and service-intensive purchases. Aiper may be the stronger starting point for cordless residential assortments and fast-moving retail channels. The final decision still belongs to the exact model, authorized seller, local service capability, shipment origin and written warranty.
