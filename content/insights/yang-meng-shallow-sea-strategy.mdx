Today I saw an article by Huxiu interviewing Anker founder Yang Meng. The article had more than 15,000 words.


This interview systematically reviewed the past and present of Anker's shallow-sea strategy in previous years.

The shallow-sea strategy was a strategic theory proposed by Anker founder Yang Meng before 2020.

He divided consumer products into deep seas and shallow seas. Deep seas are markets such as computers, cars and CPUs, where a single category can reach the scale of hundreds of billions of U.S. dollars. Shallow seas are markets such as drones and robot vacuums, with scales of several billions or tens of billions of U.S. dollars.

Then, in this terrain, blue whales that focus on deep seas grow, such as Apple and Tesla. Great white sharks lead in one to ten shallow-sea categories, with typical companies such as Dyson. A third type is orca groups: corporate groups that succeed by integrating many shallow-sea categories, such as Procter & Gamble, Texas Instruments and Anta.

Among Fortune Global 500 companies, there are only blue whales and orca groups. Based on this reality, Anker wanted to become an orca group in shallow seas.

Then from 2020 to 2022, Anker began comprehensively advancing the shallow-sea strategy, expanding its product lines to as many as 27. But 20 of these 27 product lines could not beat competitors and were not competitive in the market. When the shallow-sea strategy Yang Meng imagined could not be realized because of people-related factors, he collapsed and once fell into severe depression.

After experiencing a series of darkest moments, Anker also began a series of self-rescue measures.

First was stopping the bleeding and contraction.

It cut product lines from 27 to 17. Any business that was not in the main track and could not use Anker's existing technical capabilities was cut.

Next was rectification.

It unified organizational thinking and values. First, more than half of first-level department heads were replaced, then the middle layer was influenced and new forces were cultivated. During this process, some people left, but the values of the entire company were unified.

Third, around the three focused directions of energy, audio-video and home automation, Anker dug deeply and built underlying common technology platforms.

During that period, Yang Meng fell into a darkest moment and came out through time and meditation. Afterward, he had an updated understanding of the shallow-sea strategy. Previously, he paid too much attention to the logic of things and ignored human judgment. Now the first step is to judge whether the person is right and whether values match, and only then look at the business.

In the future, how will Anker's platform attract talent and compete with unicorn companies? It will rely on sharing money. Anker's goal is to leave 70% of distributable profit to entrepreneurs and 30% to the company. In 2025, there were more than 800 people with annual income above RMB 1 million.

At the same time, Anker has the ability to support some businesses strategically losing money for several years. For example, the robot vacuum business lost nearly RMB 1 billion from 2021 to 2025 and only began to make a profit in 2025.

This is a very wonderful interview. You can see the struggles and confusion experienced by a top entrepreneur, from full confidence to falling into the abyss and then returning to the peak.

What surprised me was that the failure of the shallow-sea strategy in 2022 actually caused Yang Meng to fall into severe depression. The most frightening thing for entrepreneurs is not failure, but the collapse of belief. Later, he still came out through time. Most things are not that serious; time can smooth all wounds. If even big leaders go through this, small shrimp like us are nothing special.

As a small entrepreneur, I agree very much with many of Yang Meng's views. No matter when or where, opportunities always exist. Whether something can succeed does not depend on how much capital or resources there are, but on the core leader. If the right person is found, the probability of success is already half higher.

Anker's future success depends on talent. Attracting talent depends on the platform and money sharing. In future projects, 70% of returns should go to entrepreneurs. If the right people are found and money is distributed properly, business will naturally grow. China's most successful company, Huawei, relies on distributing large amounts of money and continuously attracting talent to become China's strongest technology company.

Values are something vague and intangible, and many people regard values as a tool for brainwashing. From a management perspective, when a company becomes large enough, because of physical distance, people cannot communicate often. Their understanding and judgment of the same simple matter can differ greatly. At this time, values are the simplest tool for building consensus.
