A few days ago I found a report on the future market growth ofrobot vacuum from Roborock's Hong Kong IPO prospectus.


Globalrobot vacuum sales in 2024 are expected to reach 20.6 million units with revenues of $9.3 billion, and by 2029, they are projected to grow to 43.2 million units with revenues of $25.2 billion.


Roborock's revenue for 2024 is RMB11.9 billion, with a target of over RMB70 billion in 2029, representing nearly six times the growth. Such a large market share will inevitably attract everyone's attention. Therefore, let us discuss together the recent and future landscape of therobot vacuum industry along with DJI's entry.


Firstly, Roborock: In 2024, Roborock's revenue is RMB11.9 billion, with 3.5 millionrobot vacuum units sold, generating RMB10.89 billion in sales; for washing machines and floor sweepers, it sold 619,000 units, generating RMB1.07 billion in sales. Currently, if we only considerrobot vacuum, Roborock should be the global leader. From an overall perspective, Roborock appears to have a strong combat capability, with a young and stable team, high efficiency, and very high compound annual growth rates.


Roborock's current disadvantage is that it has not yet found its second growth curve. Althoughrobot vacuum sales are robust, there is no significant gap compared to other competitors in terms of technology; the top few should be on par with each other. The growth of floor sweepers is also impressive, but it will be difficult for them to achieve the current industry status ofrobot vacuum. As for the main promotional washing machine, I believe that it may only see regular updates annually without significant investment.


What could Roborock's second growth curve be? I think it would be garden robots. Currently, lawn mowers and pool cleaners have already proven market capacity; the next challenge is to invest in talent. According to various sources, Roborock's lawn mower should start shipping this year with several tens of thousands of units, aiming for 200,000 by next year. Pool cleaners are expected to be launched next year as well. I believe that Roborock has the capability to challenge other garden robot companies and become a leader in the industry.


---------------

Next is Ecovacs. In 2024, Ecovacs' total revenue is RMB165.42 billion, with globalrobot vacuum sales of 2.95 million units and revenues of RMB80.82 billion; for the Tineco brand (mainly floor sweepers), it sold 4.14 million units generating RMB80.61 billion in revenue.


Ecovacs quickly recovered from its losses in 2023, with both brands showing good growth in 2024. Inrobot vacuum, Ecovacs is behind Roborock. For 2025, Ecovacs' goal should be to achieve double-digit billion sales, which shouldn't be too difficult.


Ecovacs currently has an advantage: the team has recovered from its losses in 2023, and newrobot vacuum products have gained market recognition under President Qian's leadership. The stable team is now firmly established.


With the increasing volume of floor sweepers overseas, Tineco's growth can be expected. This is good news for Ecovacs Group. I previously did not see much potential in the stir-fry pots, but they reportedly sell over 100,000 units annually in this price segment, which is a significant achievement.


Currently, Ecovacs has self-developed garden robots, selling several tens of thousands of units per year and now chasing Ninebot, Kuma, etc. The new investment in pool robot companies will take some time to show results.

Roborock's strengths and weaknesses in the vacuum cleaner and floor cleaning machine markets are clear. To achieve sales of 3-5 billion yuan, lawn robots and pool robots will also be essential points to compete for. The competitors in this field are not to be underestimated. Does Roborock have enough top-tier talent to lead the competition with other giants in lawn and pool robots?

Next is Zhiyi Technology. Zhiyi has always been a phenomenon in the industry over these years, reportedly achieving revenues of over 10 billion yuan in 2024, most of which came from cleaning appliances. In my previous articles, I mentioned that although Zhiyi has not gone public, its profitability depends on companies like ST Stones and Roborock. Once Zhiyi starts a price war and increases marketing investment, everyone will have to follow suit, diluting their own net profits.

Currently, Zhiyi's growth in vacuum cleaners is also quite strong; lawn robots launched last year reportedly reached over 100,000 units, and it aims for 200,000 this year. The intense competition in the cleaning appliance industry was largely driven by Zhiyi, but the entire industry should thank Zhiyi as well; without Zhiyi, current industry salaries might not be as high.

Zhiyi's fundamentals in cleaning appliances are still quite good, with clear strengths and weaknesses. Starting from zero in 2019 to achieving over 10 billion yuan, they created many miracles. However, the physical world follows certain laws; rapid growth is not a norm. Zhiyi needs to accept non-growth external environments; only by accepting such an environment can there be a chance for the next wave of growth.

For Yunjin, I recently made a video called "The Revival of Yunjin." We had been following this company since its crowdfunding phase and wrote many articles about them. Their team has experienced numerous ups and downs, making it very challenging. Last year, they reportedly achieved revenues close to 3 billion yuan, breaking even—a remarkable achievement for Yunjin considering the current market leaders are listed companies like ST Stones and Roborock, with Zhiyi burning a lot of money.

To be honest, Yunjin has made significant contributions to the vacuum cleaner industry; without their self-cleaning mopping robot with clean and dirty water tanks, the vacuum cleaner industry would not be as prosperous today.

Teams that have not undergone tests of life and death are incomplete. Starting last year, Yunjin began catching up with the industry's pace in vacuum cleaners and floor cleaning machines, even outperforming others in some areas. Their overseas market, which was previously absent, is now gradually expanding. Currently, Yunjin should be on a path to rapid growth; they need to stabilize their basic operations and achieve sales of around 5 billion yuan.

Yunjin's weakness is that compared to the leading companies in the industry, it remains relatively fragile with an unstable basic operation. It would be wise for them to focus on steady development. I've heard that Yunjin is preparing for an IPO; I wish Yunjin all the best.

Xiaomi. Xiaomi Technology's vacuum cleaners currently sell around 1-20 million units per year. Early on, companies like ST Stones, Zhiyi, and Senchuan's vacuum cleaner business grew through Xiaomi's channels and supply chain. However, as these companies have grown, they have gradually separated from Xiaomi. This is not just an issue for vacuum cleaners but the entire Xiaomi ecosystem; most of the early successful ecosystem companies have since left Xiaomi.

So how Xiaomi's ecosystem division handles its relationship with ecosystem companies will determine the future development of Xiaomi's core business.


I believe that in the future, Xiaomi's business model for robotic vacuum cleaners may follow that of major appliances, fully building its own factories to produce them. Currently, Xiaomi's major appliances are performing very well, and they aim to rank third nationwide (with Haier and Midea ahead). Recently, I saw that Xiaomi is recruiting a large number of people in Suzhou, which might indicate their resolve to establish their own base in the industry center rather than continuing to rely on Beijing. With the self-built factories based on Foxconn's technology, the next step may be to comprehensively compete with Roborock, Zhiwei Ximi, and Ecovacs.


Xiaomi's advantage in the robotic vacuum cleaner business lies in its channels; Xiaomi's global channel is impressive. It can leverage its mobile phone business to secure collaborations that others cannot. Moreover, Xiaomi has a good brand reputation overseas, with high consumer trust.


Xiaomi's disadvantage in the robotic vacuum cleaner business is its excessive reliance on Roborock, Zhiwei Ximi, and Sankuan for R&D capabilities. If they plan to build their own factories, it may take some time to catch up. Once Xiaomi fills these basic gaps, the impact of its global channels will be terrifying.

DJI Innovation


DJI is a revered existence in the tech industry, selling over 70 billion yuan worth of products annually and holding 70% of the consumer drone market share. No one has challenged DJI's position in the drone market. The first robotic vacuum cleaner from DJI was rumored for four years and finally released on August 6th.


From the perspective of its first product, it might be impressive to other companies but only average for DJI itself. This product currently offers no significant advantage over leading companies. According to supply chain reports, DJI placed orders with suppliers for 200,000 units per year, with the initial order being around 10,000 units.

From this single product alone, it might be difficult to challenge the dominant positions of leading companies. However, if continuously iterated over 2-3 years, there is still a chance. The only question now is how much commitment DJI has to this robotic vacuum cleaner project? If DJI believes that the business could potentially create another DJI in five years, their investment and commitment should be concerning for all industry players.

Anker Eufy


Anker Eufy has long been a major player in overseas robotic vacuum cleaners, selling over 1 million units annually at one point. Recently, I went through Anker's entire cleaning appliance product line. This video is worth watching if you have the time. Early on, Anker's best-selling models were cheaper ones; in recent years, domestic robotic vacuum cleaner technology has evolved rapidly, and Anker hasn't kept up as well as iRobot. Their market share has declined somewhat.


Last year, Anker launched new products for its robotic vacuum cleaners with more aggressive product development. They have brought to market some of the ideas we thought of but were too cautious to implement. From this perspective, Anker's resolve is significant.

For the domestic market, Anker likely won't enter; it's already a highly competitive environment where they would likely lose money.

In overseas markets, with more and more major Chinese companies going global and DJI and other top-tier companies entering the fray, overseas channels might be even stronger. How Anker responds to these situations and makes its choices will be an area of focus for everyone in the future.

Midea and Haier

Midea and Haier are major household appliance giants with annual sales in the tens of billions of yuan. Many friends unfamiliar with this industry often said that if Midea, Haier, and others entered, no one else would have a chance. However, reality has proven otherwise.

Previously, Fang Hongbo from Midea also mentioned this issue. Due to the rapid technological changes in therobot vacuumindustry over these years, it takes time for large companies like Midea and Haier to perceive and make decisions. If they wait until the product technology stabilizes and start competing on price and channels, their advantages will become apparent.

Therefore, the best strategy for Midea and Haier now is to stay in the game and wait for technological stability. I believe this point should be around 2027-2029. By 2029, the entire industry's product technology route should have unified, with a significantly increased market capacity (40 millionrobot vacuum). At that time, products will no longer be an advantage; it will be channels. Currently, the two companies with the strongest global channels are Midea and Haier. By then, they might create another growth curve in addition to their existing household appliance business.

iRobot and Ecovacs

Before 2019, iRobot was the sole dominant player in the industry, while Ecovacs was an unknown entity. Ecovacs leveraged its laser navigation technology and partnered with major companies like Xiaomi, whereas iRobot clung to visual navigation, leading to a gradual erosion of market share. Currently, iRobot's market value is less than 1 billion US dollars, and there are constant rumors about potential acquisitions.

However, no one expected that these two companies would eventually combine due to cost issues. Now, Ecovacs manufactures iRobot products, selecting some models for direct use from their own product line. This has given iRobot a certain level of price competitiveness in the market.

Ecovacs plans to produce 6 million units this year, which should be a significant lead over other companies in therobot vacuumcontract manufacturing industry. Previously, Ecovacs' autonomous brand 3i products were launched domestically with too much aggression and did not sell well; reportedly, they sold only a few thousand units per year. Now there are rumors that Ecovacs will act as an agent for iRobot's domestic brand and introduce two models: one priced around 2000 yuan with a disc washing all-in-one base station, and another priced around 4000 yuan with a tracked cleaning all-in-one base station.

It seems that Ecovacs is following the path of Xinbao acquiring Dyson, first trying in China before potentially acquiring other country authorizations. If not for political factors, it would indeed be an excellent choice for Ecovacs to acquire iRobot. iRobot's channels, patents, and brand have significant value for Ecovacs, while Ecovacs' product development and supply chain capabilities are scarce for iRobot. If these two companies truly combine, it should have some impact on the leading companies.

Due to political factors, the possibility of such a combination is low. However, with all the black swans we've seen in recent years, who knows what curveball might come? Anything is possible.

Moma Power. Moma is a subsidiary of Roborock, and now it is making significant strides in the market for robotic vacuum cleaners, reportedly aiming to produce 40-500,000 units this year. This number seems somewhat exaggerated. There are rumors that Songling has already begun laying out plans for home-use robotic vacuums, but it remains unclear whether these will fall under Moma or another brand.

Ninebot. Ninebot was once an enterprise in the Xiaomi ecosystem and initially gained momentum with its two-wheeled vehicles. In 2024, Ninebot's total sales reached RMB 14.196 billion, of which electric two-wheelers accounted for RMB 7.211 billion, balance scooters and skateboards for RMB 3.381 billion, all-terrain vehicles for RMB 0.976 billion, and robot business for RMB 0.895 billion. I previously read an interview with Ninebot's founder, Wang Ye, who mentioned that from the beginning, Ninebot was a robotics company, just entering the balance scooter business by chance. Due to regulatory restrictions on balance scooters, they began venturing into electric two-wheelers. He also stated that embodied intelligence will not be absent in their future plans. Currently, Ninebot is already among the leading companies for robotic lawn mowers, with sales of around 100,000 units last year and a target of approximately 250,000 units this year. Entering the market for robotic vacuum cleaners should only be a matter of time.

Yunda Intelligent has been making significant progress in pool robots, reportedly becoming the second-largest brand globally in this field. Recently, they have secured several rounds of funding and now cover the entire product line from high-end to low-end in pool robots. They have also recently launched lawn robot products. Facing competition from Ecovacs, Roborock, and Zhiwei, entering the market for robotic vacuum cleaners is inevitable.

Xingmai Innovation. Founded by Wang Shengle, one of the original co-founders of Zhiwei, Xingmai Innovation released its Beatbot pool robot last year, which sold 500 million yuan in its first year, making it the top brand in high-end pool robots. They have also secured several rounds of additional funding and are currently on a strong growth trajectory. If trends continue to merge, entering the market for robotic vacuum cleaners should be a matter of time.
