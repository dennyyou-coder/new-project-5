In 2018, a significant development in the coffee industry was Starbucks' re-entry into Italy with a new flagship store. Having previously been defeated by local Italian coffee shops, Starbucks aimed to make a comeback and see if it could establish itself in this coffee-loving country.

It is no surprise that the leading coffee machine brand found its way to Italy, given how much Italians love their coffee. The century-old De'Longhi Group, founded by the De'Longhi family in 1902, has a history spanning 116 years and is headquartered in Italy.

In 1979, the company acquired Supercalor, which specialized in heating appliances.

In 1985, De'Longhi first ventured into home appliances with Sfornatutto.

![Original image 2](/images/insights/delonghi-small-appliance-brand-history-image-002.jpg)

In 1986, the company acquired Elba, which produced standalone cookware and built-in ovens.

In 1986, the company acquired Radiators, which manufactured circulation water heating units for central heating systems.

In 1987, the company acquired Ariagel, which produced air conditioners.

In 1986, De'Longhi developed Pinguino, a portable install-and-go air conditioner launched globally.

![Original image 3](/images/insights/delonghi-small-appliance-brand-history-image-003.jpg)

In 1987, the De'Longhi brand launched the FriggiMedlio deep fryer product.

![Original image 4](/images/insights/delonghi-small-appliance-brand-history-image-004.jpg)

In 1989, the company acquired Vetrella, a manufacturer of floor care products.

In 1992, the first ride-on vacuum cleaner, Colombina, was launched.

![Original image 5](/images/insights/delonghi-small-appliance-brand-history-image-005.jpg)

1994, Climaveneta joined the De’Longhi Group.

In 1995, the company acquired Simac Micromax, which produced ironing products and food processing appliances.

In 2001, the De’Longhi Group listed on the stock exchange in Milan, Italy.

During the same period, De’Longhi established subsidiaries in major countries across Europe and other continents, including De’Longhi USA (1988), De’Longhi France (1990), De’Longhi Netherlands (1993), De’Longhi Germany, and De’Longhi Japan. Additionally, the company set up offices in Moscow (1995), Shanghai (1996), Canada (1997), and Belgium (1999).

In 2001, the company acquired the renowned small appliance brand Kenwood (Kenwood) from Britain, along with the Ariete brand under Kenwood. Following the acquisition of Kenwood, De’Longhi began using the manufacturing facility in Dongguan Qingxi, China.

In 2012, the company acquired Braun’s (Braun) permanent rights for small appliances from Procter & Gamble, while P&G retained ownership of the Braun brand.

Currently, the De’Longhi Group has four major brands:

De’Longhi, Kenwood, Braun, Ariete.

Currently, the De'Longhi group generates annual sales of approximately 2 billion euros. Sales for the first nine months of 2018 amounted to about 1.3 billion euros.

![Original image 11](/images/insights/delonghi-small-appliance-brand-history-image-011.jpg)

Currently, the group's market distribution is as follows: Germany accounts for 12%, with North American markets right behind at about 11%, the Italian domestic market at 10%, Russia, Ukraine, and CIS markets at 8%, and China at 4%.

![Original image 12](/images/insights/delonghi-small-appliance-brand-history-image-012.jpg)

Divided by product lines, coffee machines account for 46% of the group's sales, food preparation/cooking products account for 31%, and Confort and home care products account for 23%.
