c. Mopping Function and Introduction, Pros and Cons (Who Should Purchase a Mopping Machine)

It is not surprising that Bissell invented the mopping machine because it has long been renowned as a leading cleaning appliance brand in America, particularly dominant in water-based cleaning products such as wet vacuums. The mopping machine originated from carpet washers and was later adapted to suit Chinese market needs by changing the brush roll into a plush brush.

### Current Advantages of Mopping Machines:

- All dry and wet waste can be handled.
- The mopping module is suitable for the Chinese market.
- Self-cleaning modules simplify subsequent cleaning of the brush roll.

However, mopping machines are not as perfect as some influencers claim. Currently, there are several issues:

1. **Water Stains**: Due to continuous water supply from the pump to the brush roll, this allows real-time live water cleaning of stains. However, the brush roll remains wet, leading to a continuously damp floor (imagine using a wet towel to mop). During our live stream, many friends purchased various brands of mopping machines and returned some due to water stains.

### Solutions:
- Most brands have similar water stain issues; dual-brush models tend to have more because they have a larger contact area.
  
2. **Brush Roll Not Drying Properly and Developing Odors**: Currently, the brush roll is made of plush fabric. After mopping and self-cleaning, the brush roll remains moist. In humid regions like during the rainy season, if it does not dry properly, odors can develop. This is a common complaint.

### Solutions:
- The industry mainly uses two solutions: 1) High-speed rotation for centrifugal drying; motor-driven with scraper blades to remove moisture. 2) Low-temperature low-speed drying using separate drying speeds and blowing warm air to dry the brush roll.
  
Both methods yield similar results, improving on the original lack of drying. Some brands like Fangtai MiBo use a design without a brush roll, thus avoiding odor issues.

3. **Choosing Between Dual Brush Roll and Single Brush Roll**:

Some readers might think that dual-brush rolls offer stronger cleaning power than single-brush models. However, in our tests, the cleaning power of dual-brush models was not significantly better than single-brush models. In actual use, a single-brush model offers better overall performance. The advantage of dual-brush models is their relative motion, providing additional pulling force when moving backward, making it easier to pull.

4. **Choosing Mopping Machine Motors**:

Most mopping machine motors are in the 250W-350W range. For most use cases on hard floors, a brushless motor with around 250W is sufficient for balancing suction power and battery life. Larger motor power requires larger batteries, increasing costs and weight.

Models like Dreame M12 and M13 can switch to vacuum mode by removing the dust bucket component, requiring a 350W brushless motor when adjusting suction strength. However, it is important to note that even with a 350W motor, the daily usage power of the hard floor washer remains around 250W.

Additionally, most mopping machine manufacturers use motors from the same supplier, making little difference between them. Therefore, there is no need for excessive concern; a 250W motor is sufficient.

Additionally: Some budget models, similar to Midea X7 or Deerma VX100, use ordinary brushed motors for cost reasons (which can be about 2-3 times cheaper in terms of cost). The performance difference between brushed and brushless motors is significant. It's generally advisable to choose a brushless motor. Here’s an easy way to distinguish them: typically, brushed motors do not exceed 50,000 RPM.

### 6. Is Electrolyzed Water or Disinfectant Liquid Useful?

Since Tineco first introduced the concept of electrolyzed water/disinfectant liquid, which involves electrolyzing tap water to produce hypochlorous acid for disinfection and sterilization, this feature has become a standard in high-end models. Many people have asked me whether this function is just a form of "tax on intelligence" or mere deception. First, the function does have some effect but it is limited.

There are mainly two aspects:

1. Preparing electrolyzed water requires time; often, by the time you pick up the device to use it, the electrolyzed water may not have been generated yet.
2. The sterilization effect of electrolyzed water is limited due to the distance from the clean water tank to the roller brush, where some efficacy might be lost.
3. Electrolyzed water works well against certain bacteria but less effectively against others.

### Summary: Electrolyzed water/disinfectant liquid has some effectiveness but it is limited. This function does not need much consideration. The actual effective sterilization and disinfection are provided by the disinfectant liquids that come with each brand, which have been used for decades in the United States and are both safe and effective.

### 7. Choosing Between Single-Edge and Double-Edge Mop Heads

Firstly, it is clear that a single-edge mop head is better than no edge at all, and a double-edge mop head is even better. However, the difference is minimal.

Primarily, edge mops are designed for cleaning corners and edges. If there is some dry debris in the corner, both types of floor washers can suck it up. The main difference lies in that without an edge, small parts might be left uncleaned during the mopping process.

Currently, most mid-to-high-end models have started to include edge mops as a standard feature with no additional cost increase.

### 8. Can I Use 84 Disinfectant or Dish Soap with My Floor Washer?

Many people ask if they can use household dish soap or 84 disinfectant instead of the expensive cleaning solutions after purchasing a floor washer for over 3,000 RMB. Based on our actual tests, neither dish soap nor 84 disinfectant should be directly added to the floor washer as their different pH levels may damage the plastic components inside.

Even between different brands, cleaning liquids cannot be interchanged due to varying pH levels.

### Summary: For now, cleaning solutions cannot be used casually. As the industry matures, the cost of these solutions is expected to decrease significantly.
