In the small-appliance industry, if you do not know SharkNinja, you probably have not entered the industry yet.

![Generated industry image](/images/insights/sharkninja-road-to-10-billion-dollars-image-01.jpg)

As one of the companies I personally admire and like most, what I admire about Shark is its ability to continuously break through itself.

My earliest contact with people from SharkNinja should have been around 2012. At that time, their company name was still Euro-Pro. Later, because the brands developed so well, the company name was changed to match the brands.

Then in 2018, the entire brand was acquired by Joyoung for RMB 10 billion. I was also the first to report this matter that year, which made me suddenly popular in the industry overnight. I previously made a video on this topic; those interested can watch it.

SharkNinja listed on Nasdaq in 2022. Its 2022 sales data was USD 3.7 billion. By 2024, this figure had become USD 5.5 billion. This growth data is simply exaggerated.

Next, let us look at where Shark's growth points are and why it could achieve them.

First, let us briefly look at some SharkNinja figures. In 2024, SN achieved USD 5.5 billion in sales, of which Shark contributed USD 2.6 billion and Ninja contributed USD 2.9 billion. Compared with 2022 data, Shark's sales then were USD 2.0 billion and Ninja's were USD 1.7 billion. In recent years, the main growth point has been Ninja, which grew by USD 1.2 billion.

In terms of markets, it strongly promoted internationalization, and international markets contributed USD 1.7 billion in sales. In the United Kingdom and Japan, Shark's market share is very high.

Its globalized supply chain is also very strong. At the beginning of the trade war, it started laying out a global supply chain. At present, it has production and manufacturing bases in Southeast Asia and other countries. SN's supply-chain team is the strongest I have personally seen. Once the team sets a goal, it basically achieves it at any cost. When normal companies develop new products, making three to five prototypes is already a relatively large investment. Some friends previously said that for Shark's new vacuum cleaner products, they would make 100 hand-made prototypes and send them to the United States for consumers to use and provide feedback. The cost involved is extremely high.

SN's growth in cleaning categories is not very large, but there are also several opportunities Shark has not yet seized and released. One is hard floor washers, where Shark's volume is still small. Another is robot vacuums with stations, which still need improvement.

I think another major growth point is Shark's corded upright vacuum cleaners. Once these vacuum cleaners are converted to lithium batteries, they will bring very large growth. Therefore, how Shark leads the industry in this area in the future is also something cleaning-appliance practitioners watch closely.

Ninja has added many categories, mainly because it entered categories such as coffee machines, outdoor appliances and personal-care appliances, including hair dryers and facial-mask devices. Products like hair dryers sold several million units within one year of launch, which is extremely exaggerated.

If the likes are high, I will make a video focusing on Shark's high-growth product lines.

Why could it achieve such results?

Here is Shark's own summary.

### 1. Disruptive Innovation

They have more than 1,000 engineers and designers globally constantly thinking about various innovations. Teams in the United States, the United Kingdom and China communicate 24 hours a day, seven days a week, and the latest ideas are quickly tested and realized in the shortest time. Globally, they have more than 5,200 patents. To be honest, in cleaning appliances, Dyson has not been doing very well in recent years. The only company still continuously innovating is Shark. Everyone is watching Shark's new products.

### 2. Global Agile Supply Chain

The global supply-chain layout has now been completed. China, Thailand, Vietnam, Singapore, Malaysia and other places all have suppliers, and core products have two to three suppliers opening molds and shipping simultaneously.

A flexible supply chain can quickly change according to market changes. The benefit is that even if external changes occur, the company can adjust supply chains at any time to meet the market. For example, with tariffs, SN can switch among these six countries to ensure its tariff level is not higher than competitors'.

The advantage of having two to three suppliers open molds simultaneously is that if product orders explode, the company will not miss the order boom because of production bottlenecks. Multiple suppliers also allow continuous price pressure, ensuring purchasing-price advantages.

### 3. 360-Degree Marketing

Since 2009, Shark has had very strong marketing capability. In the early stage, its products benchmarked against Dyson, ensuring that its parameters beat these rivals while the price was only half. This strategy was very successful and successfully tied Shark together with Dyson in consumers' minds, somewhat similar to Xiaomi in China. Later social-media marketing and other methods were also no problem.

### 4. Integrated Channels

SN summarizes its marketing and sales strategy as an integrated channel strategy.

In the past, when we went to offline malls to buy things, we had to see something before deciding whether to buy. If there was no need, we might not buy. What is different about SN is that it creates demand, then offline stores complete the demand loop.

A simple example: through TV, social media, livestreaming and other channels, they create various use scenarios. Then consumers complete purchases in offline stores, offline channels and similar places. This is similar to planting demand on Xiaohongshu and Douyin in China, then selling on Taobao and JD. The difference is that their demand creation and sales coverage are broader.

They also summarized three key growth points:

Increase within existing scenarios.

Develop more emerging product lines.

Enter more countries.

The above is some of their official information. I will also share some personal views.

In the small-appliance industry, I think SN's development capability and supply chain are both among the strongest tier.

Over the past ten-plus years, it has continuously opened up various impressive products. Among the companies I know, SN has the strongest execution. Once they set a timeline, they will achieve it together with suppliers at any cost. What is even more impressive now is that this capability has become a company system and can be continuously replicated into other industries. The high growth of Ninja's product lines in the past two years is an expression of this system. In the future, they can enter more categories and achieve greater growth. Personally, I feel that within five to eight years, SN is very likely to become a company with USD 10 billion in sales.

At present, SN's core is still its CEO, Mark Barrocas.

Mark B was a partner who worked with Mark R for many years. After Mark R left, Mark B took on the company's burden alone. Facts have proven that he has the ability to bring the company to a higher level. He is also still in his prime and can work for at least another 20 years.

In addition to the company's own culture, the core leadership team is extremely important.

I think SN's treatment of suppliers and employees is also worth learning from. SN's employee compensation has always been among the highest in the industry. Even if overtime is needed, people do not complain much. For suppliers, SN orders should have relatively high profits, and if SN asks suppliers to develop products, it pays development fees. Customers with large order volumes, high profits and little hesitation are welcomed everywhere.

Ordinary companies can let customers make money with them. Great companies can also let employees and suppliers make money. Huawei, Apple, Dyson and others are all like this.

I am only a very small blogger, but five or six years ago they could already connect to this extent. This shows how strong their marketing coverage is.
