## Billion-Dollar Surge in Cleaning Appliances: The Rise of Roborock's Wet Mop

![Hard floor washer products and components](/images/insights/story-of-hard-floor-washers-image-01.jpg)

Recent years have seen a massive influx of capital into the cleaning appliance market. A key catalyst for this surge was the success of Tineco's hard floor washers.

Tineco is a subsidiary brand under Ecovacs, focusing on smart home appliances. At that time, Ecovacs' robot vacuum cleaners were highly successful. When Tineco first approached me in 2019 to discuss their business challenges, they expressed concern about breaking through the market. I suggested that with their cordless vacuums priced similarly to Dyson's, consumers would likely opt for the latter.

However, by 2020, Tineco broke through with its hard floor washers, achieving several billion in sales within a year and reaching tens of billions over the next two years. This led Ecovacs' stock price to soar from around $30 to as high as $200 in less than a year.

Tineco's success sparked interest across the industry, ushering in an era of rapid growth for cleaning appliances. Everyone told me, "Denny, this is our strategic opportunity; we must seize it at all costs."

The first hard floor washers were launched by American giant Bissell in 2018-2019, selling hundreds of thousands and generating over $100 million in revenue, propelling Bissell into the billion-dollar club. This product was developed by Bissell's Chinese team, primarily sold overseas at prices ranging from $400 to $500. I wrote about this product back then, lamenting that it wasn't available domestically.

In 2020, the hard floor washer became a phenomenon. Here’s a look at some of the key players in the domestic market:

- **Leaders:** Tineco (Ecovacs)
- **Foreign Giants:** Bissell, Shark, Dyson, Philips, Karcher
- ** Xiaomi Ecosystem:** Xiaomi, Dreame, SWDK, Shunzao, Roidmi, Viomi, Deerma
- **Traditional Cleaning Appliance Manufacturers:** Miduo (Aipu), Wanmou (Pufa), Kingclean (Tianlang)
- **Major Home Appliances Companies:** Midea (Youruijia), Haier, Supor, Ninebot, Gree
- **Robot Vacuum Giants:** Roborock, Narwal, Yinxing (internal)
- **Emerging Brands:** Bobot, Uwant, Drip, MiBo (Fotai subsidiary), Zhaoguang (smartphone industry), Narwal, Anker's Eufy

The number of participants in this market is unprecedented. Some companies invested hundreds of millions, while others started with several million. Over the years, total R&D and marketing investments for hard floor washers likely exceeded billions.

This list includes not just brands but also manufacturers producing hard floor washer molds. Factories like those in Suzhou (vacuum cleaners) and Shenzhen (robot vacuums such as Picea, LD Robot) were all vying to get a piece of the pie.

When Tineco became popular in 2020, everyone was looking for suitable manufacturers. At that time, this product category was niche, with only Bissell and some people from Tineco having experience. Other companies lacked relevant talent. The entire industry flocked to these two companies to find skilled engineers, offering salaries several times higher.

Design firms charged around $100,000 for a set of designs for traditional vacuums, but for hard floor washers, it was between $500,000 and $800,000. Early on, there were even rumors of selling blueprints for $5 million. Bissell's team was based in Shenzhen, while Tineco’s was in Suzhou, so most companies followed the Bissell style, with others adopting Tineco's.

In 2020, only Tineco and Bissell were major players; Tineco led by a wide margin. Bissell once held second place but struggled due to its American design, which was too muscular for Chinese markets. Its brushes were not suitable for carpets or floors, lacked self-propulsion, and were less effective at removing stubborn stains. Bissell's market position was temporary because other brands had yet to launch their products.

From 2020 to 2021, any company that could get a hard floor washer would earn substantial profits. A company called Shansmile in Dongguan had such products and earned over $100 million during those years.

Another Xiaomi ecosystem company, SWDK, had developed a hard floor washer but remained largely unknown despite early investments. They seemed to have missed the boat.

Tineco's hard floor washers were in high demand; on JD.com, they sometimes required pre-ordering with delivery times of 1-2 months. This led to significant profits for Tineco dealers. Consequently, all brands rushed to enter the market.

In 2021, Dreame was the fastest mover, releasing H11 and updating its products every two to three months. With $3.6 billion in funding, they leveraged their resources on TikTok, becoming the top brand in the industry within a few months. For several years, Tineco and Dreame dominated the market.

In early 2022, a new brand called Zhaoguang launched a dual-brush hard floor washer with an aluminum casing. They were backed by Luxshare Precision Industry, a smartphone manufacturer that had raised significant funds. We watched their online launch with concern; they seemed to have entered the market with formidable competition. However, Zhaoguang's product was a classic example of overpromising and underdelivering. Due to quality issues, it faced high returns—around 90% upon release—and quickly disappeared from the market.

## The Evolution of Cleaning Appliances in China

Midea Clean Appliances Division acquired the Eureka brand and decided to launch it in the Chinese market as a premium brand called Eureka. At that time, they hired Yi Yang Qianxi as an ambassador. Initially, both the Midea and Eureka brands operated concurrently.

Several companies were hiring celebrities for their campaigns at the time. Roborock chose Xiao Zhan, while Dreame partnered with Su Bingtian. The early progress in the high-end segment was not smooth until the launch of the GX5 model, which helped establish a foothold in the hard floor washer market.

Roborock's first product was a double-brush hard floor washer, which was seen as a breakthrough against Tineco's offerings. While their washers were well-received, there was criticism for adding a support leg to improve maneuverability, which we referred to as a "cane." At the time, Roborock's robot vacuum cleaners were thriving, and hard floor washers did not receive much attention. This year, they have invested heavily in hard floor washers, with the A30 cordless steam washer reportedly selling well during the 618 shopping festival.

After releasing the J1 robot vacuum, Narwal faced some confusion about its product direction. The first hard floor washer had a good design and craftsmanship but was released too late to compete effectively against industry giants. Over the years, Narwal has focused on improving both their robot vacuums and washers, leading to positive feedback.

Haier placed significant importance on hard floor washers, elevating its small business unit into a dedicated division. They invested 1 billion yuan in Picea Robotics in Shenzhen for better R&D capabilities. Haier's first double-brush washer was manufactured by Picea, which had established an office in Suzhou and recruited many talented individuals. Haier excelled on the Douyin platform, being one of the earliest companies to conduct live-stream sales. Their consistent sales via Douyin helped them maintain a top-three market share.

Xiaomi has always been focused on clean appliances. Some industry friends were concerned that if Xiaomi entered with high cost-performance, it would make it difficult for others to compete. However, Xiaomi was aiming to establish itself as an upscale brand and thus did not enter the market with cost-effective products. They launched a highly complex washer with a hot water base station, priced at over 3000 yuan. Due to its complexity and short development timeline, this product faced numerous issues upon launch and had limited sales. If Xiaomi had released a similar machine for around 2000 yuan in the first year, it might have sold one million units, making it difficult for others to compete at that price point.

Shunzao was also part of the Xiaomi ecosystem and saw significant growth after Roborock and Dreame's successes. Xiaomi's first high-temperature washer was manufactured by Shunzao, which did not perform as well as expected. Instead, their second model with a tracked design and flat body sold very well, leading to this feature becoming a standard in the market.

In 2019, Supor entered the clean appliance market. Some of their early leaders were familiar to me. They saw the hard floor washer market as strategic but invested less compared to Midea and Haier. Their efforts on the hard floor washer project remained moderate. A former colleague told me that they missed out on a strategic opportunity, but I advised them not to continue, as further investment might have resulted in significant losses.

Compared to Midea, Haier, and Supor's substantial investments in clean appliances, iLife was more conservative with their marketing efforts. The Eufy brand also saw limited success domestically. A common trend over the years is that heavy investment does not always translate into sales, while lack of investment guarantees no sales.

Anker's Eufy Mach. Anker placed great importance on the cleaning appliance project and hired many people in Suzhou but did not achieve significant success. Yang Meng later reflected on this project. Besides using the Eufy brand for traditional vacuum cleaners, they also established a separate Eufy Mach brand. Their first product was a cordless steam washer, which I analyzed as difficult to succeed. Eufy Mach sold only a small number of units and eventually stopped operations in China. It is rumored that Anker lost several hundred million yuan on this project.

Deerma has always been focused on clean appliances and collaborated with many Suzhou manufacturers. Deerma's small appliance products are known for their aesthetics and cost-effectiveness, but they struggled to match prices. During the hard floor washer wave, Deerma made a clear move by positioning themselves in the 1000 yuan price segment when giants were competing for higher segments. At that time, there was no major brand at this price point, only some white label products. Deerma likely achieved significant sales.

BoBot. BoBot initially sold dual-disc electric mops and could sell units priced around 2000 yuan while generating nearly two billion in annual revenue when other brands were selling for 3-500 yuan. In 2019, before the hard floor washer market exploded, they recognized its potential and recommended a factory with development capabilities to them. Unfortunately, they faced challenges from industry giants, leading to delayed product launches by half a year. They eventually exited the market due to other reasons.

## Hizero and DriWater

Hizero and DriWater were early players in the hard floor washer market. Their product design did not include a suction motor; instead, they used a sponge roller to collect debris. The advantage of this design was its excellent water absorption capability, leaving floors very dry after cleaning. Additionally, without a suction motor, it produced less noise and had a lower cost. Personally, I believe that as a low-cost hard floor washer, this product could be viable. Unfortunately, in 2022, the industry collectively chose to adopt traditional vacuum motors, making it nearly impossible for Hizero and DriWater to go against the current trend. Consequently, sponge roller washers are now seen as a historical branch of development.

## Uwant

Uwant is an exceptionally capable company that has driven several product categories in the market, such as dual-bucket deodorizers and fabric cleaning machines. Their first hard floor washer featured dual brushes with automatic hair cutting, showcasing some innovative features but also had various minor issues. Despite these challenges, Uwant has shown remarkable resilience over the years, surviving intense competition in China's cleaning appliance market. Currently, they sell tens of thousands of hard floor washers annually.

## Dyson

Before the rise of hard floor washers, Dyson was consistently at the top of the domestic vacuum cleaner market during major sales events like 618 and Double Eleven. However, with the advent of hard floor washers, Dyson's ranking in China dropped to around tenth place. The company’s R&D team is primarily based overseas, focusing on markets outside China. Consequently, they failed to capitalize on the domestic market's sudden surge for hard floor washers. During this period, Dyson attempted to launch a few products: one was a brush attachment compatible with their vacuum cleaner, and another was a standalone hard floor washer. These products were only announced but not officially launched in China. Industry insiders criticized these launches as copying ideas from small factories in Suzhou, significantly tarnishing Dyson's reputation.

## Kingclean

Kingclean has been building its brand for many years, positioning itself at the high-end level and achieving success with water purifiers and air purifiers. In this wave of hard floor washers, Kingclean did not miss out; they continued to adhere to their product direction and strategy. Compared to other companies, Kingclean's marketing efforts were relatively limited. Currently, while Kingclean remains in the market, some more aggressive brands have disappeared.

## Traditional OEM Giants

The traditional OEM giants are now multi-billionaires, so a few billion here or there does not significantly impact their business. Inspired by Ecovacs' successful transition from an OEM to a brand, these companies also wanted to establish their own brands. However, the challenge of transforming from an OEM to a brand is immense, primarily due to mismatches in talent and resources. These traditional OEM giants likely incurred losses during this wave of hard floor washers and are now rarely mentioned as potential new brands.

## Reflection on the Hard Floor Washer Industry

Apart from Tineco, most other brands have not made significant profits in the hard floor washer industry. Many friends who once saw it as a strategic opportunity are no longer active in the field. Only Dreame truly seized this strategic opportunity.

Regardless of future developments, these industry heroes deserve respect.
