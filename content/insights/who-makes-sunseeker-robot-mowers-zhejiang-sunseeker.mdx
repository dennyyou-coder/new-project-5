**Sunseeker robot mowers are made by a Chinese garden-equipment and robotics business whose product documents identify Zhejiang Sunseeker Industrial Co., Ltd. as a manufacturer.** The EU declaration for the Sunseeker S4 names the Zhejiang company as manufacturer and names Germany-based Sunseeker Intelligent Technology SE as the authorised representative.

The wider company map is more complicated. Shanghai Sunseeker Robotic Technology Co., Ltd. runs a dedicated robotic-mower website and product operation. SUNSEEKER US, Inc. appears in U.S. website terms, while the German company operates key European legal, support and promotional functions. The reviewed public official sources do not publish a complete parent-subsidiary ownership chart connecting every Sunseeker entity.

For distributors, importers and procurement teams, the useful answer is therefore document-specific: identify the manufacturer on the exact mower, then separately verify the trademark owner, invoice seller, importer, app operator and warranty route.

## Who Makes Sunseeker Robot Mowers?

The strongest model-specific answer comes from Sunseeker's own compliance documentation. The [S4 EU instruction manual and declaration](https://sunseekertech.com/global/docs/manual-s4-eu.pdf) identify **Zhejiang Sunseeker Industrial Co., Ltd.** as manufacturer for model RMR1001M20V. The declaration gives the company's Jinhua, Zhejiang address and states that the manufacturer assumes responsibility for the declaration.

The same document names **Sunseeker Intelligent Technology SE** in Hamburg as the authorised representative. This distinction matters: an EU authorised representative provides a regional regulatory contact, but it is not automatically the factory, the trademark owner or the parent company.

Sunseeker's [official Zhejiang company profile](https://sunseekerpower.com/introduce/) supports the broader manufacturing answer. It describes Zhejiang Sunseeker Industrial as a Chinese garden-equipment manufacturer engaged in R&D, manufacturing and sales of lithium garden tools, robotic lawn mowers and engine-powered garden tools. It also calls SUNSEEKER 山科 the company's own brand.

The safe conclusion is:

- **Verified S4 legal manufacturer:** Zhejiang Sunseeker Industrial Co., Ltd.
- **Broader commercial maker:** the Sunseeker garden-equipment and robotics business.
- **Physical factory for every current SKU:** not established by one declaration or company address.
- **Universal manufacturer for every past and future model:** must be rechecked model by model.

## Who Owns the Sunseeker Robot Mower Brand?

For mower-related trademark rights, current official evidence points to **Zhejiang Sunseeker Industrial Co., Ltd.**

The [Canadian Intellectual Property Office record](https://ised-isde.canada.ca/cipo/trademark-search/2217672) for SUNSEEKER registration TMA1284622 lists the Zhejiang company as registered owner. The goods include lawnmowers, robotic lawnmowers and robots for lawn mowing. The official Zhejiang company page separately describes SUNSEEKER 山科 as its own brand.

Trademark conclusions remain territorial and class-specific. The Canadian record does not by itself decide ownership of every SUNSEEKER word mark, logo, app, retail service or product class worldwide. The name is also used by unrelated yacht, hotel, travel, apparel and other businesses.

For commercial due diligence, search the destination registry for:

1. the exact SUNSEEKER or Sunseeker Elite word mark and logo;
2. the owner name and address;
3. mower, machinery, battery, software and retail-service classes;
4. assignments, licences, oppositions and renewal status;
5. the distributor's right to use the mark in stores, listings and advertising.

The reviewed public sources do not disclose an audited ultimate-shareholder chart for the private Sunseeker mower business. Trademark ownership answers who holds a brand right in a specific registry; it does not reveal every shareholder above the operating companies.

## What Is Zhejiang White Horse Technology's Relationship With Sunseeker?

Sunseeker's bilingual corporate website creates an important naming issue.

The English page identifies the business as **Zhejiang Sunseeker Industrial Co., Ltd.** The corresponding [Chinese company introduction](https://cn.sunseekerpower.com/?a=index&c=Lists&m=home&tid=73) identifies it as **浙江白马科技有限公司**, or Zhejiang White Horse Technology Co., Ltd. The two pages present substantially matching businesses, SUNSEEKER branding, production systems, company scale and contact channels.

That is strong evidence that the official website presents both names as part of the same corporate operation. It is not enough to determine whether:

- one name is a current legal entity and the other a former name;
- the English name is a trade translation rather than the registered translation;
- the companies are related operating entities; or
- assets, trademarks and contracts are divided between them.

A buyer should not solve this with a website assumption. Request the current Chinese business licence, unified social credit code, English-name declaration, bank-account evidence and an explanation of which company owns the factory and signs the supply contract.

## What Does Shanghai Sunseeker Robotic Technology Do?

The [Shanghai Sunseeker Robotic Technology website](https://www.sk-robot.com/) identifies the company in Chinese as 上海山科机器人有限公司. It publishes RMA-series robotic-mower products, instructions, app guidance and product-security policies.

Sunseeker's global [brand history](https://sunseekertech.com/global/about-us) says “Sunseeker Robotics” was founded in Shanghai in 2009, a robotic-mower R&D centre was established in 2017 and first-generation robotic mowers launched in 2019. This supports Shanghai's importance in the robotics story.

It does not, by itself, prove the current equity chain. A brand timeline is not a company-registry extract, and the phrase “Sunseeker Robotics” should not automatically be treated as the exact legal name of one company throughout the entire period.

The official Zhejiang [contact page](https://sunseekerpower.com/contact/) also lists a Shanghai Sunseeker Garden Tools Co., Ltd. at a Shanghai address. Multiple Shanghai names therefore appear across official Sunseeker properties. Until a current group chart or registry package connects them, the accurate formulation is functional:

> Shanghai Sunseeker entities are part of the public Sunseeker garden-equipment and mower operating picture, but their precise ownership and division of responsibility require company-level documents.

## Where Are Sunseeker Robot Mowers Made?

The reviewed official product documents support **China** as the origin of the models examined, while leaving the exact factory for each batch open.

The S4 declaration names a manufacturer in Jinhua, China. Official EU manuals for the [V3](https://sunseekertech.com/global/docs/manual-v3-eu.pdf) and [S5](https://sunseekertech.com/global/docs/manual-s5-eu.pdf) show product-label examples marked “Made in PRC.” The Zhejiang company also presents manufacturing workshops, assembly lines, warehouse systems and product testing on its official site.

This evidence does not justify saying that every component or every future Sunseeker mower comes from one Jinhua plant. A legal-manufacturer address can be different from the physical assembly site, and a group may use additional factories or contract manufacturers.

For the exact shipment, request:

1. the manufacturer and origin on the unit and carton;
2. the declaration matching the exact model and radio configuration;
3. the factory name, address and audit report;
4. serial-number and production-batch traceability;
5. customs origin and bill-of-materials support;
6. battery, charger, radio and safety documentation;
7. written disclosure of any alternate or overseas production site.

Do not rely on an office address, warehouse address or unsupported industry rumour as proof of factory origin.

## Are Sunseeker and Sunseeker Elite Different Companies?

Official product materials use both names as brand architecture, not as proof of two independent manufacturers.

The S4 EU declaration lists the brand name as “SUNSEEKER, Sunseeker Elite.” Current websites separate mass-market S and V products from Sunseeker Elite X-series and commercial products. This looks like a product and channel segmentation strategy.

For procurement, the branding still needs model-level checking. Confirm whether a quotation covers Sunseeker, Sunseeker Tech or Sunseeker Elite; which regional company authorizes the dealer; which app and warranty portal support the SKU; and whether batteries, blades, charging stations and diagnostic tools are interchangeable.

Different logos or product tiers do not automatically mean different owners. Likewise, a shared website or app does not prove that every product has the same seller and warranty terms.

## Why the Company Map Matters to Buyers and Dealers

Robot mowers combine mechanical equipment, batteries, radio modules, navigation software, mapping data, cloud accounts and seasonal service. The supplier relationship continues long after a container arrives.

A buyer may receive a technically compliant mower but still face commercial risk if:

- the local seller is not authorized for the marketplace;
- the importer cannot provide the declaration or technical file;
- the app region does not support the destination country;
- replacement boards or wheel motors are not stocked locally;
- warranty freight is not allocated in the contract;
- a new legal entity cannot honor the old entity's obligations;
- the trademark licence does not cover the distributor's advertising.

## Sunseeker Distributor and Procurement Checklist

Before approving a Sunseeker mower, dealership or OEM-related transaction, verify:

1. **Exact counterparty:** Which legal company issues the quotation, invoice and credit note?
2. **Chinese identity:** Does the business licence match the Chinese name, English name, registration number and bank account?
3. **Brand rights:** Who owns the relevant SUNSEEKER or Sunseeker Elite mark in the destination and product class?
4. **Authorization:** Is the seller authorized for the country, channel, marketplace and exact model?
5. **Exact SKU:** Do model, part number and radio configuration match the label, manual, declaration and quotation?
6. **Legal manufacturer:** Which company is named on the product and current declaration?
7. **Factory and origin:** Which site assembled the batch, and what origin will customs receive?
8. **Regulatory holder:** Who controls the CE, UKCA, FCC, RED, battery and safety files?
9. **Importer and GPSR role:** Who carries local customs, recall and regulatory responsibility?
10. **Software region:** Will the app, maps, 4G, account and firmware work in the destination?
11. **Warranty route:** Does the claim go to the dealer, distributor, regional Sunseeker company or service centre?
12. **Parts and tools:** Which batteries, motors, sensors, boards, blades and diagnostic tools are stocked locally?
13. **Field obligations:** Who pays for failed units, transport, labour, recalls and obsolete seasonal inventory?
14. **Change control:** What happens if the factory, importer, app operator or legal manufacturer changes?
