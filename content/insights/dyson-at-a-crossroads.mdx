In 2020, it was the most challenging year I can remember for Dyson. We had to change all our plans and fundamentally reshape our business from the outset. Sir James Dyson, the former Britishrichest man, expressed these sentiments.

In October 2019, Dyson officially announced the cessation of its N526 electric vehicle project. The project was formally initiated in 2017 and had already cost Dyson £500 million (approximately RMB 4.2 billion) by 2019. This SUV-style car featured two 200kW motors, with a rated power of 536 British horsepower and torque of 480 pound-feet. It was designed to travel up to 600 miles on a single charge, accelerating from zero to 62 mph in just 4.8 seconds, with a top speed of 125 mph. Originally scheduled for release in 2021, Dyson officially halted the project just before its intended market launch.

![Original image 1](/images/insights/dyson-at-a-crossroads-image-001.jpg)

The reason is simple: according to calculations, this car would need to be sold for at least 150,000 pounds, and no one can sell cars at that price point.

Dyson invested significant manpower and resources into this project, recruiting many talents from the automotive industry. They acquired a solid-state battery company and even developed high-performance motors on their own, but they still underestimated the complexity and difficulty of the automotive industry.

![Original image 2](/images/insights/dyson-at-a-crossroads-image-002.jpg)

For Dyson, the cessation of its automotive project is not merely a loss ofupfront investment; it also faces a significant blow to team morale. Unfortunately, the challenges faced by the automotive project are not Dyson's onlychallenge. In 2020, the global outbreak of COVID-19 plunged the world into panic, and except for China, supply chains were disrupted. Unlike its competitors, all of Dyson's manufacturing bases are located in Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Singapore), which proved particularly vulnerable during the pandemic.

However, Dyson's competitors did not pause to wait for it. In 2020, most Chinese cleaning appliance factories operated at full capacity. Dyson's major competitors saw a growth of 50-80% that year.

For instance, in the North American market, the leader Shark reported a 65% overall increase, with its cleaning appliances growing by 40.7%.

![Original image 3](/images/insights/dyson-at-a-crossroads-image-003.jpg)

![Original image 4](/images/insights/dyson-at-a-crossroads-image-004.jpg)

In such a comparison, Dyson's performance appears particularly dismal. As the pandemic continues, Dyson has even changed its long-standing practice of not producing in China, moving some products to be manufactured within China by Foxconn.

![Original image 5](/images/insights/dyson-at-a-crossroads-image-005.jpg)

The COVID-19 pandemic highlighted Dyson's shortcomings in its supply chain. However, the weaknesses had already begun to show several years ago.


Dyson Digital Motor (DDM), Dyson's digital motor, has been at the core of recent disruptions in vacuum cleaner and personal care industries. It is also the fundamental reason why vacuum cleaners could transition to lithium-ion batteries earlier. Dyson has consistently pushed the boundaries of motor performance, from 80,000 RPM to 100,000 RPM, 120,000 RPM, and then to 150,000 RPM.


**Theoretically, Dyson's digital motor could reach much higher speeds. We estimate that if Dyson were to push the limits, the motor could achieve over 200,000 RPM.**


However, a product is an integrated system; even if the motor spins faster, other components must keep up. Currently, two main factors limit Dyson's full motor output:

1. Battery: The faster the motor runs, the more battery power it consumes. In Dyson's own vacuum cleaners, the Max setting, which corresponds to the motor running at full speed, only provides a maximum of 8 minutes of usage time. For normal use, switching to the standard setting can extend the runtime to about 30 minutes. No matter how strong Dyson's technological prowess is, if it runs at full power, the battery will only support up to 8 minutes. An 8-minute runtime is not acceptable to consumers. Therefore, Dyson is forced to compete with its rivals on a level playing field in terms of motor performance.

2. Suction: Theoretically, the higher the suction, the cleaner the cleaning. However, once it reaches around 200W (equivalent to about 80,000 RPM for brushless motors), the performance is sufficient. Dyson's stronger motor performance comes at a higher cost; reportedly, a single brushless motor in Dyson products costs nearly 200 yuan, which is almost half the price of an ordinary domestic wireless vacuum cleaner.


Due to various factors, Dyson's advantages in motor technology cannot be fully utilized, leading to its inability to significantly outperform competitors. As a result, Dyson's claimed cutting-edge technologies appear somewhat awkward.

Several innovations on Dyson's recent products have consistently indicated that the company has partially lost its ability to lead the industry.


>Dyson V11's high-torque carpet cleaning brush head was heavily marketed with smart features when it was first released. It could automatically adjust the speed of the roller based on the type of floor and carpet material. First, switching between different brushes during use is a cumbersome operation. Another question arises: why switch speeds based on material properties? A more reasonable approach would be to adjust the motor's performance based on the dirtiness of the surface, which would make it smarter and potentially extend battery life.

![Original image 6](/images/insights/dyson-at-a-crossroads-image-006.jpg)

Dyson V12 Laser Detection Technology: A laser beam is emitted from the brush bar, allowing one to see tiny dust particles. This innovation seems utterly pointless with no practical use, and the laser could potentially harm some pets.

![Original image 7](/images/insights/dyson-at-a-crossroads-image-007.jpg)

Dyson V15, hair-clogging floor brush. The idea and implementation are good; however, there is one point that stands out: every time I see hair on the ground, I need to detach the main floor brush and attach this floor brush. Isn't this a bit inconvenient?

![Original image 8](/images/insights/dyson-at-a-crossroads-image-008.jpg)

All of these products were heavily promoted as part of each generation's flagship models, which brought joy to Dyson as no one in the cleaning appliance industry had followed or mimicked this technology. However, competitors continuously launched cheaper and better alternatives.

More sorrowful for Dyson was not the loss of innovation direction but the fact that their previously solid market position was replaced overnight by others.

In 2020, robotic floor cleaners, leveraging a super-hit product during the pandemic in the Chinese market, enabled the Tineco brand to become a superleading company in the cleaning appliance industry within just one year, transforming from an unknown company into a major player.

![Original image 9](/images/insights/dyson-at-a-crossroads-image-009.jpg)

Vacuum cleaners were originally a niche category within cleaning appliances and remained lukewarm until before the pandemic. With the outbreak of the pandemic, China's unique habit of mopping floors led to a significant surge in vacuum cleaner sales.


Dyson was particularly disappointed by this development, as vacuum cleaners fall into the highest price segment for cleaning appliances—3000-5000 RMB—which had been exclusively Dyson’s market. Consequently, we can see that vacuum cleaners are continuously encroaching on Dyson's market share. The core of a vacuum cleaner does not rely on high-speed motors, and most of the supply chain is in China, making it difficult for Dyson to quickly develop similar products during the pandemic. Whether or not Dyson has developed such products remains to be seen, but even if they did, by the time they hit the market, they would likely just be categorized as "others" in the industry.


Apart from the unexpected surge of vacuum cleaners impacting Dyson, it also faces strong competition in the vacuum cleaner market.


In the North American market, Shark has already become the leading brand in cleaning appliances. In Dyson’s home base of the UK, after several years of development, Shark has taken the top spot with a 36% market share. Given that the UK is Dyson's stronghold, it was quite surprising for an outsider like Shark to enter and dominate so quickly.


In the European market, Xiaomi-ecosystem companies led by Dreame have become leaders in multiple online markets.


It can be said that Dyson faces unprecedented competition in the cleaning appliance industry.


However, this is far from the only challenge Dyson faces.


Currently, Dyson's products are divided into four categories: vacuum cleaners, personal care devices (hairdryers, curling irons), environmental appliances (fans, air purifiers), and lighting.


Lighting is a category that was pioneered by James Dyson’s son Jake Dyson and later incorporated into the Dyson company. From a product highlight and sales perspective, it is essentially dispensable.


The proportion of environmental appliances is also relatively small. Currently, the main two business areas are cleaning appliances and personal care (hairdryers, curling irons).

![Original image 10](/images/insights/dyson-at-a-crossroads-image-010.jpg)

Unlike the fierce competition faced by cleaning appliances, Dyson currently faces relatively weaker competitors in personal care. But this is only temporary.


Dreame, a Xiaomi-ecosystem company and one of the fastest-growing companies in the cleaning appliance industry in recent years, raised RMB 3.6 billion in financing in 2021 and has an unlisted valuation of RMB 17 billion. Companies led by Dreame have already launched intense attacks on Dyson's hairdryer products and invalidated some of Dyson's patents for hairdryers.


>Recently, the National Intellectual Property Administration declared that Dyson'sinvention patent right (Patent No. CN201410321250.5, titled "Hand-held Appliance") related to hairdryers is entirely invalid. This patent application was made in 2014, predating Dyson's release of the hairdryer in 2016 and thus belongs to an earlier patent. Although this is just one of many patents Dyson holds for hairdryers, it is a key patent used by Dyson in its current legal battles.


Additionally, Dyson's old rival Shark has also launched a range of personal care products, which reportedly are performing well in sales.

![Original image 11](/images/insights/dyson-at-a-crossroads-image-011.jpg)

Roborock has consistently sought significant development in personal care after amassing substantial resources and funds through its hard floor washers. Mr. Qian Dongqi, the boss, is one of the best product managers in the industry. Having successfully created the KEEWOOrobot vacuum and Roborockhard floor washer as two super brands, Mr. Qian is also looking for the next move in smart home appliances. Can he once again create a disruptive product like the wash floor machine in personal care products? The industry holds great expectations for this.

Now, these companies still cannot challenge Dyson's market share in personal care. The question is: How long can Dyson's leading advantage last?

Dyson stands at a crossroads.
