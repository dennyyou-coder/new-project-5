Will Dyson Enter the Hard Floor Washer Market? This Is the Greatest Unknown in China's Cleaning Appliances Industry and Could Be the Final Piece to Shake Up the Industry Landscape.


If Dyson were to enter the hard floor washer market, it could potentially change the positions of the top players in the industry under its non-limited marketing efforts.


China is currently Dyson’s largest market. If Dyson believes that there is no significant demand for hard floor washers, then one can only say "nothing to see here." It would be incorrect to assume that Dyson does not pay attention to other brands' moves; such an assumption shows a lack of understanding of Dyson's strategy. Previously, I had a friend tell me that when LG or Samsung introduced a vacuum cleaner with better motor performance than Dyson’s, the company in Malaysia insisted on surpassing Dyson in specifications and parameters.


If Dyson were to enter this market, let us discuss from a technical perspective what Dyson’s hard floor washer might look like and its features. We welcome any future comparisons that could "put words to paper" regarding these expectations.


Overall Design:

The overall design would likely be similar to a standing vacuum cleaner, with the basic framework possiblyborrow from the previous Dyson Ball series。

![Original image 1](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-001.jpg)

There are a few classic elements that are bound to be retained.


1. The multi-whirl cyclone design, which is Dyson's classic technology and has not been abandoned even in robot vacuums. This design will certainly not be omitted from hard floor washers either.

![Original image 2](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-002.jpg)

2. Dyson Ball Design: Before the release of wireless handheld vacuums, Dyson heavily promoted its Ball series in both upright and stick models, integrating a significant amount of advanced technology. It is foreseeable that this design will continue to be used.

![Original image 3](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-003.jpg)

2. Brushless Motor for Suction Force

Limited by battery life, Dyson's main suction motor is unlikely to be oversized and should be slightly larger than those of other brands, estimated to fall within the range of 300W-350W.

![Original image 4](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-004.jpg)

3. Direct Drive Floor Brush Motor

The use of direct drive floor brush motors in vacuum cleaners is most prominently featured by Dyson's cylinder models. Previously, due to concerns over Dyson's patents, many floor brush manufacturers were hesitant to adopt the direct drive motor solution. In the era of hard floor washers, several industry leaders have adopted direct drive solutions, with others following suit. The actual patent risk remains unclear.

Regardless, in terms of direct drive motors, Dyson is likely to continue leading the industry and has the capability to address issues such as motor overheating and waterproofing.

![Original image 5](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-005.jpg)

4. Displays and Sensors

After several iterations by Dyson, the company now uses precise piezoelectric sensors on its vacuum cleaners to count the amount of dust collected, which is displayed on the screen. This feature represents a significant advancement for certain leading brands in the industry.

![Original image 6](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-006.jpg)

5. Battery Cells

The battery cells are likely to still be from Moli and Sony Murata. There is a high probability that 10-12 cells of the 21700 type will be used, which should enable the vacuum cleaner's suction motor to have a higher power output than its competitors.

6. Brush Roll

Single brush roll designs are most likely to be adopted, as there has been no significant improvement in cleaning efficiency with dual brush rolls. The only advantage of using a double brush roll is that it requires less force when pulled backward. Therefore, Dyson is expected to use single-brush roll machines. Additionally, the previous velvety material brush roll had issues such as odor development and poor dirt recovery. I believe this area will be where Dyson's main innovation lies.

![Original image 7](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-007.jpg)

Personal estimates suggest the use of some composite innovative materials that do not emit odors and where stains are less likely to adhere, making them easier to dry. If this level is achieved, it would essentially mean overtaking competitors in the industry.

7. Brush Motor with Driving Force

Tineco 3.0 features a brush motor for easy front-to-back operation, which also reduces effort during actual use. This function is expected to become a standard feature in high-end machines. Dyson's floor washer is likely to further enhance this functionality by predicting the forward and backward movements ahead of time, making these actions smoother.

![Original image 8](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-008.jpg)

8. Water Tank Capacity

The clear water tank and dirty water tank are each designed for 850 mL, which should be sufficient. If they were any larger, the machine would become unbalanced and particularly unattractive. There is also no special necessity for them to be any bigger.

![Original image 9](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-009.jpg)

9. Flexibility in Maneuverability

Currently, most floor washers lack high flexibility and are not capable of performing a full 360-degree turn. It is estimated that Dyson will address this aspect.

![Original image 10](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-010.jpg)

10. Water Level Misreporting

Currently, most of the sewage sensing systems used in the industry frequently misreport water levels. The core issue is still the lack of high-precision sensors. Dyson should employ more advanced high-precision sensors to eliminate water level misreporting.

![Original image 11](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-011.jpg)

11. Water Pumps

Current water pumps do not yet offer very precise flow control. I believe Dyson would require its suppliers to develop a new, high-precision pump and sensor system to achieve more accurate water management. This could allow for flow adjustments based on the mop's condition, floor dirtiness, and the amount of standing water.

The benefit of this approach is that it can reduce water usage when the mop is already wet, significantly decreasing surface water marks.

12. Mop Edges

Dual edges on the mops should become a standard feature.

![Original image 12](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-012.jpg)

13. Hair Tangle Prevention

Hair tangle prevention has long been a challenge in the cleaning appliance industry. I estimate that there should be some improvements in this area; at minimum, it should not fall behind other competing products.

![Original image 13](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-013.jpg)

14. Electrolyzed Water/Antibacterial Solution

Currently, the industry widely uses electrolyzed water and antibacterial solutions, but due to their limited practical effects, Dyson is unlikely to adopt this approach. This could potentially be a focal point for significant advertising campaigns by Dyson to attack its competitors.

![Original image 14](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-014.jpg)

15. Drying Solutions.

If Dyson has incorporated some new materials that make it easier to dry and less prone to odor, they might opt for a spin-dry and air-dry method rather than using a drying solution. I believe it is highly likely that they will adopt the Dyson Hand Dryer Airblade along with special brush materials to quickly dry the surface.

![Original image 15](/images/insights/dyson-hard-floor-washer-exposure-image-015.jpg)
