In recent years, billions of dollars have flooded into the cleaning appliance market, with a key catalyst being the success of Tineco's hard floor washers.

Tineco is a subsidiary brand under the Ecovacs Group, focusing on smart home appliances. At that time, Ecovacs' robot vacuums had achieved great success. The Tineco brand initially started with vacuum cleaners; I remember in 2019 when friends from Tineco invited me for tea, they were quite troubled and asked how to break through the current situation. I said that if their vacuum cleaners cost over 2,000 yuan, people would definitely buy Dyson.

However, by 2020, Tineco broke through with its hard floor washer, achieving several billion in sales within just a year, and quickly reaching tens of billions in the following two years. This caused Ecovacs' stock price to rise from several dozen yuan to over 200 yuan in less than a year.

Tineco's success spurred everyone, initiating an era of rapid growth for cleaning appliances. At that time, everyone told me, "Denny, cleaning appliances are our strategic opportunity; we must seize this chance at all costs."

The hard floor washer was originally a hit product by the American giant Bissell, selling hundreds of thousands in just 2018-2019 and generating one billion US dollars in sales. This also helped Bissell enter the ten-billion-dollar club. At that time, it was the Chinese team at Bissell that developed this product; its main market was overseas with prices ranging from $400 to $500. I wrote about this product back in 2019 and lamented that it wasn't sold domestically. But I never imagined that by 2020, this product would become a phenomenon.

Let's review the situation of domestic hard floor washer wars from 2020 to 2023:

--Leading Brands: Tineco (Ecovacs)

--Foreign Giants: Bissell, Shark, Dyson, Philips, Karcher

--Xiaomi Background: Xiaomi, Smartmi, SWDK, Shunzao, Roidmi, Viomi, Deerma

--Traditional Cleaning Appliance Manufacturers: Miduo (Aiper), Wanmou (Pufa), Kingclean (Sirius)

--Traditional Home Appliances Giants: Midea (Youruijia), Haier, Supor, Joyoung, Gree

--Robot Vacuum Giants: Roborock, Narwal, Yinxing Intelligent

--New Brands: BoBoT, Uwant, DripWater, MiBo (Fotai subsidiary), Zhaoguang (mobile industry), Narwal, Anker's Eufy

The number of giants involved in this market is unprecedented; some companies have invested hundreds of millions, while the least has started with several million. Over these years, total R&D and marketing investments in hard floor washers likely exceed billions.

All of these entities are brand participants, with numerous contract manufacturers producing hard floor washer molds. Vacuum cleaner factories are located in Suzhou, while robot vacuum factories can be found in Shenzhen (such as Sanshuan and LeSheng). Various other industries are also vying for a share of the market.

When Tineco became a hit in 2020, everyone was looking for suitable contract manufacturers. However, this product belonged to a niche category at that time; only Bissell and some people from Tineco had actually made hard floor washers. Other companies did not have the right talent. The entire industry flocked towards these two companies to find talent. At that time, any engineer who had worked on hard floor washers was offered salaries several times higher than before.

At the time, designing a vacuum cleaner involved around 100,000 RMB for structural and ID design, whereas a hard floor washer cost between 5-8 million RMB. Even early on, there were people selling hard floor washer blueprints for 5 million RMB. At that time, Bissell's team was in Shenzhen, while Tineco's team was in Suzhou; thus, most companies in Guangdong followed the Bissell style design, whereas those in Jiangsu and Zhejiang regions followed the Tineco style.

In 2020, only two brands were available on the market: Tineco and Bissell. Tineco led by a wide margin, with Bissell ranking second in the industry. At that time, Bissell's executives personally came to Shanghai, investing substantial funds to prepare for market entry, even considering raising capital to fight this battle.

Bissell's style was heavily influenced by American aesthetics, with too much muscle and visible design elements. The brush roll was designed to fit carpets and floors but did not adhere well to the ground, lacking self-propulsion. Due to its elevated position from the ground, it struggled to remove stubborn stains effectively, making it unsuitable for the Chinese market. Bissell managed to rank second in the industry because other brands had yet to launch their products, briefly occupying a favorable position during this product vacuum period. However, Bissell's iterations in the Chinese market were slow; once other brands launched their products, its market share dropped significantly.

Between 2020 and 2021, any company that could get a hard floor washer to produce under brand name would make substantial profits. At that time, Shenzhen-based SWSM had hard floor washers, and the entire industry approached them; rumors suggest they made over 100 million RMB in those two years.

Another company from Xiaomi's ecosystem, SWDK, also developed a hard floor washer early on but remained largely unknown, despite having invested heavily. It was as if they woke up too late to seize the opportunity.

Tineco's hard floor washers were highly sought after; sometimes, you had to wait 1-2 months for them on JD.com and needed to make reservations. Dealers of Tineco products made significant profits during this period. Consequently, all brands rushed to enter the market.

In 2021, Dreame was the fastest mover, launching the H11 hard floor washer, followed by new product releases every 2-3 months, constantly updating their offerings. At that time, Dreame had just raised RMB 3.6 billion and held substantial funds. They also keenly identified opportunities on Douyin, pouring all resources into it, becoming the top seller of hard floor washers on Douyin within a few months and second in the industry overall. In recent years, the market for hard floor washers was essentially a competition between Tineco and Dreame. I have detailed how Dreame surpassed Tineco on the Douyin channel in this video.

By 2022, most brands had their own developed products, entering a period of intense competition.

In early 2022, a brand called Zuiguang announced a dual-brush hard floor washer with an aluminum casing and dual brushes, manufactured by Luxshare Precision. Zuiguang was said to have a background in the mobile phone industry and raised significant funds. My friends and I watched their online launch live stream; we were concerned about this formidable competitor. However, Zuiguang's product was a typical example of a failed launch. Due to its immature basic functions, the better the promotion, the faster it would fail. The product faced massive returns upon release, with reported return rates nearing 90%. After the launch, the company almost ceased operations.

At that time, Midea had just acquired the Eureka brand and decided to introduce it into the Chinese market as a premium brand. They heavily invested in Yi Yangqianxi as their spokesperson. At that time, both Midea and Eureka operated concurrently.

In the early days, many companies were looking for brand ambassadors; Roborock chose Xiao Zhan as their spokesperson, while Dreame also signed Su Bingtian. The initial progress in the high-end market was not smooth until the launch of the GX5 at the 1500 price segment, which helped them stabilize their position in the hard floor washer market.

Roborock's first released product was a dual-brush hard floor washer. Dual brushes were seen as a breakthrough point against Dyson's machines by many companies. To be honest, Roborock did quite well with their hard floor washer. At that time, one feature was widely criticized; to achieve more flexible turns, the machine could not stand upright and had an additional support added underneath the body. This was then referred to as a cane. During this period, Roborock's robot vacuums were thriving, but hard floor washers did not receive much attention. This year, Roborock has invested heavily in hard floor washers; the A30 cordless steam cleaner is reportedly selling well during the 618 promotion.

Narwal released its J1 robot vacuum after which the company's product direction became somewhat disorganized as they tried many different directions. The first hard floor washer had some merits in terms of design and craftsmanship, but it was launched too late to compete with the giants in that market segment. The company faced numerous challenges during this period. Over the years, Narwal has begun to focus on its product direction; both their robot vacuums and hard floor washers have many positive aspects, and they are now experiencing a virtuous cycle.

Haier placed great importance on hard floor washers and upgraded a small business unit into a separate department. To enhance their R&D capabilities, Haier invested RMB 1 billion in Shanchuan Robotics in Shenzhen. The first dual-brush hard floor washer from Haier was manufactured by Shanchuan. At that time, Shanchuan placed great emphasis on the development of hard floor washers and established an office in Suzhou to recruit many talented individuals. Haier has been very successful on the Douyin channel; they were likely one of the first companies to conduct live store broadcasts on this platform. Relying on stable sales through the Douyin channel, Haier's market share was often among the top three or four.

Xiaomi always placed great importance on cleaning appliances. Some industry friends were concerned that if Xiaomi entered with high-value-for-money products, it would be difficult for others to compete. However, at that time, Xiaomi was focusing on becoming a premium brand and did not choose to enter the market based on price-to-performance ratio. Xiaomi produced what is considered the most complex hard floor washer in the industry; it featured a hot water station and high-temperature hot water cleaning, with a relatively high price of over 3000 yuan. Due to the complexity of the product and the short development time, various issues arose after its launch, leading to average sales performance.

We discussed this with several friends and concluded that if Xiaomi had launched a machine priced around 2000 yuan with similar features as the Dyson model in the first year, selling one million units would not have been difficult. This would have made it hard for other companies to compete at the 2000 price point. However, by this time, it was too late to enter the market.

Shunzao was also a star player in Xiaomi's ecosystem; the success of Roborock and Dreame raised expectations about Shunzao's development. The first high-temperature hard floor washer from Xiaomi was manufactured by Shunzao. This product did not perform particularly well, but their second model featuring tracks and lying down sales were very successful, leading the market to adopt this feature as a standard.

Zhejiang Supor entered the cleaning appliance market in 2019, and some of its early leaders were somewhat familiar to me. They viewed the market for wet vacuums as a strategic opportunity but invested fewer resources compared to Midea and Haier, though they did invest significantly. Supor's approach to the wet vacuum project was generally conventional. A friend who had left Supor recently told me that it was regrettable they missed this strategic opportunity; I responded that fortunately they didn't continue with it, or their losses would have been substantial.

Compared to Supor’s significant investments in cleaning appliances, Ninebot's approach in the domestic market for cleaning appliances has been relatively conservative. They did not aggressively invest in marketing and promotion. Similarly, Shark's brand presence in China was also lukewarm. A basic trend over these years is that investing money does not necessarily lead to sales, while not investing means no sales.

Anker’s Eufy Mach. Anker placed great importance on the cleaning appliance project, hiring many people from Suzhou but without much success. Yang Meng later reflected on this project. Besides using the Eufy brand for traditional vacuum cleaners, they also established a separate Eufy Mach brand. Their first product in the hard floor washer category was a cordless steam mop. I analyzed that such a cordless steam mop would be difficult to succeed. After selling only a small number of units, Eufy Mach stopped operations domestically. It is said that Anker lost several hundred million yuan on this project.

Deerma. Deerma has always been very focused on cleaning appliances and has collaborated with many companies in Suzhou. Known for its aesthetically pleasing and cost-effective small appliances, a friend told me that Deerma's pricing was challenging to achieve. In the wave of wet vacuums, Deerma acted decisively. They partnered with local manufacturers in Suzhou to get existing machines while simultaneously developing their own products. Deerma’s positioning was clear; as major players were competing for the 2000 yuan and above price segment, Deerma stayed at the 1000 yuan level. At that time, there were no big brands in this price range; only some white label products existed. Their strength could not be compared to Deerma’s. At that point, Deerma likely had a good sales volume.

BoBot. BoBot initially sold dual-disc electric mops domestically at prices of 2000 yuan when others were selling for 3-500 yuan, and they achieved nearly two billion in annual sales. In 2019, before the wet vacuum market exploded, they saw potential in this segment and recommended development-capable manufacturers to me. Unfortunately, they faced challenges from major players, resulting in a delay of several months in product launch. Later, due to other reasons, BoBot was eliminated in this wave of wet vacuum competition.

Miborg. FOTILE, the leader in exhaust hoods, also saw an opportunity in cleaning appliances. They capitalized on the difficulty of cleaning roller brushes and their tendency to smell and leave water marks on floors by launching a roll-free hard floor washer. The roller brush part was entirely made of plastic and silicone, allowing it to be washed directly with water. Initially, there were many inquiries about this product, but its significant drawback was that it was very heavy and inconvenient to push. Only after the second-generation model added an assist motor did this issue improve slightly. However, hard floor washer technology evolved too quickly for a startup like Miborg to keep up. After losing several hundred million yuan, FOTILE Group likely stopped investing in hard floor washers.

Hizero/Drop Water. Hizero and Drop Water are companies that were early players in the hard floor washer market; however, their products do not feature suction motors but instead use a sponge roller to collect waste by deformation. The advantage of this roller is its excellent water absorption, leaving the ground very dry afterward. Since there is no suction motor, it also produces low noise and has a relatively lower cost. Personally, I think such a product can be considered for a low-cost hard floor washer. Unfortunately, at that time point in 2022, all players in the industry chose traditional vacuum motors, making it virtually impossible to go against the established direction.

Uwant (Uwant). Uwant is an extremely capable company that has brought several hit products to market, such as dual-bucket dehumidifiers and fabric cleaning machines. Their first hard floor washer featured dual brushes with automatic hair cutting; while innovative in some aspects, it did have various minor issues. Despite these challenges, Uwant's resilience is evident; after years of intense competition in the domestic cleaning appliance market, they are still thriving. Currently, their annual sales of hard floor washers reach hundreds of thousands.

Dyson. Before the rise of hard floor washers, Dyson was consistently at the top of the domestic vacuum cleaner market during 618 and Double Eleven events, easily outperforming its competitors. However, in the era of hard floor washers, Dyson's ranking in China dropped to outside the top ten. Due to their R&D team being primarily based overseas and products mainly targeting international markets, they failed to capitalize on the sudden surge in demand for hard floor washers in China. During this period, Dyson attempted to release a few floor cleaning products, including a floor brush that matched their vacuum cleaner and a standalone hard floor washer. These products were only announced but did not seem to have been officially launched in China. Industry insiders criticized these products as copying the creative ideas of small factories in Suzhou, which ultimately led Dyson's reputation to decline.

Kingclean. Kingclean has long focused on building its brand and positioning it at a high-end level, achieving great success in water purifiers and air purifiers. In this wave of hard floor washers, Kingclean did not miss out; they continued to adhere to their product direction and strategy. Compared to other companies, their marketing efforts were somewhat reduced. Looking back now, while Kingclean still exists, some more aggressive brands have disappeared.

Traditional ODM Giants. The traditional ODM giants are already worth hundreds of billions, so a few billion in business is not a significant issue for them. Seeing the success of EcoVacs Group's transition from an ODM to a brand, they also aspire to create their own brand. However, transitioning from an ODM to a brand is extremely challenging due to mismatches in talent and resources. These ODM giants likely incurred losses during this wave of hard floor washer brands and are now rarely heard discussing the creation of their own brands.

Looking back at the hard floor washer industry from this perspective, aside from Trifo, most other brands did not make a profit. Many friends who told me that hard floor washers were strategic opportunities have since left the industry; only Dreame truly seized this strategic opportunity.

No matter what the future holds, these heroes in the industry are worthy of respect.
