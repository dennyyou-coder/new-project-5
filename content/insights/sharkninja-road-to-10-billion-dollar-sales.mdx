## SharkNinja: A Company That Continuously Pushes Boundaries

As one of the companies I deeply admire and appreciate in the small appliances industry, I am particularly impressed by Shark's relentless pursuit of self-improvement.

My first interaction with SharkNinja was around 2012 when their company name was still Europro. Later, due to the tremendous success of the brand, they changed their company name to match their brand identity.

In 2018, the entire brand was acquired by Ninebot for $1 billion, a piece of news I broke in my industry reports, which made me an overnight sensation. If you are interested, you can watch the video I did on this topic.

### SharkNinja's Growth and Success

SN went public on NASDAQ in 2022 with sales reaching $3.7 billion. By 2024, that number had grown to $5.5 billion, a staggering increase.

Now let’s look at where SharkNinja has seen its growth and how they achieved it:

Firstly, some basic facts: In 2024, SN generated $5.5 billion in sales, with Shark contributing $2.6 billion and Ninja adding $2.9 billion. Comparing the data from 2022, Shark’s sales were around $2 billion while Ninja was at $1.7 billion. The main growth driver has been Ninja, which saw a significant increase of $1.2 billion.

In terms of market expansion, SN has aggressively pursued international markets, with international sales contributing $1.7 billion. In the UK and Japan, Shark holds a very high market share.

Their global supply chain is also robust. They began setting up global supply chains early on during trade wars, with production bases in Southeast Asian countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, Singapore, and Malaysia. I have seen SN’s supply chain team, which is one of the strongest. Once they set their goals, they are willing to do whatever it takes to achieve them. Typically, a company might invest in 3-5 prototypes when developing new products; however, SharkNinja would often produce over 100 prototypes and ship them to the U.S. for consumer feedback, which is an enormous investment.

While SN’s growth in cleaning appliances has not been as significant, there are still opportunities that have yet to be fully exploited. One of these is hard floor washers, where SharkNinja currently has a small presence. Another area with potential is all-in-one docks for robotic vacuums. I believe another major growth opportunity lies in SharkNinja’s corded upright vacuum cleaners once they transition to lithium-ion batteries, which could lead to substantial growth.

### Ninja's Diversification

Ninja has seen significant diversification into new categories such as coffee machines, outdoor appliances, and personal care devices like hair dryers and facial masks. For instance, their hairdryer sales in just one year reached several million units, a remarkable achievement. ### Key Factors Behind SN's Success

SharkNinja attributes its success to four main points:

1. **Disruptive Innovation**: With over 1,000 engineers and designers globally, they continuously innovate. Teams in the U.S., UK, and China work around the clock, ensuring that new ideas are tested and implemented quickly. They hold more than 5,200 patents worldwide. Currently, Dyson is not as innovative as SharkNinja; all eyes are on their latest products.

2. **Global Agile Supply Chain**: SN has a well-established global supply chain with suppliers in China, Thailand, Vietnam, Singapore, and Malaysia. Core products have multiple suppliers to ensure flexibility and responsiveness to market changes. For example, they can switch between these countries to maintain competitive tariff levels. Having multiple suppliers also allows them to continuously negotiate better prices.

3. **360-Degree Marketing**: SN’s marketing capabilities are strong, starting from 2009. They initially positioned their products against Dyson, ensuring superior specifications at half the price. This strategy was highly successful and helped bind SharkNinja with Dyson in consumers’ minds. They have since expanded into various social media platforms for marketing.

4. **Integrated Channel Strategy**: SN integrates their marketing and sales strategies to create a unified channel approach. Unlike traditional offline stores, where customers need to see the product before deciding to buy, SN creates demand and completes the purchase cycle through offline channels. For example, they create usage scenarios on TV, social media, and live streams, then complete the sale in physical stores.

### Growth Strategies

SN has identified three key growth points:

1. Expanding existing markets.
2. Introducing new product lines.
3. Entering more countries.

These are some of their official statements; here are my personal insights:

In the small appliances industry, I believe SN’s development capabilities and supply chain management are among the best. Over these past ten years, they have continuously introduced innovative products. Among all the companies I know, SN has the strongest execution capability—once they set a timeline, they will do whatever it takes to achieve it. Moreover, this capability is now embedded in their company system, allowing them to replicate it across other industries. The rapid growth of the Ninja product line over the past two years is a testament to this. In five to eight years, SN could potentially become a $10 billion company.

## Current Leadership and Company Culture at SN

Currently, the core of SN remains with their CEO, Mark Barracos. Mark B is a longtime partner who has worked closely with Mark R for many years. After Mark R's departure, Mark B took on the leadership role and has proven capable of elevating the company to new heights. At just his prime, he still has at least two decades ahead in his career.

Beyond the company culture and core leadership, SN’s approach to suppliers and employees is also noteworthy. SN consistently offers some of the highest employee benefits in the industry, which means that even when there are overtime requirements, team members generally have no complaints. Regarding suppliers, SN's order margins are relatively high, and they compensate for any development work required from them. Such a combination of substantial orders and generous margins makes SN highly sought after by customers.

Typically, companies can make their clients profitable; however, truly exceptional ones also ensure that both employees and suppliers benefit. Companies like Huawei, Apple, and Dyson exemplify this principle.
