In the small household appliances industry, if you don't know SharkNinja, it likely means you haven't entered the field.

As a company I deeply admire and appreciate, I am particularly impressed by Shark's continuous self-improvement. 

My earliest interaction with SharkNinja was around 2012 when their company was still called Europro. Later on, due to the brand's tremendous success, they changed their name to match their brand.

In 2018, the entire brand was acquired by Ninebot for $1 billion. I was the first to report this news, which made me famous overnight in the industry. Previously, I produced a video that you can watch if interested.

SN went public on NASDAQ in 2022. In 2022, their sales reached $3.7 billion, and by 2024, this figure had grown to $5.5 billion. This growth is truly staggering.

Next, let's look at where Shark's growth comes from and why they can achieve it.

Firstly, a brief overview of Shark: In 2024, SN achieved sales of $5.5 billion, with Shark contributing $2.6 billion and Ninja contributing $2.9 billion. Comparing the data from 2022, Shark's sales were $2 billion, while Ninja was at $170 million. The main growth in recent years has been in Ninja, which increased by $1.2 billion.

In terms of market expansion, they have aggressively pursued internationalization, with international markets contributing $1.7 billion in sales. In the UK and Japan, Shark's market share is very high.

Their global supply chain is also extremely strong; they began setting up production bases in Southeast Asia and other countries at the beginning of the trade war. I've seen SN's supply chain team as one of the strongest; once a target is set by the team, it will go to any lengths to achieve it. Normally, during new product development, companies can invest in 3-5 prototypes, but SharkNinja would make hundreds of prototypes and send them to the U.S. for consumer testing and feedback collection, which is an extremely costly endeavor.

SN's growth in cleaning appliances has not been significant, but there are a few opportunities that Shark hasn't yet seized or fully exploited. One is the wet vacuum cleaner; currently, Shark's sales in this category are still low. Another is the robotic vacuum with a base station, which also needs improvement. I believe another major growth area is Shark's corded upright vacuums once they transition to lithium-ion batteries, as this could bring about significant growth. Therefore, how Shark leads in this aspect will be of great interest to those in the cleaning appliance industry.

Ninja has seen a tremendous increase in categories due to entering several new ones: coffee machines, outdoor appliances, personal care appliances such as hair dryers, and skincare devices like facial masks. Hair dryers alone, which were launched just one year ago, have already sold millions of units, which is truly impressive. SharkNinja themselves summarize four key reasons for their success:
1.

1. Disruptive Innovation. They have over 1,000 engineers and designers globally constantly thinking of various innovations. Teams in the United States, the UK, and China communicate 7*24 hours without interruption. The latest ideas are rapidly tested and implemented within the shortest possible time. Globally, they hold more than 5,200 patents. To be honest, Dyson has not been performing well in recent years; only SharkNinja continues to have sustained innovation. Everyone is watching SharkNinja's new products.

2. Global Agile Supply Chain.

The global supply chain has already been set up with suppliers in China, Thailand, Vietnam, Singapore, and Malaysia. Core products are sourced from 2-3 suppliers simultaneously for mold-making and shipping. The agile supply chain can quickly adapt to market changes. This advantage allows them to adjust their supply chains according to the market even if external factors change. For example, SN can switch between these six countries to ensure that tariffs do not exceed those of competitors. The benefit of having 2-3 suppliers for mold-making is that they will not miss order peaks due to production bottlenecks, and multiple suppliers continuously drive down procurement costs to maintain a competitive advantage.

3. 360-Degree Marketing.

SharkNinja has been very strong in marketing since 2009. Early products were benchmarked against Dyson, ensuring superior parameters while pricing was only half that of competitors. This strategy was highly successful and effectively linked SharkNinja with Dyson. Similar to how Xiaomi operates domestically.

4. Integrated Channels.

SN integrates their marketing and sales strategies into an integrated channel approach. Traditionally, when we go to a physical store to buy small appliances, we see the product before deciding whether to purchase it; if there is no demand, we may not buy anything. SN creates demand and completes the demand cycle through offline stores.

For example, they create various usage scenarios on TV, social media, live streaming channels, etc., and consumers complete sales in physical stores or online channels. This is similar to how domestic platforms like Xiaohongshu, Douyin, Taobao, and JD.com work, but their reach is broader.

They have identified three key growth points:

- Expanding existing scenarios
- Opening up more emerging product lines 
- Entering more countries

The above information comes from the company's official statements. Here are my personal views as well:

1. In the small appliance industry, I believe SN has the strongest development and supply chain capabilities. Over these 10+ years, they have continuously developed various innovative products. Among the companies I know, SN is the most execution-driven; they will do whatever it takes to meet their timelines with suppliers. Moreover, this capability has become a company-wide system that can be replicated across other industries. The rapid growth of the Ninja product line in recent years is a manifestation of this system. In the future, they could enter more markets and achieve even greater growth. I personally feel that within 5-8 years, SN could potentially become a company with over $1 billion in sales.

2. Currently, the core of SN remains its CEO, Mark Barracos. Mark B was a long-time partner of Mark R and took on the leadership role after Mark R left. He has proven his ability to take the company to new heights. Moreover, he is still in his prime and could continue for at least another 20 years.

3. Besides the company's culture and core leadership, I also find SN to be worthy of learning from in how they treat suppliers and employees. SN has always offered some of the highest employee benefits in the industry, so even when overtime is required, there are no complaints. Regarding suppliers, SN pays fees for any development work they do. High order volumes with good profits and non-nagging clients are always the most sought-after.

Companies that can make their customers profitable are already impressive; companies that can also make their employees and suppliers profitable are truly outstanding. Huawei, Apple, Dyson, and others all fall into this category.
