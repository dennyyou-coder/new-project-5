## Future Trends in the U.S. Market

Based on my overall assessment, I will make a judgment regarding the future trends of the U.S. market. **Disclaimer: This is purely my personal judgment; if the actual trend differs from what I predict, please feel free to correct me.**

### A Bold Prediction
**Firstly, I make a very bold prediction:** Trump's presidency may mark the end of the Sino-U.S. trade war or significantly reduce its intensity.

In recent years, I have written extensively about the impact of the Sino-U.S. trade war on cleaning appliances. The effects of this trade war on these devices were detailed in my previous articles.

### Current Status
After several years of turmoil, most major manufacturers related to North American clients have established factories in Southeast Asia.

Through repeated negotiations, all brands are now prepared for the worst-case scenario and have pulled their respective suppliers into Southeast Asian factories, regardless of their willingness.

#### Brand Sides:
- **Shark:** Shark's internal strategy is clear; top management requires a 100% transfer to Southeast Asia within 2-3 years.
- **TTI:** TTI saw significant industrial parks in Vietnam several years ago and initially required a 100% transfer. However, after practical operations over these years, about 50% of their business is still domestically based due to the inability to fully follow the original plan.
- **Bissell:** Bissell has also required their key suppliers to visit factories in Southeast Asia; production orders are currently proceeding smoothly.
- **iRobot:** Despite a severe decline in order volumes, iRobot has found some companies for overseas production.

#### Contract Manufacturers:
Due to client threats, the absence of factories in Southeast Asia means no orders. Over these years, all major manufacturers have established factories in Southeast Asia—some rented premises, others bought land early on, and still others formed joint ventures with local companies. In summary, each has found its own way.

From a purely investment perspective, most factories are likely to be unprofitable. The initial large expenses and additional equipment costs make it so. Moreover, orders from overseas factories are essentially transferred from domestic operations, offering little in the way of new business volume. Thus, this is considered a loss-making venture.

#### Component Supply Chain:
Over several years, some core component suppliers have also established factories abroad to meet client needs. To comply with local requirements, the supply chain continues to be refined and improved.

Therefore, the current situation is that, over these years of development, the cleaning appliance industry's supply chain in Southeast Asia has been continuously perfected, meeting North American clients' order demands. Can we then conclude that Southeast Asia can now replace China's cleaning appliance supply chain?

### Current Issues with Southeast Asian Supply Chain:
1. Production efficiency is currently on par with domestic levels; some countries (Vietnam) are experiencing rising labor costs. Additionally, these nations have a shortage of engineers, making it difficult to conduct further research and development in the short term. It will take 5-10 years for similar scale R&D and manufacturing integration.
2. The transfer of industries like textiles had a significant characteristic: overseas labor costs were extremely low, providing substantial price advantages over domestic markets. In contrast, initial product costs for this cleaning appliance supply chain are 8-10 percentage points higher than in China. Even now, they are only on par with domestic prices. From an industry-wide perspective, excluding tariff factors, Southeast Asia is unlikely to gain a price advantage over China in the next few years.
3. The supply chain is constantly evolving, with new products and technologies emerging rapidly. In the short term, Southeast Asian suppliers may struggle to keep up with these rapid updates. Currently, most machines sent overseas are older models, simpler, and produced in larger quantities.
4. Customer base is too narrow; only a few major North American clients require transfers, and no similar requirements exist from other countries (and likely won't). This results in insufficient order volumes spread across different nations (Indonesia, Vietnam, etc.). Without forming clusters, the local cleaning appliance industry could disappear if these current client orders are not sufficient.
5. Over-reliance on tariff policies; currently, tariffs on cleaning appliances stand at 25%, with rumors suggesting they may increase to 60% or even 100%. From a supply chain security perspective, all brands must prepare for this. However, given the development of trade wars over these years, such conflicts have not severely impacted China's various industries; instead, increased costs were passed on to American consumers. If tariffs are no longer maintained or further increased, overseas industrial foundations will cease to exist.

### What Will Happen After Trump's Presidency?
#### Scenario 1: Tariffs continue to increase to 60% or higher.
I believe this scenario is unlikely. Increasing tariffs even more would only result in higher daily costs for American consumers. Moreover, Trump’s previous policies aimed at bringing manufacturing back to the U.S., not Southeast Asia or Mexico. Therefore, I think further increases in tariffs are improbable.

### Possibility 2: Maintain Current Tariffs and Add Additional Duties on Certain Countries

Maintaining the current tariffs while adding duties to countries like Vietnam and Mexico. For products exported from China, keep the existing tariff level (25%) unchanged but increase the tariffs for Vietnam, Mexico, etc. The current 25% tariff is a mutual understanding that has already absorbed all policy costs. Keeping this at 25% would boost US tariff revenues. Additionally, increasing tariffs on some countries like Vietnam and Mexico can serve as a reminder to these "followers" to contribute more.

### Possibility 3: Lower or Even Eliminate Additional Tariffs

Originally, I thought this option was less likely. However, given the significant changes in international circumstances recently, it now seems that this possibility might be the highest.

The Russia-Ukraine war has been ongoing for four years, and the Israel-Palestine conflict is nearing its first year. The US is deeply involved in these conflicts, and these "followers" are no longer as obedient as before; there have been increasing instances of dissent. If the US fails to resolve these issues in the short term, it can be expected that America's prestige will continue to decline. This is more important than imposing additional tariffs on China’s vacuum cleaner industry.

Another variable is Elon Musk. As one of the most famous entrepreneurs and business leaders globally, Musk has founded companies like Tesla, Starlink, and SpaceX, which are leaders in their respective industries and have changed the world. Musk was deeply involved in Trump's re-election campaign. If Trump wins, Musk could be seen as having played a significant role, similar to how Lu Buwei supported Qin Shi Huang. The Shanghai factory of Tesla under Musk’s leadership produced vehicles within less than a year, which can be considered a lifeline for Musk.

From this perspective, if Trump were to take office and Musk had an influence on his policies, it is likely that China and the US could quickly reach multiple agreements (such as China purchasing Boeing aircraft) in just half a year. Trump might declare victory in the trade war under the guise of lowering living costs for Americans, thus ending the trade war.
