With the popularity of cordless vacuum cleaners, industry giants like Dyson have announced that they will no longer develop corded vacuum cleaners. Similarly, other major players such as TTI (Vax, Hoover, Dirt Devil), Rowenta, and others have also determined to cease developing wired products, fully transitioning to cordless vacuum cleaners.

![Who Will Be Eliminated in the Cordless Vacuum Era? image 1](/images/insights/who-will-be-eliminated-in-cordless-vacuums-image-001.jpg)

From feedback in the international market, traditional upright vacuum cleaners have seen a significant decline in sales in Europe and North America, with only wireless products maintaining sales in the Middle East, South America, and Africa. According to some friends and clients, the European market is currently undergoing a rapid transition towards wireless products.

In terms of OEM manufacturing, current information indicates that most development centers for OEM factories are focusing on wireless products. From a supply chain perspective, traditional upright vacuum cleaners have essentially been abandoned, with a full transition to the wireless market only a matter of time.

How long will it take for the market to fully transition to cordless vacuum cleaners? We can look at the adoption rate of smartphones as an example.

The following graph shows the global smartphone market from 2009 to 2013. It illustrates that, starting from less than 7% sales in Q1 2009, by Q4 2013 (with the iPhone launched in 2007), the market share more than quadrupled within just four years.

![Who Will Be Eliminated in the Cordless Vacuum Era? image 2](/images/insights/who-will-be-eliminated-in-cordless-vacuums-image-002.jpg)

Of course, we can say that the market comparison between wireless and corded vacuum cleaners is not as significant as the difference between smartphones and feature phones. Indeed, this chart is merely used to illustrate a point: the true market shift may come faster than anticipated. **Based on my judgment, within 2-3 years, the market share of cordless vacuum cleaners will account for approximately 30% of all vacuum cleaner sales. Currently, global annual vacuum cleaner sales are around one hundred million units, meaning that 30 million units would represent a 30% share.**

In the past, a significant portion of cordless vacuum cleaner sales consisted of low-end models using nickel-metal hydride or lithium-ion batteries with very weak suction power. These lower-end products will be phased out and replaced by more powerful wireless models.

![Who Will Be Eliminated in the Cordless Vacuum Era? image 3](/images/insights/who-will-be-eliminated-in-cordless-vacuums-image-003.jpg)

During the transition from wired to wireless models, significant impacts may be observed in the vacuum cleaner industry, particularly for industry professionals.


**Some current leading companies might fall behind, while new enterprises could seize opportunities to become new market giants.**


The upstream and downstream industries may also be affected:


1. Motor Industry. The motor industry is undergoing a complete transformation; motors used in cordless vacuum cleaners are fundamentally different from traditional brushed motors. Consequently, some sectors around the motors will disappear, such as the carbon brushes used in brushed motors losing their market.

Currently, leading companies in brushless motors include Japanese Nedic and domestic Starsheng. However, it's important to note that Dyson’s V10 motor remains unmatched in functionality among all current models, while maintaining a compact size.

![Who Will Be Eliminated in the Cordless Vacuum Era? image 4](/images/insights/who-will-be-eliminated-in-cordless-vacuums-image-004.jpg)

Additionally, the information I previously found indicates that Dyson developed the V10 motor (at 125,000 RPM) as early as four years ago. It can be seen from the following report that they developed a brushless motor with 100,000 RPM in 2009 and a motor with 120,000 RPM in 2014.

![Who Will Be Eliminated in the Cordless Vacuum Era? image 5](/images/insights/who-will-be-eliminated-in-cordless-vacuums-image-005.jpg)

![Who Will Be Eliminated in the Cordless Vacuum Era? image 6](/images/insights/who-will-be-eliminated-in-cordless-vacuums-image-006.jpg)

It is reasonable to speculate that Dyson has already developed the next-generation "V11" brushless motor (which could be either higher in speed or smaller in size). When competitors catch up with V10, Dyson will launch a new product to once again lead the market.

2. Circuit Boards. Traditional vacuum cleaners use simple circuit boards, which basically consist of some circuits and resistors—something that can be handled by a few people on an assembly line. In the wireless era, more complex circuit board designs and algorithms are currently beyond the capabilities of these small manufacturers. It is foreseeable that these smaller circuit board factories will soon be eliminated.

![Who Will Be Eliminated in the Cordless Vacuum Era? image 7](/images/insights/who-will-be-eliminated-in-cordless-vacuums-image-007.jpg)

Currently, entering the market for wirelessly controlled motor circuit boards are primarily some substantial suppliers with significant scale. Additionally, there is a trend where circuit control board development is increasingly being integrated with brushless motor manufacturers. Leading brushless motor factories such as Xingdesheng and Leku are also forming their own circuit board teams to take control of the core motor management.

3. Wire Cable Factories

Wire cable factories have had a direct impact, disappearing directly from cordless vacuum cleaners.

![Who Will Be Eliminated in the Cordless Vacuum Era? image 8](/images/insights/who-will-be-eliminated-in-cordless-vacuums-image-008.jpg)

4. Tubing and Other Accessories.

Since cordless vacuum cleaners do not require tubing, the leading manufacturer Jinhu Chun Guang has planned to transition into producing other accessories. According to Jinhu Chun Guang's prospectus, the funds raised will be extended to other vacuum cleaner attachments such as water tanks, floor washers, and extension tubes.

![Who Will Be Eliminated in the Cordless Vacuum Era? image 9](/images/insights/who-will-be-eliminated-in-cordless-vacuums-image-009.jpg)

5. Dust Bags

Dust bags were a significant component in upright vacuum cleaners, often sold alongside the vacuum cleaner and its accessories. The profit margin on dust bags and other attachments was typically higher than that of the vacuum cleaner itself. However, this market segment is gradually disappearing. Although G Tech and Oreck have introduced wireless charging dust bag vacuums, market feedback suggests that these products are not mainstream, with relatively modest sales volumes.

![Who Will Be Eliminated in the Cordless Vacuum Era? image 10](/images/insights/who-will-be-eliminated-in-cordless-vacuums-image-010.jpg)

6. Paint Factory.

Wireless vacuum cleaners are smaller in size compared to traditional models. More importantly, Dyson has consistently used injection molding for its vacuums, which can affect consumer aesthetics and may lead to an increase in manufacturers adopting this method. Currently, many people online criticize Dyson for using injection molding on such an expensive machine, resulting in noticeable marks.

![Who Will Be Eliminated in the Cordless Vacuum Era? image 11](/images/insights/who-will-be-eliminated-in-cordless-vacuums-image-011.jpg)

7. Lithium Battery Industry

In the era of cordless vacuum cleaners, the manufacturers of lithium batteries have reaped the greatest benefits. Many of these suppliers are major players with listed company backgrounds that have entered this currently booming market. Currently, Bluewei Electronics in Huizhou has positioned itself early in the competition for wireless lithium batteries and holds a high market share. Large battery suppliers from the power tool industry, such as Jiangsu Tianpeng, Haisiada, and Changhong Sijie, are also making strong entries into this field. Additionally, some leading battery companies from the lead-acid and nickel-metal hydride battery era are not lagging behind and are preparing to enter the market for a share of the profits.
