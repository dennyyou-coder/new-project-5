b. Introduction to the Vacuum Cleaner Industry

When I started writing articles a few years ago, vacuum cleaners were not widely discussed. Therefore, it was necessary to educate readers on basic knowledge. Over the past few years, the industry landscape has changed significantly with new players and technologies emerging continuously. The core of a vacuum cleaner lies in three areas: motor, battery, and electronics.

After several years of development, currently, domestic products have begun to catch up with Dyson in terms of motor performance. From the perspective of motors and batteries, the differences are no longer significant.

Currently, the vacuum cleaner market is dominated by domestic brands rather than foreign ones.

c. Types of Vacuum Cleaners

Vacuum cleaners can be categorized into upright vacuums, stick vacuums, handheld vacuums, canister vacuums, cordless handheld vacuums, robotic vacuums, and hard floor washers. Robotic vacuums and hard floor washers have seen significant technological advancements and changes in the market structure, which I will cover separately.

Upright Vacuums: These were previously the mainstream vacuum cleaners outside of North America. When we exported to Europe, most of them were this type. The key feature of these vacuums is that they are corded and relatively inexpensive. During our export phase, the factory price was mostly between $10-20, with a retail price abroad ranging from $20-40. Due to their inconvenience in use, they have gradually been replaced by cordless models. These vacuum cleaners are almost completely phased out of the domestic market. Here, I will provide this knowledge point without going into further detail. If you want to purchase such a vacuum cleaner, one that costs no more than $300 would be sufficient.

Interesting fact: Since the onset of the Russia-Ukraine war this year, expenses in Europe have risen significantly, making expensive vacuum cleaners less popular. As a result, affordable upright vacuum cleaners are seeing increased demand again.


Upright Vacuum Cleaners: Upright vacuum cleaners are called "upright" in English and are larger in size, primarily found in the North American market as well as countries like the UK and Australia. This type of vacuum cleaner has never been particularly popular in our country due to its large size and inconvenient use. Therefore, we will only provide knowledge about this type of vacuum cleaner here without recommending its purchase.


Handheld Vacuum Cleaners: Handheld vacuum cleaners are a simplified version of upright vacuum cleaners. They are cheaper and smaller in size. When we exported them previously, the price was around $10 or so. The advantage of small handheld vacuums is their affordability and compact size.

Canister Vacuum Cleaners: Canister vacuum cleaners can be considered a type of power tool as they can be used with various attachments and are primarily suited for industrial settings such as farms or factories. In China, the main usage scenarios for canister vacuum cleaners include rural bungalows, factories, and offices. Many people purchase these for initial cleaning tasks due to their relatively affordable price and large capacity.

Wireless Handheld Vacuum Cleaners (Pistol-Style): These wireless handheld vacuum cleaners mainly refer to Dyson's line of products, such as the V6, V8, and later V12 models. The classic V8 model is one of the most imitated designs in the market. Prior to Dyson, Electrolux also had a good sales record with its cordless upright vacuum cleaners. However, why did Electrolux's products disappear? The primary reason was that Dyson introduced high-speed digital motors (known as brushless motors domestically), which could reach 80,000 RPM initially, compared to around 20,000 RPM for other vacuum cleaner motors. Thus, Dyson led the era of high-speed motors, and all subsequent Chinese manufacturers followed suit, completely shifting the industry towards Dyson's technology. We will focus on recommending these types of vacuum cleaners.

Ultrasonic Mattress Pests Eliminators: The first ultrasonic mattress pests eliminators were invented by Raycop in South Korea, adding a UV lamp to the vacuum cleaner for sterilization purposes. However, the actual UV lamp is ineffective; its main function is to vibrate and suck, meaning that any ordinary wireless vacuum cleaner can achieve similar results. Are ultrasonic mattress pests eliminators a form ofIQ tax (a term often used in China to refer to unnecessary purchases)? Not entirely. 1. Ultrasonic mattress pests eliminators are relatively inexpensive, typically costing between 200-300 RMB. Although the UV lamp is not very effective, people do not pay extra for it. 2. Ultrasonic mattress pests eliminators serve as a specialized vacuum cleaner for beds, increasing bed cleaning frequency.

Currently, many brands have introduced dual-canister ultrasonic mattress pests eliminators. In reality, there is no significant difference between a single-canister and a dual-canister model, so there is no need to purchase the more expensive dual-canister version.

Wireless Stick Vacuums: Early wireless handheld vacuum cleaners were limited by motor and battery technology; their suction power was very weak, and battery life was poor. Charging for 10 hours could only provide about 5 minutes of use... quite a waste. With the advent of high-speed brushless motors and advanced lithium-ion batteries, wireless stick vacuums emerged with stronger suction capabilities that can be used at any time.

Car Vacuum Cleaners: Car vacuum cleaners are a niche within the vacuum cleaner category, primarily powered by either cigarette lighter sockets or lithium-ion batteries.

Key Points for Choosing a Vacuum Cleaner:

Canister Vacuum Cleaners: We no longer recommend these. If you must purchase one, consider models priced under 300 RMB. Early canister vacuum cleaners from brands like Dyson, Miele, and Frigidaire cost several thousand RMB but are now less convenient than wireless handheld models.

Upright Vacuum Cleaners: These are bulky and inconvenient to use; few domestic manufacturers produce them, so we do not recommend purchasing them.

Push-Along Vacuums: Typically have motors under 400-600W, come with cords, and cost no more than 200 RMB. Performance differences are minimal; the key points for selection are: choose a model priced below 200 RMB and select one you like in terms of design; ensure the motor power is at least 400W to guarantee sufficient suction.

Canister Vacuum Cleaners: When choosing canister vacuum cleaners, focus on two aspects: power (typically between 1000-1400W), as higher power means greater suction, and the capacity of the canister. Canister vacuum cleaner technology is not particularly complex, so there's no need to spend too much; because they use dry-wet dual-use motors, these models are generally louder than standard ones.

Wireless Handheld Vacuum Cleaner: Currently, the wireless handheld vacuum cleaner is a major focus in the industry and fiercely contested by various brands. Prices range from Dyson's V12 at around $5000 to domestic models starting at just over $500. How should one choose? The core of a wireless handheld vacuum cleaner is its high-speed brushless motor, with speeds ranging from 60,000 RPM to 160,000 RPM. Higher speed motors offer better performance but come with higher costs and greater power consumption. A typical choice would be an 80,000 RPM motor; higher speeds often do not fully utilize their capabilities. For example, the Dyson V12 has a maximum speed of 120,000 RPM but can only run at this speed for less than 10 minutes before shutting down.

Some cheaper wireless handheld vacuum cleaners use ordinary carbon brush motors with speeds around 20,000 RPM, offering weaker performance and lower prices.


Ultrasonic Mattress Toppers: The UV lamps in ultrasonic mattress toppers are ineffective; they need to remain stationary for about an hour to achieve sterilization. The actual effectiveness comes from the beating function and the vacuum motor. Currently, dual-chamber ultrasonic mattress toppers cost more than twice as much as single-chamber models but offer no significant additional benefits. In earlier years, dual or even triple cyclone designs appeared on upright vacuums but were eventually phased out due to lack of practical use.

For most users, a single-chamber ultrasonic mattress topper is sufficient. Wired models under $300 and wireless models under $600 are generally adequate.


Point-of-Sale Vacuum Cleaner: A point-of-sale vacuum cleaner essentially serves as a single-point solution; it should feature a brushless motor for better performance on larger debris.

e. Brand Introduction of Vacuum Cleaners (Dyson, Roborock, Ecovacs, Tineco, Xiaomi, Anker, Eufy, Kingclean):


Dyson: James Dyson, former British billionaire and the country's largest private landowner, is known as the father of cyclonic vacuum cleaners and wireless intelligent vacuums. Two of the most important functions in the vacuum cleaner industry—cyclone systems and high-speed brushless motors (industry terms for "wireless")—were invented by Dyson. In previous years, Dyson's vacuum cleaners were among the highest-selling, priciest, and best-performing models. Dyson’s position in the vacuum cleaner market is somewhat akin to Apple’s in the smartphone industry. However, with competitors catching up, Dyson’s advantages are not as significant as before. Recent products have also deviated from optimal technical paths, garnering less attention from the industry.


Dyson's strength lies in its brushless motors, which were about 2-3 years ahead of the industry early on. While motor parameters today may not differ much, Dyson still stands out among high-end brands compared to other companies like Electrolux and Miele.


Tineco: Tineco is a part of Xiaomi's ecosystem. In 2019, Tineco experienced significant growth in the cleaning appliance sector, with its first wireless vacuum cleaner selling over 700 million yuan in its first year, creating an industry miracle. Over just four years, it achieved sales of 4 billion yuan. Last year, it raised 3.6 billion yuan and was valued at 17 billion yuan. Tineco has made substantial investments in motor technology; the V16 motor released last year reached a speed of 160,000 RPM, surpassing Dyson's production models.


Currently, Tineco’s products primarily compete with Dyson while offering more affordable prices as a substitute for Dyson. In reality, Tineco’s vacuum cleaners perform well in terms of functionality and craftsmanship.

Roborock: As one of the first Taobao brands, Roborock is the only survivor among those that were named alongside it back in the day. The reason is simple; Roborock has been a dedicated player in the cleaning appliance industry for many years and continuously invests in research and development. Its products are still worth recommending within the domestic vacuum cleaner market.


Midea: Midea, as a giant in small appliances with sales reaching several hundred billion yuan, boasts strong brand awareness and distribution channels. Since its acquisition of Chunhua Appliances over a decade ago, Midea has continued to acquire brands such as Toshiba, Youree Home, Sharp, etc., investing significant resources into cleaning appliances. Currently, Midea holds considerable influence in the cleaning appliance industry, especially in mid-to-low-end models where other companies cannot compete with it.


SUPOR: SUPOR is a giant in kitchen small appliances and competes fiercely with Midea in this sector. In recent years, SUPOR has also invested heavily in the cleaning appliance market. Its advantages include fast execution and financial resources, allowing it to dominate the mid-to-low-end vacuum cleaner market. Currently, most mid-to-low-end vacuum cleaners are dominated by Midea and SUPOR.


Foreign Brands: Foreign brands such as Electrolux, Philips, Miele, Panasonic, Bosch, Siemens, and Vorwerk have largely retreated in the Chinese vacuum cleaner industry over the years, falling behind domestic brands in terms of product performance, price, and features. I do not recommend these brands; currently, only Dyson is recommended among foreign brands, as other domestic brands are performing well.


Narwal: Formerly known as TEK, Narwal was a major contract manufacturer for vacuum cleaners before transitioning to its own brand. Creo started as a robot vacuum brand and went public, with Narwal being the smart home appliance brand under Creo. In recent years, Narwal has gained significant attention in the industry due to its hard floor washers; however, prior to the popularity of floor washers, Narwal primarily promoted intelligent handheld vacuum cleaners. Despite this, Narwal did not find a breakthrough point in smart vacuums but excelled in floor washers.


Kingclean: Kingclean is a domestic contract manufacturing giant and listed company. Kingclean leads in manufacturing, quality control, and motor production. Due to its contract manufacturing capabilities, Kingclean surpasses other companies in the industry. Since the 2008 financial crisis, Kingclean has launched its own brand "LEXY," positioning it as a premium brand with relatively higher prices.


Shark (SharkNinja): Shark is the leading brand by sales in North America and has the largest shipment volume there. In 2019, it was acquired by Joyoung. I wrote about this article in 2019, which caused significant buzz in the industry. Later, Shark attempted to enter the Chinese market by introducing popular models from abroad. However, due to differences between domestic and international markets, Shark's products perform better in North America than in China.
