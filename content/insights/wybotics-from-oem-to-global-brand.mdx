WYBOTICS entered pool-cleaning robotics in 2005. A year later, it developed an automatic cleaner able to work across the floor and walls of an in-ground pool. Nearly two decades later, the manufacturer behind other pool-robot brands filed for a Hong Kong listing and began moving from supporting actor to lead role.

The most important part of its filing is not only revenue growth. It is the change in where that revenue comes from.

From 2023 to 2025, revenue increased from RMB 378 million to RMB 544 million and then RMB 812 million, a compound annual growth rate of 46.6%. Net profit rose from RMB 61 million to RMB 71 million and RMB 100 million. In 2025, production reached about 615,700 units against capacity of 624,000 units, a utilization rate of 98.7%.

![WYBOTICS moves from manufacturing to brand ownership](/images/blog/wybotics-from-oem-to-global-brand-image-002.webp)

Earlier, WYBOTICS looked like a typical OEM and ODM supplier. It developed and manufactured products for customers while the customer owned the brand and end-user relationship. That model offers clearer orders and lets a factory scale engineering, quality, cost and delivery. Its limit is equally clear: the supplier does not control the brand asset, channel pricing or direct feedback from users.

The structure has now changed. Branded products accounted for 67.1% of revenue in 2023, 72.4% in 2024 and 83.5% in 2025. ODM products fell to 15.6% in 2025. This is more than opening an online store. The nature of the company's revenue has shifted.

Amazon contributed RMB 404 million in 2025, or 49.7% of total revenue. Shopify contributed RMB 121.7 million, while offline third-party sellers of branded products generated RMB 131 million. The top five customers represented only 22.0% of revenue.

## Cordless products opened a brand window

Two changes helped the transition: pool robots moved from corded to cordless, and distribution moved from specialist offline channels toward e-commerce. Cordless products lowered the operating barrier for households. Amazon and direct-to-consumer websites weakened the exclusive grip of traditional pool channels and gave manufacturers a way to reach global users directly.

According to the market data cited in the filing, global cordless pool-robot shipments grew from about 200,000 units in 2020 to 2.5 million in 2025, while cordless penetration rose from 6.6% to 51.5%. In 2024, WYBOTICS held about 9.2% of total global pool-robot shipments and 19.7% of cordless shipments.

![The global pool robot competitive table](/images/blog/wybotics-from-oem-to-global-brand-image-003.webp)

That does not mean WYBOTICS has displaced traditional leaders across the entire market. It means the company achieved scale early in the cordless product cycle. Corded pool robots favored established brands, specialist dealers and service systems. Cordless products are lighter, easier to explain online and closer to consumer electronics.

But the same shift creates the next challenge. A manufacturer is measured by cost, quality and delivery. A consumer brand must also manage product definition, communication, pricing, advertising, returns, compliance, recalls and after-sales service. In 2024, WYBOTICS' global shipment share was higher than its estimated GMV share, showing that volume has grown faster than brand premium.

Going public would only be a milestone. Becoming the lead actor means defining the user experience and carrying responsibility for the product long after it leaves the factory. That is the real test of WYBOTICS' move from OEM to global brand.
