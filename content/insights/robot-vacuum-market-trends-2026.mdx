## The category is moving beyond basic cleaning

Robot vacuum buyers are no longer only comparing suction power and price. The category is becoming a broader home cleaning system, where navigation, wet cleaning, docking automation, and after-sales reliability all influence purchasing decisions.

For brands and distributors, this means product selection needs to start with the target customer segment rather than the feature list.

## What buyers are asking for

- Better obstacle avoidance
- More reliable mopping performance
- Easier maintenance
- Stronger app experience
- Lower return risk
- Clear product positioning for each price band

## Sourcing implications

Buyers should pay close attention to supplier experience, firmware stability, spare parts support, and product certification. A lower unit price can quickly become expensive if returns or customer complaints increase.

## Business opportunity

There is still room for differentiated private label products, especially when brands focus on a clear customer problem instead of copying the same feature stack as every competitor.
