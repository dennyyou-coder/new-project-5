With CloudWhisk's explosive growth in self-cleaning robotic vacuums, its valuation has risen accordingly. It is reported that by mid-2021, the valuation had surpassed $500 million, and shares were long since fully subscribed.

![Original image 1](/images/insights/robot-vacuum-crossroads-category-shift-image-001.jpg)

The valuation logic for CloudWhale is quite straightforward, mirroring that of Roborock. At the time, Roborock had a market value of over 6 billion yuan and sales of approximately 4 billion yuan. In 2020, CloudWhale's sales were around 1 billion yuan, with an expected 2 billion yuan in sales for 2021—essentially reaching half the sales and valuation of Roborock.

With the emergence of CloudWhale J1 as a phenomenon-level product, existing industry giants such as Ecovacs, Roborock, Dreame, Xiaomi, and others have launched similar product lines. This has put some pressure on CloudWhale. The industry is also closely focusing on CloudWhale's next-generation products.

![Original image 2](/images/insights/robot-vacuum-crossroads-category-shift-image-002.jpg)

In September 2021, Roborock released the new J2 self-cleaning robot vacuum, which added automatic drying of cloths, automatic addition of cleaning agents, and water supply and drainage functions to its existing product lineup. The most distinctive feature is the automatic water supply and drainage function, eliminating the need for manual water replenishment and waste disposal. It has been said that Roborock aims to compete with washing machines in the future, thereby expanding the market's potential size.

![Original image 3](/images/insights/robot-vacuum-crossroads-category-shift-image-003.jpg)

For this new product, opinions in the industry are mixed.

As self-cleaning becomes the mainstream direction for robot vacuums, many new players have entered this market. The long-dormant market is set to experience another wave of excitement.

Founded on January 6, 2021, ShellBug Smart has completed two rounds of financing. The first round was led by KPCB and Wework Capital, while the second round was co-led by Huize Capital and MaiXing Investment, with follow-on investments from KPCB and Wework Capital, raising over 100 million RMB in total.

ShellBug Smart's team has released its first fully automatic floor washer, XWOW. The innovation lies in its cleaning module, which does not use traditional brushes or dual disks but instead employs a track-based cloth. Due to the larger contact area with the ground, this design provides more thorough cleaning compared to conventional models in a single pass.

**However, Methrix is not the only company entering the robotic vacuum cleaner market. Another major player rumored to be entering this space may actually take action soon.**

DJI Innovation, the global leader in drone technology with a market share of over 70%, has released products that have significantly impacted its competitors each time. Estimated sales for 2020 were around 15 billion yuan, and its overall valuation is close to 200 billion yuan.

Since a few years ago, there have been rumors that DJI has been researching and developing robotic vacuums, with the product nearing market readiness.


DJI's advantages include:

1. The company excels in autonomous navigation technology for drones, making it relatively easy to transfer this expertise to robotic vacuums, potentially even enhancing its capabilities.
2. DJI products are known for their high quality, surpassing many companies currently operating in the vacuum cleaner industry.
3. DJI's global brand and sales prowess far exceed those of most current market players. It is reasonable to expect that if DJI enters the market with a robotic vacuum, achieving several billion yuan in annual sales within its first year would not be a significant challenge.
4. DJI has deep expertise in the field of robotic vacuums. The company's former chairman, Li Zexiang, is an investor in CloudWhisk (a brand known for its robotic mowers and cleaners).
