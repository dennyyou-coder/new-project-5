A few days ago, I heard from a supply chain friend that Laifen plans to enter the hard floor washer market. The team has come to Suzhou for an inspection.

Previously, one of my videos about Laifen's electric shavers received recognition from Mr. Ye, the CEO, with over 100,000 views across several platforms. This time, I will help analyze how Laifen can enter this market if they decide to do so.

Firstly, let's examine the current industry landscape and identify any potential breakthrough points:

According to AVC data, Tineco remains at the top with a market share of around 40%, followed closely by Dreame at about 25%. Haier comes next, then other brands.

Next, we will look at Tineco's product line and explore the breakthroughs made by other brands:

Tineco's products are roughly divided into four price segments, each with a different series to defend against competitors:

1. Above 5000 yuan is the FV Art Station, Space Station series. At this price point, sales volume is limited; it mainly serves to establish brand image. I noticed that all possible features have been included: hot water cleaning, automatic turning, and automatic water supply and drainage. The current product's appearance and functionality have seen significant improvements as well.

### 2. The Artist Series

The Artist series spans a price range from RMB 2,000 to 4,000 and focuses more on aesthetics. The high-end version includes cordless steam functionality.

### 3. Aesthetic Master Series

The Aesthetic Master series is essentially the volume-oriented version of the Artist series, with prices ranging from RMB 2,000 to 2,500. It features basic functionalities such as flat mopping, electrolyzed water, hot water, and front/rear assistance.

### 4. Main Volume Price Segment: Streth Series

The Streth series targets the main volume price segment with prices ranging from RMB 1,500 to 2,200. It includes basic functionalities like flat mopping, electrolyzed water, hot water, and front/rear assistance.

### 5. The Roborock 2.0 as a Gatekeeper

The Roborock 2.0, originally priced over RMB 3,000, has now been reduced to around RMB 1,000, positioning it as a gatekeeper for the hard floor washer market. This price point puts significant pressure on competitors. Compared to other brands, the Roborock 2.0 offers strong product capabilities.

From the Roborock product lineup, we can see that they have robust offerings across all price segments to defend against competition. In 2022, the key breakthroughs were flat mopping and multi-functional integration in vacuum cleaners. This year, Roborock has made flat mopping a standard feature while introducing multifunctional models in each series to counteract competitors.

### Other Competitors' Responses

#### Dreame Product Line

Dreame's product line ranges from RMB 1,100 to 2,100 and largely avoids the high-end segment above RMB 3,000. They focus on volume sales with competitive pricing, positioning themselves against Roborock in this price range.

#### Roborock Product Line

Roborock has consistently invested less in hard floor washers. Their first released model was criticized for not being able to stand upright. Roborock's strategy focuses on key products with fewer SKUs. Currently, their main product is the A30 series priced from RMB 1,999 to 2,999, targeting the RMB 2,000-3,000 segment of Ecovacs' aesthetic master series. The steam version of A30 (RMB 2,999) competes with Roborock's Artist 70 (around RMB 4,499).

#### Uwant

Uwant has successfully launched dual-cup mite removers and upholstery cleaners. Currently, they focus on the RMB 1,200-1,500 segment for hard floor washers. Their promotion on Bilibili is intense, often compared to major brands like massage devices and electric toothbrushes. Sales are strong, with annual sales of several hundred thousand units.

#### Narwal

Narwal follows a similar single-product strategy as Roborock. Prices range from RMB 900 to 2,000. The entry-level price of RMB 900 is surprising; at this price point, including various deductions, it barely covers costs and could result in losses if the return rate is high. Narwal sells several hundred thousand units annually with positive market feedback.

#### Midea

Midea's product line ranges from RMB 1,000 to 3,000, with volume sales concentrated around RMB 1,300. In 2023-24, Midea seized an opportunity in hard floor washers and achieved decent sales. At the RMB 1,000 price point, Midea is a household brand, making it easier for consumers to purchase lower-priced models.

#### Haier

Haier's hard floor washer prices range from RMB 1,000 to 3,000. They entered the market early with Shanchuan double-brush washers and briefly ranked third in the industry. Currently, their main volume model is priced between RMB 1,200 and 1,500. Haier was one of the first teams to sell hard floor washers on Douyin, achieving strong sales. Annual sales are around 1 million units.

#### Supor

Supor's price range is from RMB 800 to 1,500. While Supor is a well-known brand, its primary association remains with kitchen appliances and cookware. They have performed well in the cleaning appliance market with reasonable pricing strategies that allow for volume sales while avoiding direct competition with Roborock and Dreame.

#### Deerma

Deerma's price range is from RMB 800 to 1,500, similar to Supor and Midea but with higher configurations. Deerma adopted a low-price strategy early in the hard floor washer market. Unlike Xiaomi, they do not follow its pricing model. However, their technical capabilities are weaker, making it challenging to profit from this complex product line.

#### Xiaomi

Xiaomi's hard floor washers range from RMB 799 to 3,899. Xiaomi is a formidable competitor for all product manufacturers due to its price advantage in configurations compared to Roborock, Ecovacs, Narwal, and Dreame. However, this advantage is not as pronounced as it was with "half-price big brands."

Xiaomi's current challenge lies in selling exclusively through their own channels due to low profit margins, resulting in minimal presence on platforms like Douyin or Xiaohongshu. Following Lei Jun's electric vehicle venture, Xiaomi's ecosystem products have seen significant sales growth. Their focus is now on the RMB 2,000 price segment.

#### Dyson

Dyson's hard floor washers are not considered in this analysis.

### Industry Summary

Tineco remains the market leader, having occupied all possible product forms at this stage. Other brands have not achieved breakthroughs in their products as they did before, but Tineco's extensive product range has also brought significant choice challenges to consumers. This is an unavoidable situation.

### Price Segments

#### Above 3000 RMB
Products above 3000 RMB are mainly focused on brand marketing and do not significantly boost sales.

#### 2000-3000 RMB
Currently, Tineco and Dreame dominate this price segment. Other brands find it difficult to enter this market. Given the current economic conditions, products above this price point struggle to attract consumer spending. The functionalities of these products should cover all mainstream features.

#### 1500-2000 RMB
This is the most competitive and profitable price segment. Roborock, Narwal, Tineco, and Dreame have deployed a large number of products and marketing resources here. Competition in this segment is fierce, with weaker brands hesitant to enter.

#### 1000-1500 RMB
This segment is primarily targeted by domestic brands such as Midea, Haier, Supor, Uwant, etc., competing mainly against Tineco. Products at this price point should focus on basic functionalities while controlling costs to avoid losses.

#### 800-1000 RMB
The cost pressure in this segment is significant. Tineco and Dreame have largely abandoned it. The main competitors here are Deerma, Midea, Haier, Supor, etc., with Narwal launching a product priced around 900 RMB.

#### Below 800 RMB
This segment is dominated by white-label companies, which we will not discuss further.

The industry has become highly competitive, with limited room for product innovation. Brands are fiercely guarding their main price segments. Tineco's net profit margin has declined from an initial 30% to single digits. If Laifen were to enter the market, it is unlikely that they would make a profit given their focus on better craftsmanship and lower prices. Given that Xiaomi has struggled with this pricing strategy in the hard floor washer segment for years, Laifen's lack of its own traffic channels makes breaking through difficult.

### Potential Breakthrough Points for Laifen

1. **Product Price Segment**: Set the price range between 1800-2000 RMB. Products above this price point struggle to sell, and below it, cost constraints limit product innovation. Considering marketing investments, a certain profit margin is necessary to drive sales.

2. **Product Form Factor**:
   - Basic functionalities: 180-degree flat design, single brush roller, hair entanglement prevention, brush drying, hot water and electrolyzed water.
   - Dual brushes are unnecessary; the combination of functions 5 and 1 can be optional.

The only potential breakthrough could be in the base station for automatic water supply and waste disposal. Tineco calls it a Space Station, currently priced around 4499 RMB. This price point is unlikely to generate significant sales volume. Currently, most robot vacuums on the Chinese market come with a base station. However, for hard floor washers, the issue lies in the fact that collected waste either needs filtration or direct disposal into the drainage system. If filtered, it requires regular cleaning, negating the utility of the base station. Direct disposal could potentially clog the pipes.

A module similar to a kitchen garbage disposal can be added to the base station, breaking down and flushing waste from the sewage tank directly into the drainage system. This would make hard floor washers as convenient as robot vacuums, with no post-use maintenance required.

The cost of implementing these features is high, making it difficult to maintain a price point around 2000 RMB. If Laifen can sell this product at that price, it could pose significant challenges to Tineco and Dreame, whose product lines are already well-defined in terms of functionality and pricing.

3. **Laifen's Strengths**: Strong marketing capabilities. With such a product, they have the potential to sell hundreds of thousands of units annually, adding approximately 1 billion RMB in sales revenue.
