**"Tekai (TEK) is just in trade; their technology is not good."** During the early years when Ecovacs was still Tekai, although it quickly rose to become one of the top three players in the industry, evaluations within the sector consistently stated that "their trade capabilities are decent, but their technology and quality are average."

**"Ecovacs' robot vacuums are just toys; they have no real utility. Look at how they stopped their robot vacuum project." "Yes, I heard that their robotic vacuums insist on using their own brand for export, not providing private labeling to customers. How can they sell them?"** Ecovacs received a lot of skepticism when it started its robotic vacuum business.

**"Ecovacs spends such high marketing costs every year; all their sales are driven by advertising. They cannot compare with companies likeRoborock (STone), which are true high-tech firms."** After listing, evaluations from various fund company investors were predominantly negative towards Ecovacs.

**"I have never heard of the Tineco brand; why can it sell for as much as Dyson? Where does this confidence come from?" "Their hard floor washers are not practical at all. Major foreign brands struggle to sell more than a few units per year, so how can they possibly sell them at such high prices?"** The Tineco brand faced external skepticism during its first two years of existence, and even internal employees shared the same doubts.

**All these doubts became history in 2020.**

In 2020, due to the outbreak of the pandemic and the rise of live-streaming sales, the Tineco brand's hard floor washers suddenly became a hit in the industry, opening up a new category and becoming its champion and symbol. Riding on the success of Tineco, Ecovacs' stock price surged 12 times in 2020, with its market capitalization reaching over 150 billion yuan.

Tiankang's explosive growth from 2020 to 2021 was multifaceted and not easily replicable:

1. The uniqueness of the mopping machine product: In 2020, only Tiankang and Maytronics offered mopping machines in the industry. This meant that Tiankang could leverage this product to penetrate all channels. Other brands began following suit with their own mopping machine products only starting to launch in late May or June of 2021, by which time Tiankang had already released several new generations of products.

2. The surge in demand for cleaning appliances due to the pandemic: During the early stages of the pandemic, the first wave of increased demand was for cleaning appliances as people stayed at home and focused on household chores and cooking.

3. The rise of live-streaming e-commerce: On traditional e-commerce platforms like JD and Taobao, emerging brands such as Tiankang could not reach a large audience even with extensive advertising investments because mopping machines were a new category that consumers were unfamiliar with and there was no established traffic pool to expand through ads. However, the rise of live-streaming and short videos changed this landscape. Through the promotion by KOLs (Key Opinion Leaders) at both the top and mid-tier levels, Tiankang could immediately reach more consumers and generate significant sales.

**Through these factors acting in concert, Tiankang achieved its remarkable success from 2020 to 2021.**

The victory with the mopping machine campaign has led to widespread acclaim for Ke Guowei, the CEO of Ecovacs (Tiankang's parent company), as "a one-hit wonder." It seems that Ke Guowei has always been in the right place at the right time throughout his entrepreneurial journey. However, a deeper analysis reveals that Ecovacs’s path to success was not as smooth as it might appear.

Mr. Qian has a very keen market insight and is always able to see various opportunities ahead of the market (early vacuum cleaners, robotic vacuums, and mopping machines were no exception). However, due to technical limitations, many times seeing an opportunity does not necessarily mean that a suitable product can be developed. For a company leader at such moments, whether one can make the correct judgment and stick with it is very rare. If someone can make decisions in multiple critical moments, it indicates that they are not relying on luck.

Now, Mr. Qian is optimistic about another product: the smart wok. Mr. Qian has promoted this cooking pot vigorously in various public appearances, and I have personally stepped up to support this product. Personally, I do not hold high expectations for the short-term performance of this smart wok because its form is still too early-stage. However, it is possible that after 2-3 iterations, the smart wok could become as ubiquitous as microwaves or ovens.

As an entrepreneur, the success or failure of a single product does not determine the fate of the company. Cultivating a qualified successor before retirement is the most important.

David Cheng Qian (English name: Qian Cheng), born in 1990 in Nanjing, Jiangsu Province, graduated from the University of British Columbia with a bachelor's degree. He joined Ecovacs in 2012 and currently serves as Deputy Chairman of the Ecovacs Group and General Manager of the Home Robot Division. Unlike other second-generation wealthy heirs who hold many titles in family enterprises merely for appearance, Qian Cheng is the actual operator of Ecovacs' robot vacuum business.

![Article image 4](/images/insights/ecovacs-at-a-crossroads-image-004.jpg)

Qiong Dongqi and many bosses of large and small factories in Suzhou are veterans who started their businesses on the front lines. As they age and the market environment continues to change, facing new-generation entrepreneurs like Chang Jing from Roborock and Yu Hao from Dreame Technology, using internet + capital + new marketing strategies, the older generation often feel powerless. And Qian Cheng, this 90s CEO, has rotated through various positions, bringing in new ideas and leading Ecovacs with direct competition against new rivals.

If Tineco's hard floor washers represent a revolution for vacuum cleaners, Ecovacs's new X1 is a head-on declaration of war on the robot vacuumindustry.

![Article image 5](/images/insights/ecovacs-at-a-crossroads-image-005.jpg)

After Narwal launched its self-cleaning robot vacuum J1 and iRobot introduced its dust collection robot, which both achieved success, several leading companies followed suit. Xiaomi, Roborock, Ecovacs, Tineco, Narwal, Dreame, MOVA, Aiper, Beatbot, Maytronics, SharkNinja, Dyson, Deerma, Uwant, Anker, and Xiaomi all promptly released similar products in the market.

However, Ecovacs not only launched its dust collection robot but also its self-cleaning robot vacuum N9+. In 2021, it further introduced its flagship robot X1. This product combined both dust collection and self-cleaning functionalities, potentially making standalone dust collectors and self-cleaning floor moppers transitional products.

According to rumors, Ecovacs aimed for a target sales volume of 1.5 million units for its 2022 flagship model X1. Some friends questioned whether this figure was too exaggerated and unlikely to be achieved. Given that Narwal's single self-cleaning robot vacuum sold around 1 million units in 2021, if Ecovacs increased its marketing efforts and slightly reduced prices, it could easily achieve a significant market impact on such products.

During Qichen's tenure managing the robotic vacuum segment, Ecovacs' products showed noticeable improvements in product quality and design compared to previous years when they only focused on advertising. To control raw material costs, Ecovacs even invested 1.2 billion yuan in establishing a battery factory.

![Article image 6](/images/insights/ecovacs-at-a-crossroads-image-006.jpg)

Ecovacs aimed to dominate the market for robotic vacuums and hard floor washers in 2022, but its competitors clearly did not share this ambition.

In January 2022, Roborock unveiled the S7 MaxV Ultra at the CES exhibition in the United States. This model featured similar functional modules to Ecovacs X1, clearly positioning itself to compete directly against the Ecovacs X1.

![Article image 7](/images/insights/ecovacs-at-a-crossroads-image-007.jpg)

On February 2, 2022, Bissell Inc. filed a petition with the United States International Trade Commission (ITC) in Grand Rapids, Michigan, requesting a Section 337 investigation against Tineco to prohibit the import and sale of Tineco-branded hard floor washers into the United States. Hard floor washers are the cornerstone of the Tineco brand; Tineco cannot afford to lose this battle.
