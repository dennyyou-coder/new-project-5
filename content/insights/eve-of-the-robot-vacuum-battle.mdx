## Market Growth Report for Robotic Vacuums


In the recent Hong Kong Stock Exchange prospectus of Roborock, I found a report on the future market growth of robotic vacuums. According to the data, in 2024, global sales of robotic vacuums reached 20.6 million units with a total revenue of $9.3 billion. By 2029, these figures are expected to rise to 43.2 million units and $25.2 billion respectively.

Roborock's sales in 2024 amounted to RMB 11.9 billion, with 3.5 million robotic vacuums sold at a revenue of RMB 10.89 billion. The company also sold 619,000 hard floor washers and mop machines for RMB 1.07 billion. Currently, Roborock is leading in the global market when it comes to robotic vacuum sales alone. Overall, Roborock's team is strong, young, stable, and highly efficient with a high compound annual growth rate.

However, Roborock still lacks a second growth curve. Although its robotic vacuums are performing well, they have not yet significantly outpaced competitors in terms of technology or market share. The hard floor washers have seen rapid growth but may struggle to achieve the same level of industry dominance as the robotic vacuums. The company's primary focus on washing machines might shift towards regular updates with minimal investment.

### Denny’s Commentary

Roborock’s second growth curve is likely in outdoor robots, specifically lawn mowers and pool cleaners. These markets have already demonstrated significant potential, making it a logical next step for the company. According to various sources, Roborock plans to launch its lawn mower this year with an initial production of tens of thousands of units, aiming for 200,000 by next year. Pool cleaner launches are also expected in the near future. With these capabilities, Roborock could challenge other outdoor robot manufacturers and become a market leader.

## Ecovacs: A Strong Recovery

Ecovacs reported total revenues of RMB 165.42 billion for 2024, with global sales of its main brand at 2.95 million units and revenue of RMB 80.82 billion. The Tineco brand (primarily hard floor washers) saw a global shipment of 4.14 million units and generated RMB 80.61 billion in revenue.

Ecovacs has quickly recovered from its setbacks in 2023, with both brands showing strong growth. While it lags behind Roborock in robotic vacuum sales, Ecovacs aims to achieve double-digit billion revenues by 2025, which is a realistic goal given the company’s current performance.

Ecovacs’ strengths lie in its team's resilience and new product recognition. Led by Mr. Qian, the company has stabilized its operations after overcoming challenges. The growth of hard floor washers overseas bodes well for Ecovacs' overall business. Even the previously underperforming stir-fry pots have shown promising sales, reaching over 100,000 units annually.

Ecovacs is self-developing lawn mowers and pool cleaners, with annual sales in the tens of thousands. The company is also investing in new pool cleaner ventures, which will take some time to yield results. Ecovacs’ strengths lie in its robust market presence in robotic vacuums and hard floor washers, but it faces stiff competition for higher sales targets. The company needs top-tier talent to compete with major players.

## Dreame: A Phenomenon in the Industry

Dreame has been a phenomenon in the industry over the years, generating RMB 10 billion in revenue in 2024 from mainly cleaning appliances. Although not listed, its profitability heavily depends on Dreame’s performance. If Dreame intensifies price competition and marketing efforts, it will force all competitors to follow suit, diluting their profits.

Dreame has seen significant growth in robotic vacuums, with lawn mowers hitting over 100,000 units last year and aiming for 200,000 this year. The intense competition in the cleaning appliance market is largely due to Dreame’s influence. However, industry professionals should thank Dreame for driving up salaries.

Dreame has a solid foundation in its core business but faces challenges. From zero to RMB 10 billion in just five years, it created many miracles. But growth cannot be sustained indefinitely; Dreame needs to adapt to slower growth periods and prepare for the next wave of opportunities.

## Narwal: A Resurrection

Narwal was a company I followed closely during its crowdfunding phase, writing numerous articles about them. The team has experienced many ups and downs but remains resilient. In 2023, they achieved nearly RMB 3 billion in revenue, breaking even. This is an impressive feat given the competition from Roborock and Ecovacs, as well as the intense spending by Dreame.

Narwal’s contributions to the robotic vacuum industry are significant. Without their self-cleaning mopping robot with a clean water tank, the industry would not be as prosperous today.

After overcoming numerous challenges, Narwal has started catching up with industry trends in 2023 and even outperforming others in some areas. Their overseas market is also expanding. With steady growth, Narwal aims to reach RMB 5 billion in sales.

However, compared to the leading companies, Narwal remains vulnerable and needs a more stable foundation before pursuing further expansion. There are rumors that Narwal is preparing for an IPO; I wish them all the best with their plans.

## Xiaomi

Xiaomi's robot vacuum currently operates at a scale of 1-20 million units per year. Early on, companies like Roborock, Dreame, and Picea relied on Xiaomi’s channels and supply chains for growth. However, as these companies matured, they gradually distanced themselves from Xiaomi. This is not just an issue with robot vacuums; it's a broader challenge for the entire Xiaomi ecosystem. Early successful companies within the Xiaomi ecosystem have largely severed ties with Xiaomi.

How Xiaomi's ecosystem department handles its relationships with these companies will determine the future development of Xiaomi’s core business. In the future, Xiaomi may adopt a model similar to that used in major appliances, fully self-manufacturing all products. Currently, Xiaomi has seen significant success in major appliances and aims to become the third-largest player (behind Haier and Midea) nationwide. Recent job postings in Suzhou suggest that Xiaomi is committed to establishing its own base of operations in industry centers rather than relying on Beijing. With the establishment of self-built factories, Xiaomi may soon compete head-to-head with Roborock, Dreame, Ecovacs, and other companies.

Xiaomi's strength lies in its global channels, which are formidable. The company can leverage these to secure partnerships that others cannot. Additionally, Xiaomi enjoys a strong brand reputation overseas, with high consumer trust.

However, Xiaomi has been overly reliant on Roborock, Dreame, and Picea for R&D capabilities. If Xiaomi decides to self-manufacture, it will need time to catch up. Once these foundational gaps are addressed, the global channel push could be formidable.

## DJI

DJI is a marvel in the tech industry, selling over 70 billion yuan worth of drones annually and holding a 70% share of the consumer drone market. No one has challenged its dominance. DJI's robot vacuum cleaner was rumored for four years before finally being announced on August 6th.

The first product seems impressive to others but only average for DJI. For leading companies, this product offers no significant advantage. Supply chain reports indicate that DJI orders 200,000 units annually, with the initial order being around 10,000 units.

From a current perspective, it may be difficult to challenge the market leaders, but continuous iterations over two to three years could still provide opportunities. The only question is how much commitment DJI has to this project. If DJI believes that robot vacuum cleaners could potentially create another DJI in five years, its investment and resolve will likely concern and intimidate all competitors.

## Anker Eufy

Anker Eufy has been a major player in overseas robotic cleaning appliances, selling over 1 million units annually at one point. Recently, I reviewed the entire Anker cleaning appliance product line. Early on, Anker's best-selling models were cheaper options; however, domestic advancements in robot vacuums have outpaced them. Their situation abroad resembles that of iRobot, with declining market share.

Last year, Anker launched new products for its robot vacuums, showing a more aggressive approach to product development. This indicates significant resolve from the company. For the domestic market, Anker is unlikely to enter due to intense competition and profitability challenges.

In the overseas market, major Chinese companies are increasingly expanding their presence, with DJI also entering the fray. Anker's response to these developments will be a focal point for future attention.

## Midea and Haier

Midea and Haier are major appliance giants, each generating billions in sales annually. Many people often speculate that if these companies enter the market, no one else would have much of a chance. However, reality is different.

Fang Hongbo from Midea has acknowledged this issue. Due to rapid technological changes in the industry, large companies like Midea and Haier need time to perceive and make decisions. Once product technology stabilizes, these companies can leverage their advantages in pricing and channels.

The optimal strategy for now is for Midea and Haier to stay involved until the technology stabilizes, which I believe will be around 2027-2029. By then, the industry's product technology route should be unified, significantly increasing market capacity (to 40 million units). At that point, products will no longer be a competitive advantage; instead, channels will become crucial. Currently, Midea and Haier are the two strongest in global channels, potentially creating another growth curve beyond their existing major appliance business.

## iRobot and Picea

Prior to 2019, iRobot was the industry's dominant player, with no competition. Picea, on the other hand, was an unknown entity. Picea leveraged its LiDAR navigation technology and partnerships with large companies like Xiaomi, while iRobot stuck with visual navigation, leading to market share erosion.

Currently, iRobot’s valuation is around 100 million US dollars, and there are ongoing rumors of a potential acquisition. However, neither company expected the current situation where Picea now manufactures iRobot's products due to cost issues. This has given iRobot some competitive pricing in the market.

Picea plans to produce 6 million units this year, leading the pack in the robot vacuum manufacturing industry. Previously, its autonomous brand 3i products were too aggressive and did not sell well, with annual sales of a few thousand units. Now, it is rumored that Picea will act as an iRobot agent in China, launching two models: one priced around 2000 yuan with a LiDAR navigation all-in-one dock, and another priced around 4000 yuan with a tracked hard floor washer.

## Potential Mergers and Acquisitions in the Cleaning Robot Market

It seems that Picea is also considering a path similar to what Xinbao took with Moofos—first attempting it domestically before potentially acquiring international brands if successful. If not for political factors, an acquisition of iRobot by Picea would be a good choice. iRobot's channels, patents, and brand are valuable assets for Picea, while Picea’s R&D and supply chain capabilities could also benefit iRobot. A combination of these two companies might have some impact on the leading players.

However, due to political factors, such a merger is unlikely. Yet, who knows what unconventional paths might emerge? Anything is possible with black swan events.

## Other Entrants

Currently, other companies entering the robot vacuum market seem to be doing so purely out of desperation. However, one category of companies has potential: lawn mowing robots and robotic pool cleaners. Robot vacuums, lawn mowers, and pool cleaners share significant similarities in talent structure and sales strategies. Moreover, the ceiling for lawn and pool robots is relatively clear, meaning that once these companies reach a certain scale, they will inevitably seek new growth opportunities.

Similarly, robot vacuum companies are increasingly entering the lawn and pool robot markets. The boundaries between them have become blurred.

I believe several companies could potentially make an impact in the robot vacuum industry:

- **Mammotion**: Mammotion is a subsidiary of Songling Robotics. It has been ramping up its production significantly, with plans to produce 40-50,000 units this year. There are rumors that Songling is already planning to enter the home robot vacuum market, but it remains unclear whether these will fall under Mammotion or another entity.

- **Ninebot**: Ninebot was once part of Xiaomi’s ecosystem and has been rapidly growing with its two-wheeled products. In 2024, Ninebot's sales reached RMB 14.196 billion, including RMB 7.211 billion from electric two-wheelers, RMB 3.381 billion from balance scooters and skateboards, RMB 0.976 billion from all-terrain vehicles, and RMB 0.895 billion from robot business. In an interview with Ninebot's founder Wang Ye, he mentioned that Ninebot was originally a robotics company that just happened to enter the balance scooter market due to regulatory restrictions. He also stated that they will not be absent in embodied intelligence in the future. Currently, Ninebot is among the leading companies in robotic lawn mowing, with plans to produce around 250,000 units this year. Entering the robot vacuum business seems inevitable for them.

- **Aiper Intelligent**: Aiper has been making significant strides in the pool cleaner market and is now one of the top two global brands. Recently, it secured several rounds of funding. It offers a wide range of products from high-end to low-end in the pool cleaning segment. Now, they have also launched lawn mowing robots. Facing competition from Ecovacs, Roborock, Dreame, and others entering the pool cleaner market, Aiper's entry into robot vacuum is only a matter of time.

- **Beatbot Innovation**: Founded by former joint founder Wang Shiluo of Dreame, Beatbot Innovation released its first robotic pool cleaner last year, which sold 500 million yuan in its first year and became the top brand in high-end pool cleaners. They have secured additional funding rounds and are currently on a strong growth trajectory. If trends continue to merge, Aiper's entry into robot vacuum is also likely.
