Recently, I did a video discussing how Laifen could enter the hard floor washer market and which product it should focus on. Then, I noticed that Mr. Ye from Laifen liked my post and followed me on Xiaohongshu.

![Laifen hard floor washer market entry](/images/insights/laifen-hard-floor-washer-chances-image-01.jpg)

Mr. Ye is an exemplary product manager with personal charisma. Before the rapid growth of high-speed hair dryers in China, Laifen leveraged its ultra-low prices and keen insight to launch extensive ad campaigns on Bilibili, quickly achieving sales of several hundred thousand units.

However, after the success of high-speed hair dryers, Laifen did not continue down that path. Instead, it shifted focus to other products like vibration-powered toothbrushes and electric shavers.

In the field of vibration-powered toothbrushes, Laifen innovatively developed a combined brushing-vibration toothbrush, which became a standard feature in premium toothbrush lines from various brands. Additionally, Laifen adopted Apple-like one-piece molding technology for these toothbrushes.

For electric shavers, Laifen introduced high-speed linear motors, offering better performance and lower noise compared to traditional motors.

Both the vibration-powered toothbrush and the electric shaver showcased Mr. Ye's personal style and preferences. From a commercial standpoint, neither product met expectations. The vibration-powered toothbrush was too complex in terms of manufacturing, leading to higher costs but not significantly boosting sales; competitors with lower costs outperformed it in market share.

During the electric shaver phase, due to prolonged development cycles, Laifen's new products faced competition from similar designs launched earlier by rivals at lower costs. This led to accusations of copying upon release.

Furthermore, without updates on high-speed hair dryers, Laifen lost ground in that category to competitors. Many friends suggested that if Laifen had continued investing heavily in high-speed hair dryers and established itself as a brand synonymous with this product, it might be in better shape now.

Laifen's early success story mirrored many first-hit entrepreneurs—relying on favorable timing, resources, and market conditions to achieve initial success. However, subsequent products did not replicate the same level of success, leading to significant disappointment and self-doubt for some.

In fact, most product successes are largely due to chance factors. Improving the likelihood of new product success may be a more critical step in a company's maturation process.

Recently, my supply chain contacts informed me that Laifen is now sending samples, indicating that entering the hard floor washer market was not just a rumor.

Can Laifen succeed in the hard floor washer market?

If it were last year, I would have said there was little chance due to intense competition from brands like Dreame, Tineco, and Roborock. This year's competitive landscape is different with Narwal and Uwant also making waves.

For Laifen, if its target sales are similar to those of Narwal, it could achieve 20-250,000 units annually at an average price of $2,000, generating revenues of around $40-50 million. For a new brand, reaching this scale would be impressive.

Mr. Ye is passionate about product development. If he aims to simply sell products, given Laifen's strong presence on platforms like Bilibili and Xiaohongshu, selling 200,000 units annually seems highly feasible. One can look at Uwant’s success with its hard floor washers, which, despite average product features, achieved sales of hundreds of thousands through robust marketing.

However, if Laifen becomes too fixated on product details, the costs associated with materials and manufacturing could spiral out of control, posing significant challenges in a highly competitive market for cleaning appliances.

If Laifen decides to enter the hard floor washer market, here are some suggestions:

1. Entering this market is a long-term endeavor; do not expect quick results. After several years of development, only those who can endure will remain. Given Laifen's current overall strength and single-product investment compared to industry leaders, prepare for a prolonged battle.

2. The top players in the hard floor washer market are different from those Laifen faced with hair dryers, toothbrushes, and shavers. Former competitors were mostly foreign brands that seemed large but acted slowly. Now, the giants are formidable, both in capability and reaction speed, making them faster than startups.

3. Although Laifen has a substantial business scale, its company strength and resources dedicated to hard floor washers still lag behind industry leaders. Engaging in marketing battles with these companies could be risky.

4. Opportunities persist in the market. Observe how Narwal's high-end hard floor washers compete and Uwant’s success in mid-to-low-end products. For Laifen, a favorable price range might be between $1,000 to $2,000, where competition is fierce but essential for survival.

5. So far, almost every conceivable form of hard floor washer has been developed, making it difficult for me to imagine what Levenhulme could innovate next. Even if Levenhulme does introduce some innovation, its advantage might only last 3-6 months before the major players catch up.

If there is any company that can bring fresh air to this industry, Levenhulme might be one of them.
