![Article image 1](/images/insights/sharkninja-road-to-ten-billion-dollars-image-01.jpg)
In the small household appliances industry, if you don't know SharkNinja, it's likely that you haven't entered the field yet.
As a company I deeply admire and appreciate, I am particularly impressed by Shark's continuous self-improvement. My earliest interaction with SharkNinja was around 2012 when their company name was Europro. Later, due to the success of their brand, they changed their company name to match their brand.

In 2018, the entire brand was acquired by Ninebot for $1 billion. This acquisition was reported by me and made a significant impact on my career in the industry. Previously, I produced a video on this topic; interested readers can watch it.
SN went public on NASDAQ in 2022. In 2022, its sales reached $3.7 billion, which grew to $5.5 billion by 2024. This growth is truly staggering.

![Article image 2](/images/insights/sharkninja-road-to-ten-billion-dollars-image-02.jpg)
Next, let's look at where SharkNinja's growth comes from and why they were able to achieve it. First, a brief overview of some key points about SharkNinja: In 2024, SN achieved $5.5 billion in sales, with Shark contributing $2.6 billion and Ninja contributing $2.9 billion. Comparing the data from 2022, Shark's sales were $2 billion, while Ninja's were $1.7 billion. The main growth points over these years have been in Ninja, which saw a $1.2 billion increase.

In terms of the market, they have aggressively promoted internationalization. International markets contributed $1.7 billion in sales. In the UK and Japan, Shark's market share is very high.
Their global supply chain is also very strong; they began their global supply chain layout at the onset of trade wars. Currently, they have manufacturing bases in various countries including Southeast Asia.

. SN's supply chain team is personally the strongest I have seen. Once a team sets its goals, it will do whatever it takes to achieve them. In normal companies, making 3-5 prototypes during new product development is already considered significant investment. Previously, some friends mentioned that Shark was producing 100 hand-built prototypes of their vacuum cleaners and sending them to the United States for consumer use to collect feedback. The cost involved in this process is extremely high.

SN's growth in cleaning products is not substantial, but there are a few opportunities that Shark has yet to seize and capitalize on. One is the wet-dry vac category; currently, Shark's sales in this area are minimal. Another is the robotic vacuum with a base station, which also needs improvement.

I believe another significant growth opportunity lies in Shark's corded upright vacuums once they transition to lithium-ion batteries. This will bring about substantial growth. Therefore, how Shark leads in this aspect in the future is something we, as professionals in cleaning appliances, are closely watching.

The Ninja category has seen significant growth primarily due to the entry into several new categories:
- Coffee machines
- Outdoor appliances
- Personal care appliances, such as hair dryers and mask devices.

For example, hair dryers that were launched just one year ago have already achieved sales of millions of units, which is extremely impressive.

Why has it achieved these results?
—- Shark's self-summary

1. Disruptive Innovation
They have over 1,000 engineers and designers globally constantly thinking of various innovations. Teams in the United States, the UK, and China communicate 24/7, with the latest ideas tested and implemented quickly. They hold more than 5,200 patents worldwide. To be honest, Dyson has not been as innovative in recent years; only Shark continues to have continuous innovation. Everyone is watching Shark's new products.

2. Global Agile Supply Chain
The global supply chain is now fully established, with suppliers in China, Thailand, Vietnam, Singapore, and Malaysia. Core products have 2-3 suppliers who are simultaneously prototyping and shipping. An agile supply chain can quickly adapt to market changes. This means that even if external factors change, they can adjust their supply chains accordingly. For example, SN can switch between these six countries to ensure tariffs do not exceed those of competitors.

The benefit of having 2-3 suppliers for simultaneous prototyping is that it prevents missing the order surge due to production bottlenecks and allows continuous price negotiation to maintain competitive procurement costs.

3. 360-Degree Marketing
Shark's marketing capabilities have been strong since 2009. Early products were benchmarked against Dyson, ensuring superior parameters while offering prices that were only half as much.

This strategy has been incredibly successful, successfully binding Shark and Dyson together. It's somewhat similar to what Xiaomi does in China. Various social media marketing efforts will be a piece of cake after this.

Integrated Channels
SN summarized their marketing and sales strategies as an integrated channel strategy.
Traditionally, when we go to physical stores in the offline mall, we only buy if we see something that sparks our interest. SN's approach is different; they create demand and complete the demand cycle through offline stores.
A simple example: They create various usage scenarios on TV, social media, live streaming, etc., and consumers make purchases at their offline stores or channels. This is similar to how we use platforms like Xiaohongshu and Douyin for inspiration and then buy on Taobao or JD.com. However, their reach in creating interest and making sales is broader.

They have identified three key growth points:
1. Increasing in existing scenarios
2. Expanding into more emerging product lines
3. Entering more countries

The above information is from their official sources, and I will share my personal views as well.
In the small appliance industry, I believe SN's development capabilities and supply chain are among the best.
Over these past ten years, they have continuously launched various impressive products. Among the companies I know, SN has the strongest execution; once they set a timeline, they spare no effort to work with suppliers to achieve it. Moreover, this capability has now become part of their company system, allowing them to replicate it across other industries. The rapid growth of the Ninja product line in recent years is a manifestation of this system. In the future, they can enter more markets and achieve even greater growth. Personally, I feel that within 5-8 years, SN could potentially become a company with a billion-dollar sales volume.

Currently, the core of SN remains their CEO, Mark Barracos.
Mark B has been a long-time partner with Mark R, and after Mark R left, Mark B took on the leadership role. He has proven his ability to elevate the company to new heights. Moreover, he is in his prime and can still lead for at least another 20 years.

Beyond the company culture, the core leadership is crucial.
In terms of how SN treats suppliers and employees, it's worth learning from. SN has always offered some of the highest employee benefits in the industry, so even when there are overtime requirements, everyone is generally not resentful. Regarding suppliers, SN’s order profits should be relatively high, and they pay for supplier development if requested. Large volumes and high margins make them highly sought-after clients.

Typical companies can make their customers profitable; great companies also ensure that both employees and suppliers benefit. Companies like Huawei, Apple, Dyson, etc., are examples of this.
