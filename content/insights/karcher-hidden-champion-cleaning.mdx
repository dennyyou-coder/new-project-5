American management master Herman Simon wrote a book titled "Hidden Champions—Who Are the Best Companies in the World?" where he introduced the concept of "hidden champions." The term subsequently became globally popular.

![Original image 1](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-001.jpg)

In this book, the author presents the criteria forhidden champion companies:

* A market share of either first or second place in the world market, or first place in the European market, or at least leading in the market

* Annual sales not exceeding $1 billion (with the exception of a few cases; approximately 4.4% of the companies cited have annual sales over $1 billion)

* Little known to the public, with low social recognition.

At the same time, the book summarizes some lessons learned from thesehidden champion companies:

* Set clear and ambitious goals

* Market focus should be narrow but meet customer needs and achieve high-tech standards

* Combine a narrow market focus with global targets

* Establish close relationships with customers to ensure direct contact

* Strive for continuous invention and innovation in products and processing technologies

* Create distinct competitive advantages in products and services

* Rely on one's own strength to maintain core company capabilities

* Aim for more work rather than more leaders, emphasizing the potential of employees

* Leaders should be fully involved in fundamental issues but participate in specific tasks, with a strong emphasis on selecting leaders


The book includes many examples. One section discusses the German company Kärcher.

> Kärcher is located in the town of Winnenden in Baden-Württemberg, Germany, approximately 20 kilometers from the southwestern industrial city of Stuttgart. In this small town with a population of only about 20,000 people, Kärcher has steadily grown since its establishment in 1937. By fiscal year 2015, sales exceeded €2.22 billion (approximately RMB 16.54 billion), with the sale of 12.87 million machines, accounting for 70% of global market share.
> Kärcher initially focused on producing heating technology products. In 1950, it developed Europe's first hot water pressure washer and entered the cleaning industry. From 1974 onwards, it began to focus heavily on high-pressure cleaning equipment. Although later diversification led to product expansion into indoor cleaning, garden cleaning, and other non-professional end consumer markets, high-pressure cleaning equipment and services remained Kärcher's core business.

Kärcher was founded by Alfred Karcher in 1935. Initially, Kärcher was a water treatment company. In 1950, it began producing high-pressure cleaning tools and invented Europe's first hot water high-pressure washer (DS 350 steam jet cleaner) in the same year. In 1959, at the age of 58, Alfred Karcher passed away, after which his wife Irene Karcher took over the company for 30 years.

In 1974, after attempting diversification, Kärcher focused its efforts on high-pressure cleaning equipment and changed its product color from blue to the famous Kärcher yellow.

In 1984, Kärcher entered the consumer market with its first portable high-pressure washer.

![Original image 2](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-002.jpg)

In 2001, we began our water treatment business, and in 2009, we expanded into garden care. By 2013, Kärcher had changed the color of its professional cleaning products from yellow to gray. To date, we have over 3000 product offerings.

![Original image 3](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-003.jpg)

Up to this point, Kärcher remains undisputedly the king in the high-pressure cleaner domain.

![Original image 4](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-004.jpg)

Currently, the product line is divided into two categories: personal/home use and professional products.

Home-use products include pressure washers, vacuum cleaners, robotic floor sweepers, steam cleaning devices, etc. Notably, Kärcher borrowed a feature from a robotic floor cleaner (automatically transferring debris from the cleaner to the charging dock) that Ecovacs' new product line iRobot introduced several years ago. A previous product, Window Vacs, was also widely imitated by many manufacturers. Currently, Kärcher's core business remains pressure washers.

![Original image 5](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-005.jpg)

Professional products include various commercial vacuum cleaners, cleaning machines, and cleaning equipment.

![Original image 6](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-006.jpg)

To date, Kärcher has sold products to over 140 countries worldwide. Currently, 85% of Kärcher's sales revenue is contributed by overseas companies. In the 1990s, Kärcher entered the Chinese market and established a sales subsidiary in Shanghai. A production company was also set up in Changshu, Jiangsu.

![Original image 7](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-007.jpg)

Kärcher basically covers most of the channels including DIY supermarkets, department stores, TV shopping, grocery stores, and e-commerce platforms.

![Original image 8](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-008.jpg)

As of 2017, Kärcher's sales amounted to 2.501 billion euros. However, the graph below shows that Kärcher's sales have increased from 200 million euros in 1985 to the current figure of 2.5 billion euros.

![Original image 9](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-009.jpg)

Since the establishment of its first subsidiary in France by Kärcher in 1962, the company has expanded to over 110 subsidiaries in 67 countries worldwide. The company employs more than 12,304 individuals, including over 900 research and development engineers, who dedicate 6% of annual sales revenue to R&D each year.

![Original image 10](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-010.jpg)

Kärcher has over 1,740 patents. The company is also constantly innovating, with an interesting data point: 90% of Kärcher's product lines are less than five years old.

![Original image 11](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-011.jpg)

Kärcher has adopted an interesting strategy in marketing by cleaning world-famous landmarks.

Notable examples include the Great Sphinx of Egypt, Christ the Redeemer in Rio de Janeiro, the Statue of Liberty, and statues of U.S. presidents, among others.

![Original image 12](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-012.jpg)

Currently, while Kärcher pressure washers still hold the strongest market position, they are facing strong competition from the following:

- Nilfisk

- Annovi Reverberi (AR)

- Stihl

- Briggs&Stratton

- BOSCH

- TTI

- Generac

- Stanley

- Makita

- FNA Group

- Lavorwash

- Sun Joe

Among these, Nilfisk from Denmark and Annovi Reverberi (AR) from Italy are considered the largest competitors in the pressure washer domain.

Additionally, a large group of Chinese contract manufacturers (Yilì, Anlu, Lüetian) are chasing Kärcher in the mid-to-low-end market. These companies have put significant pressure on Kärcher to the extent that Kärcher has begun using recycled materials (although the official reason given is for environmental protection).

![Original image 13](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-013.jpg)

But sometimes disruptive threats may come from unknown opponents.

Bosch, a major power tool manufacturer, launched a lithium-ion battery-powered handheld pressure washer. It is said that the product sold ten thousand units in its first year.

![Original image 14](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-014.jpg)

Thus, the wirelessization of pressure washers has become a popular choice for new entrants. Prior to this, Kingclean Electrical had attempted to develop pressure washers but was not particularly successful. This time, Kingclean also seized the opportunity to launch a wireless lithium-ion handheld pressure washer aimed at re-entering this market. To my knowledge, only a few other companies are currently working on wireless handheld pressure washers, but Vorwerk does not appear to have this business segment. In the competition for wireless pressure washers, Vorwerk does not hold an advantage.

![Original image 15](/images/insights/karcher-hidden-champion-cleaning-image-015.jpg)

Will wireless pressure washers replace corded ones, this is a critical question that Kärcher faces. Given the trend in power tools and vacuum cleaners towards wireless technology, it seems inevitable. Once fully wireless technology is adopted, Kärcher's technical advantages in corded pressure washers will be negligible.
