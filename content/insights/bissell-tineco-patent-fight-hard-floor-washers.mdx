Recently, I published information about Bissell initiating a Section 337 investigation against Tineco in the United States. This event has sparked considerable interest and discussion within the cleaning appliance industry. Many friends have asked me about the development of this situation.

Firstly, the matter is complex because the patent systems between China and the U.S. are not entirely identical. A patent that may be valid in China could potentially face issues in the U.S.

I consulted with a patent expert on this matter and have provided detailed information on these patents along with the expert's interpretation.

**U.S. Patent Number US11076735 (Patent 735), its core claim is:**

wherein the surface cleaning apparatus comprises a battery charging circuit controlling the recharging of the rechargeable battery, wherein the battery charging circuit is disabled by the actuation of the self-cleaning mode input control and remains disabled during the unattended automatic cleanout cycle.

Among which, the surface cleaning device includes a battery charging circuit for controlling the recharging of the rechargeable battery. The battery charging circuit is disabled upon activation of the self-cleaning mode input control and remains disabled throughout the unattended automatic cleaning cycle.

**United States Patent No. 11,071,428 (Patent 428), Claim excerpt:**

wherein, to execute the unattended automatic cleanout cycle for the self-cleaning mode of operation, the controller is configured to: power the brushroll motor and the pump by the battery, whereby cleaning liquid is sprayed on the brushroll while the brushroll rotates, without the vacuum motor being powered; and power the vacuum motor by the battery after the brushroll motor and the pump are powered, whereby cleaning liquid is extracted and deposited into the recovery tank and a portion of the recovery pathway is flushed out; and wherein the battery charging circuit is disabled by the actuation of the self-cleaning mode input control and remains disabled during the unattended automatic cleanout cycle.

Among these steps, to execute the unattended automatic cleaning cycle in the self-cleaning operation mode, the controller is configured such that: the brushroll motor and pump are powered by the battery, thereby spraying cleaning liquid onto the rotating brushroll without powering the vacuum motor; subsequently, after powering the brushroll motor and pump, the vacuum motor is powered by the battery to extract and deposit cleaning liquid into the recovery tank and flush a portion of the recovery pathway; and wherein the battery charging circuit is disabled upon activation of the self-cleaning mode input control and remains disabled throughout the unattended automatic cleaning cycle.
