**Aiper is generally the stronger starting point for buyers who want a broad cordless product ladder, access through major authorized retailers and a defined dealer return workflow. Beatbot is generally the stronger starting point for buyers who want high-automation flagships, selected surface-plus-underwater systems and replacement-based support on eligible products.**

There is no brand-wide winner. Aiper and Beatbot each sell several product families, and their current ranges change quickly. A fair decision starts with the pool and exact SKU, then checks the authorized seller, service route, freight, commercial-use terms and written warranty.

World Clean Biz has not run matched laboratory tests on the two ranges. Manufacturer specifications can establish product scope, but they cannot prove which brand cleans better, lasts longer or produces fewer returns.

## One Search Query, Two Different Decisions

### For a pool owner

Start with the pool: in-ground or above-ground, length, surface, slopes, steps, sun shelf, waterline, debris and cleaning frequency. Then compare exact products for coverage, filtration, retrieval, charging, app dependence, lifting weight and local support.

An Aiper Scuba X1 and a Beatbot AquaSense 2 can form a useful residential cordless comparison. They are not proxies for their entire brands. Moving to a Scuba X1 Pro Max, Sora 70 or AquaSense X changes the cleaning scope, charging system and service economics.

### For a distributor, dealer or pool service company

Start with the operating system behind the sale. Aiper provides a broad authorized-retail footprint, distributor registration, an authorized direct-dealer program and batch-return requests. Beatbot combines direct channels, dealer recruitment, offline expansion and different repair or replacement routes across its portfolio.

The commercial questions are margin after returns, channel conflict, inventory protection, failure routing, freight, replacement grade, spare parts, app support and the entity that signs the warranty.

## Who Owns Aiper and Beatbot?

Aiper’s ownership changed in December 2025. Fluidra completed an approximately $100 million investment through new shares and obtained a [27% interest in Aiper](https://www.fluidra.com/press-room/press-release/fluidra-completes-an-initial-27-investment-in-aiper/). Fluidra calls itself a significant minority owner and retains a possible path to a future majority stake.

The completed transaction did not make Aiper a wholly owned Fluidra brand. Spain’s [CNMC case page](https://www.cnmc.es/expedientes/c161625) classifies the transaction as joint control of Aiper Inc. by Fluidra Commercial and W&D United. Economic ownership and control therefore need separate descriptions: Fluidra holds 27%, while the regulatory control structure is joint.

Beatbot’s public structure is different. Its [European contact page](https://eu.beatbot.com/pages/contact-us) describes Beatbot as a technology brand operated by **Xingmai Innovation Technology (Suzhou) Co., Ltd.** Public sources identify Siler Wang as founder and connect Xingmai and related regional entities to trademarks, apps, product documents and regulatory roles.

Reviewed sources do not provide a complete current Xingmai cap table. Financing participants should not be labeled Beatbot’s parent company without current ownership and voting evidence.

## How Do the 2026 Product Platforms Differ?

Aiper’s current U.S. catalog presents a wide cordless ladder. It includes Scuba V3 and V3 Ultra, Scuba S3, the Scuba X1 family, Scuba S1 products, above-ground models and handheld vacuums. The brand also sells EcoSurfer surface skimmers, HydroComm communication and monitoring devices, and IrriSense smart irrigation products.

That architecture gives a retailer several price and feature steps under one smart-yard story. It also makes model names and generations important. A Scuba X1 does not inherit every function of the X1 Pro Max, V3 Ultra or a separate EcoSurfer.

Beatbot organizes its current platform around Sora, AquaSense, iSkim and selected retail editions. The [Sora 70](https://beatbot.com/products/sora-70) is specified for the water surface, floor, walls, waterline and qualifying shallow platforms. Sora 30 and Sora 10 occupy lower tiers and should not inherit Sora 70’s full feature set.

The AquaSense family covers a separate automation ladder. The flagship [AquaSense X](https://beatbot.com/products/aquasense-x) adds an automatic filter-cleaning station, a 22-liter external debris system, mapping, surface and underwater cleaning, and water clarification. Its station is specific to AquaSense X rather than a universal Beatbot accessory.

Both brands are therefore cordless-first, but their assortment logic differs:

- Aiper emphasizes a broad product ladder, pool-type segmentation and connected-yard expansion.
- Beatbot emphasizes high-feature systems, selected multi-zone cleaning and premium automation.
- Both also sell separate surface robots, so “waterline,” “water surface” and “water clarification” must remain separate functions.

## Which Aiper and Beatbot Models Should Be Compared?

There is no single correct model pair.

**Scuba X1 versus AquaSense 2** is one useful search pair. Both are cordless residential robots that publish floor, wall and waterline functions. Aiper highlights suction, a large debris basket and replaceable fine filtration. Beatbot highlights its navigation, charging, retrieval and support model. Those manufacturer claims still require matched testing before becoming a performance verdict.

**Scuba X1 Pro Max versus Sora 70 or selected AquaSense products** enters the surface-plus-underwater tier. Scuba X1 Pro Max combines multiple pool zones with optional HydroComm functions and a wireless dock. Sora 70 combines surface and underwater coverage with shallow-platform capability. Selected AquaSense products add clarification or different automation layers.

**AquaSense X** belongs in a separate workflow comparison because its automatic filter-cleaning station changes maintenance and storage. Pairing it with an entry Scuba model merely because temporary prices overlap would distort the decision.

New 2026 Aiper products such as Scuba V3 and Experts Duo should only enter exact tables after their final regional availability, included hardware and warranty are confirmed. Preorders and launch claims are not stable procurement baselines.

## Which Brand Has the Stronger Channel System?

Aiper has the clearer public map of large authorized retailers. Its U.S. [authorized-sales page](https://aiper.com/us/authorized-sales-channels) lists the official store alongside Amazon, Walmart, Lowe’s, Leslie’s, Home Depot, Best Buy and other channels. The page also warns that unauthorized or diverted sales are outside Aiper’s warranty coverage.

Aiper’s [distributor and dealer page](https://aiper.com/us/distributors-dealers) adds three B2B routes: distributor registration, the Aiper Authorized Direct Dealer program and an RGA form for distributor or dealer batch returns. This is useful evidence of a formal trade workflow. It does not prove that every registered dealer repairs products locally or receives identical commercial terms.

Beatbot is not an online-only brand. It recruits dealers and drop-shipping partners, while its [store page](https://beatbot.com/pages/find-a-store) says the company is “on the way to 1000+ stores.” That is a company target or marketing statement, not an audited count of active repair centers.

For both brands, channel due diligence needs more than a logo on a retailer page:

- Is the seller authorized for this country, channel and model?
- Does authorization include demonstration, installation, repair or only resale?
- Who owns customer returns and marketplace chargebacks?
- Are direct-store discounts protected by MAP or channel rules?
- Does the distributor hold replacement units and parts locally?

Fluidra’s 27% investment may create future professional-channel opportunities for Aiper, but it does not automatically make Fluidra the distributor, importer or warranty provider for every current Aiper order.

## Repair, Replacement and Dealer Returns

Aiper’s U.S. support system directs customers through repair and warranty applications. Its trade page separately provides an RGA path for distributor or dealer batch returns. A professional buyer should ask whether a failure is diagnosed locally, shipped to a central facility, replaced from distributor stock or credited through the RGA process.

Beatbot’s standard warranty allows repair or replacement after diagnosis. A separate [Full Replacement Protection](https://beatbot.com/pages/replacement-only-policy-1) covers named AquaSense and AquaSense 2 products and iSkim Ultra under specified geographic and direct-purchase conditions. It does not apply to every Beatbot model, seller or country.

Replacement can reduce consumer downtime when the unit is eligible and inventory is available. At distribution scale, it creates additional operating questions: inbound freight, returned batteries, refurbished units, failure analysis, replacement stock and what happens after the named program ends.

Repair and replacement are service models, not measures of product quality. The better program is the one that produces a predictable cost and turnaround for the intended market.

## How Do Aiper and Beatbot Warranties Compare?

Neither brand has one global warranty number.

Aiper’s current [U.S. warranty page](https://aiper.com/us/refund-and-warranty) lists model terms from 12 to 36 months. Scuba X1 is listed at 24 months and Scuba X1 Pro Max at 36 months. Accessories and consumables have shorter periods. Coverage is restricted to the country of receipt and requires proof of purchase from the official store or an authorized channel.

Beatbot’s current [limited warranty](https://beatbot.com/pages/warranty-policy) lists 24- or 36-month main-body terms for named products in the United States, Canada, the European Union, Spain and Australia. It requires proof of purchase, a serial number and service within the area where the product was purchased. Its standard remedy can be repair or replacement.

The two headline numbers conceal the costs that matter to a channel:

1. Who pays inbound and outbound freight?
2. Is the replacement new, refurbished or repaired?
3. How long is the turnaround target?
4. Which batteries, chargers, docks, filters and brushes are covered?
5. Does a replacement restart the warranty or inherit the remaining term?
6. Does the seller or brand handle the claim?

A three-year flagship warranty cannot be copied to an entry model, and a genuine gray-market unit may still lack an executable local warranty.

## Can Aiper or Beatbot Be Used in Commercial Pools?

Do not infer commercial suitability from suction, runtime or a high retail price.

Beatbot’s public warranty expressly excludes commercial, industrial, rental and other non-household use unless Beatbot authorizes it in writing. A commercial buyer should obtain a written use authorization, warranty, service-level agreement and spare-unit plan before purchase.

Aiper’s public U.S. consumer warranty does not create a universal commercial program for every product or country. Hotels, schools, gyms, rental operators and service companies should obtain the same written confirmation: intended duty cycle, warranty eligibility, service location, freight, battery plan and replacement stock.

For intensive-use projects, compare products explicitly sold for commercial duty as a separate class. Residential cordless robots should not be treated as commercial machines merely because they can physically clean the pool.

## Do the Patent Proceedings Change the Comparison?

Public USPTO records show that Xingmai, doing business as Beatbot, filed a post-grant review petition involving an Aiper Global pool-robot patent. That is a real B2B risk-monitoring item, especially for importers and distributors operating in the United States.

It is not evidence that either company owns the other, that a patent is invalid or that a product infringes. Those questions belong to the proceeding and can change over time. A procurement team exposed to IP claims should request current legal representations, indemnity language, affected-SKU lists and territory guidance from its contracting party.

## B2B Procurement Checklist

### Product and application

- Record pool type, dimensions, surface, shape, slopes, steps, sun shelf, waterline and debris.
- Confirm model number, product number, EAN/UPC, revision and included accessories.
- Separate floor, wall, waterline, water-surface, platform and clarification functions.
- Match the battery, charging method, filter and retrieval workflow to the duty cycle.

### Company, channel and compliance

- Identify the trademark owner, legal manufacturer, country of origin and importer of record.
- Confirm the contract seller and authorized status on the transaction date.
- Obtain battery, radio, charger, electrical and product-safety files for the exact SKU.
- Record territory, channel, exclusivity and restrictions on marketplaces or cross-border resale.
- Request current IP representations and indemnity where the agreement creates exposure.

### Service economics

- Map first response, diagnostics, repair, replacement, RGA and return locations.
- Allocate freight, labor, duties and lithium-battery transport.
- Price batteries, chargers, docks, filters, brushes, tracks and other replaceable parts.
- Agree on turnaround, replacement grade, spare-unit stock and end-of-life support.
- Obtain commercial-use authorization for every non-household application.
- Keep the label, manual, invoice, authorization, warranty version and serial record for each batch.

## Can Brand Marketing Decide the Winner?

No. Beatbot publishes an exact Aiper Scuba X1 versus Beatbot AquaSense 2 comparison and identifies itself as a party to the comparison. It is useful for product specifications and evidence that the query exists. Its winner conclusion is not independent testing.

Aiper also publishes performance claims, rankings and product superlatives. These pages describe Aiper’s intended product positioning, but WCB cannot convert them into a universal verdict without matching definitions and controlled tests.

A credible comparison would use the same pool, debris load, cycle, filter preparation and water condition. It would record missed areas, particle capture, surface-debris capture where applicable, retrieval effort, charging time, filter-cleaning labor, faults and actual support response.

Until that evidence exists, “better” should mean better fit for a declared pool, SKU, channel and service obligation.

## FAQ

### Is Aiper better than Beatbot?

Aiper can be the better starting point for buyers who prioritize a broad cordless assortment, major authorized retailers and dealer return workflows. That does not prove every Aiper cleans better or lasts longer than every Beatbot.

### Is Beatbot better than Aiper?

Beatbot can be the better starting point for buyers who prioritize high-automation flagships, selected surface-plus-underwater cleaning and eligible replacement-based support. Compare the exact model and local service terms.

### Is Aiper owned by Fluidra?

Fluidra owns 27% of Aiper and shares control with W&D United after the completed 2025 transaction. Aiper is not a wholly owned Fluidra brand, and a possible future path to majority ownership has not made Fluidra the current majority owner.

### Who owns Beatbot?

Beatbot is operated by Xingmai Innovation Technology (Suzhou) Co., Ltd. Reviewed public sources identify the operator and founder but do not provide a complete current cap table that supports naming an outside parent company.

### Which Aiper is comparable to Beatbot AquaSense 2?

Scuba X1 is one useful cordless residential comparison because both publish floor, wall and waterline functions. Their filtration, charging, retrieval, warranty and service routes still differ. They should not represent the entire brands.

### Which brand offers surface cleaning?

Selected products from both brands do. Aiper Scuba X1 Pro Max publishes surface and underwater functions, while Beatbot Sora 70 and selected AquaSense products publish multi-zone coverage. Aiper EcoSurfer and Beatbot iSkim are separate surface robots. Verify the exact SKU.

### Which brand has the better warranty?

There is no brand-wide answer. Aiper’s U.S. terms currently range from 12 to 36 months by product. Beatbot lists 24 or 36 months for named main bodies in several markets, and its replacement-only protection has narrower eligibility. Compare freight, remedy, exclusions and local execution.

### Can Aiper or Beatbot be used commercially?

Obtain written approval. Beatbot’s public standard warranty expressly excludes non-household use unless authorized in writing. Aiper’s consumer warranty should not be assumed to cover every commercial duty cycle, country or channel.

### Can I import either brand from another country and keep the warranty?

Do not assume so. Aiper restricts coverage to the country of receipt, and Beatbot limits service to the purchase service area. Authorized channel, invoice, serial number, importer and written regional confirmation are decisive.

## Final Answer

Aiper and Beatbot represent two different cordless-first operating systems.

Aiper is the more natural starting point for a broad product ladder, large authorized retailers and formal dealer-return workflows, with Fluidra now participating as a 27% shareholder and joint controller alongside W&D. Beatbot is the more natural starting point for high-automation flagships, selected surface-plus-underwater systems and replacement-based support on eligible products under the Xingmai-operated brand.

The final choice belongs at model and transaction level. Match the pool, verify the SKU and authorized seller, calculate repair or replacement downtime, and obtain the commercial-use and warranty obligations in writing.
