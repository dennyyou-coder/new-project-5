## Front-End Discussion with Kingclean's Chairman on Industry Trends

Recently, I had an in-depth conversation lasting over an hour with Ni Zuguang, the chairman of Kingclean, during their launch of the Mojie M12 Max. In this extended dialogue, Mr. Ni shared valuable insights into the industry’s development and future trends. This article captures his perspectives on the industry, providing a comprehensive view of his understanding.

## Insights from Kingclean's Chairman

During our conversation, Mr. Ni elaborated on several key points regarding the evolution of smart home appliances. He highlighted how advancements in technology, such as LiDAR navigation and SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping), are driving innovation in robotic cleaning devices like robot vacuums and hard floor washers.

He also discussed the competitive landscape, noting that companies like Roborock, Ecovacs, Dreame, Tineco, Narwal, Aiper Intelligent, Beatbot Innovation, WYBOT Technology, Ninebot, Wildland, Husqvarna, Positec, Mammotion, LD Robot, Kingclean itself, Mojie, Deerma, Uwant, Puppy, iLife, Smartmi, Picea, 3i, Laifen, DJI, Insta360, DEWALT, and TTI are all contributing to the growth of this sector.

Mr. Ni emphasized the importance of integrating multiple functionalities into a single device, such as the all-in-one dock for robot vacuums. He also touched on the role of LiDAR in enhancing navigation accuracy and efficiency, making these devices more reliable and user-friendly.

Furthermore, he discussed the challenges faced by companies in maintaining market share amidst increasing competition and rapid technological advancements. Mr. Ni shared that Kingclean is continuously investing in research and development to stay ahead of the curve, ensuring their products remain cutting-edge and meet consumer demands for both functionality and convenience.

Overall, the conversation provided a deep dive into the current state and future directions of smart home cleaning technology, highlighting the strategic insights and forward-thinking approach of Kingclean's leadership.

## Denny's Insight: A Conversation with CEO Ni

Thank you for this opportunity to engage in a face-to-face discussion. As many know, Mr. Ni is a legend in the vacuum cleaner industry. His early career and how he scaled Kingclean are topics of great interest. Today, we will delve into his past experiences to see if they can offer insights to other professionals.

Mr. Ni began his journey in the military for 13 years before joining Chunhua as a technical leader. How did your time in the military influence your subsequent entrepreneurial endeavors?

Mr. Ni: From my personal experience, growing up in rural areas and working on farms from an early age instilled a strong work ethic. The harsh conditions taught me perseverance. In the military, I honed these skills further. Military life is structured; one must be part of an organization to grow. Our revolutionary predecessors were ordinary farmers before joining the revolution, but their experiences propelled them forward.

We often spoke about Mao Zedong's ideology, which transformed both the army and the party. It shaped our minds as common soldiers. The military environment fostered progress and positivity. A saying in the military is "ironclad camps, changing soldiers." Every year, evaluations were conducted; those who performed poorly might be discharged after two years, while top performers could advance to officer ranks.

This required continuous learning and a strong sense of advancement. As new recruits, we eagerly took on dirty and arduous tasks, from morning cleaning to farming. The military instilled in me a sense of dedication and professionalism.

Additionally, the military taught organizational discipline. We followed policies and completed our work accordingly. The principle was "collective first, then individuals; public before private." This ethos permeated my work ethic.

The military also fostered a sense of responsibility. It wasn't just about money; as a soldier, I earned only 6 to 10 yuan per month. In the first year, with a salary of 60 yuan, I saved 50 after spending 10. Over four years, I accumulated nearly 200 yuan. This experience instilled in me a sense of frugality and upward mobility.

I was always an active member, studying Mao Zedong's works and writing extensive notes on them. The military education profoundly influenced my worldview and how I approached problems and people.

In summary, the most significant training came from learning and being educated by the military, shaping my perspective with dialectical materialism and a two-sided approach to problem-solving. These principles have guided me throughout my career.

Back then, civil servants had relatively easy lives, but I foresaw that in 20 or 30 years, things would be different. Among our peers who transitioned from the military, most went into government agencies like customs and industry. However, I chose to join a company.

Upon joining Kingclean, when asked which department I wanted to work in, I said I didn't want an office job; instead, I wanted to go to the factory floor where I could be of greatest use. When told that motor production was lagging, I volunteered for the task and spent two years as an engineer, overseeing every process from scratch.

## Denny's Insight: From Zero to Engineer

Was it truly starting from zero?

Mr. Ni: In the military, I was a transferred officer who served in technical roles for five years before moving into political work. Typically, after transitioning, one would join government agencies like customs or industry. However, with the onset of reform and opening up, I saw the rapid growth of private enterprises in Zhejiang and Shenzhen.

I wanted to learn new skills to avoid being left behind by society. I proposed joining a company for hands-on technical work, becoming China's first motor localization engineer. At that time, several factories had purchased advanced imported equipment but failed to produce functional products. I personally went to the frontlines, conducting extensive trials and validations over three months to resolve the issue and win an award.

Reflecting on this experience, despite not having a university education, I learned through self-study and application. At the factory, I applied my knowledge practically. My strong learning ability allowed me to master technical subjects like motor theory, automatic control, analog circuits, and digital circuits. These skills laid the groundwork for future success.

## Denny's Insight: The Vigor of Mr. Ni

Many have noted that you are exceptionally energetic, arriving at work early in the morning and working late into the night without stopping, especially during your startup years. How do you maintain these habits even as you've grown to manage multiple industries? Is there a connection to your military background?

Mr. Ni: The habit of hard work became second nature after growing up in tough conditions. I don't see it as hardship; rather, it's a natural inclination. If forced for short-term goals like making money, one’s willpower would wane. It is the ideal and passion for my career that sustain me.

During our startup phase, we worked non-stop for ten years, taking only a few days off during the Spring Festival.

## Denny's Insight: Kingclean's Early Success

Was there any significant obstacle in those early years? The company seemed to achieve industry leadership within just two or three years and maintained high growth over the next decade.

Mr. Ni: We became national leaders in our second year of operation. In our first year, we generated 50 million yuan, while Chunhua had about 300,000 units worth a billion yuan. By the second year, Chunhua reached 150 million yuan, and we hit 200 million, making us the industry leaders.

## Denny's Insight: Key Decisions and Success Factors

Looking back over these ten years, it seems that Kingclean faced no significant obstacles and experienced smooth growth with high rates of expansion. What do you attribute this success to? Were there specific decisions that proved particularly effective?

Mr. Ni: Our rapid rise was due to our early focus on innovation and quality. We outperformed competitors like Ningbo Fuda and Shanghai Joy, who struggled to gain traction.

The key lies in continuous improvement and a commitment to excellence.

## Innovation Leadership and Business Strategy

General Ni believes that most companies follow others rather than lead by innovating based on user needs. As an early pioneer in the industry, he reduced motor costs from 125 RMB to 80 RMB while increasing performance and lifespan by 50%. He introduced the first Dragon Cyclone high-capacity vacuum cleaner, the first handheld high-power vacuum cleaner, and the first upright cyclone vacuum cleaner. These innovations led to high sales volumes, but following companies would always be a step behind with declining profits.

Innovation is key; only through continuous research can we develop products that meet user needs while ensuring buyers make a profit. General Ni’s philosophy was to produce European-quality products at Chinese costs. The company emphasizes three core areas in product development: industrial design, technological innovation, and quality assurance. Industrial design should be modern and future-oriented, as it is the face of the product. Technological innovation aims to enhance performance and address user pain points. Quality ensures low maintenance rates and repeat purchases.

General Ni stresses that focusing solely on buyers or users alone will not sustain a business. A balance must be struck where both parties benefit—users want products they can rely on, while buyers need profitable sales. The company’s approach is to create value through innovation rather than cost-cutting. For example, adding one dollar in costs could result in ten dollars in retail price increase, ensuring higher margins for the company and its partners.

### Challenges and Growth

General Ni discusses the challenges faced by Kingclean over the years. Despite economic downturns like the 2008 financial crisis and subsequent economic slowdowns from 2011 to 2012, General Ni believes that difficulties are shared across all industries. The current pandemic initially impacted sales but has since led to a recovery in growth.

### Product Development

General Ni explains that product development is driven by user insights and technological advancements. User feedback helps identify pain points and unmet needs, which must be translated into actionable product ideas. While the team executes these plans, General Ni provides strategic direction and technical guidance when needed.

### Innovation Strategy

Innovating for long-term growth requires a dual focus on users and technology. Understanding user needs through market research is essential but not sufficient; technological breakthroughs are necessary to identify new opportunities. General Ni emphasizes that innovation should be driven by observing the world and understanding technological trends, rather than sitting in an office.

### Competitive Advantage

General Ni compares Kingclean’s approach with that of companies like Dyson. While Dyson has a strong brand and marketing presence, its products have room for improvement. For instance, half of Dyson users are dissatisfied, presenting opportunities to address their pain points. General Ni argues that competition is not about one-time success but long-term growth. Just as the Chinese army overcame initial disadvantages in the Anti-Japanese War through continuous development and adaptation, Kingclean can achieve similar success by focusing on innovation and brand building.

### Brand Building

General Ni acknowledges that while Dyson has a strong global presence, Kingclean is still developing its brand recognition. Over the past decade, Kingclean’s brand value and user awareness have grown significantly. The company plans to focus on enhancing brand recognition through strategic marketing efforts, learning from Dyson’s successful brand-building strategies.

In summary, General Ni’s vision for Kingclean involves continuous innovation, a balanced approach between users and buyers, and a long-term strategy focused on addressing market needs while maintaining competitive advantage.

## Denny on Robotic Vacuums and Beyond

### Why Does Kingclean's Mojie Focus on Upright Vacuum Cleaners?

Ni, the CEO of Kingclean, explains that their Mojie brand positions itself in the upright vacuum cleaner market. This differs from mainstream domestic or Dyson-style vacuums. "We aim to create a differentiated brand," Ni says. "I always tell my internal team to learn from Germany's four cars: Mercedes, BMW, Audi, and Porsche. They all target high-end markets, but if their features are the same, only one can stand out; the rest will fail."

Nee elaborates on how these brands represent different categories: "Mercedes is for business executives with drivers, while BMW caters to young professionals seeking driving fun. In Germany, you rarely see a working professional driving a Porsche." Audi, he notes, is more modest and represents middle managers or civil servants. "In German offices, the most common car is still Audi, followed by BMW; Mercedes and Porsche are less seen."

This differentiation allows brands like Kingclean to stand out from competitors such as Dyson, Philips, and Electrolux. "We can't be like Xiaomi or Dyson," Ni says. "We need our own brand characteristics. That's why we designed the upright model with a focus on portability."

### Brand Characteristics of Kingclean

Kingclean positions itself as an upscale brand that champions innovation to enhance quality of life, Ni explains. This involves two key points: using technology to meet high-end user needs and emphasizing portability in their products. "Just like how two sodas or two fast-food chains are different," he says, "the principle behind branding is the same. If Kingclean's brand doesn't have a unique selling point, it will be meaningless."

### Marketing Strategies for New Products

Nee discusses future marketing strategies: "We need to focus our efforts on highlighting portability through various channels such as traditional media, high-speed rail advertisements, TV commercials, and online content marketing. We want customers who value ease in home cleaning to think of Kingclean when they need a vacuum cleaner."

### Future Goals for the Upright Vacuum Cleaner Category

Nee envisions Kingclean becoming a representative brand in this category. "The market is divided into handheld, upright, and robotic vacuums," he says. "In the future, these three categories will likely dominate the market." While only Kingclean currently focuses on uprights, Ni believes that handheld models may consolidate into fewer brands.

Nee notes that China's vacuum cleaner penetration rate is still low but has a promising outlook: "If you have hard floors or tiles, a mop is sufficient. But with soft furnishings like carpets and rugs, a vacuum cleaner becomes essential." As living standards rise, the use of vacuum cleaners will increase, particularly cordless models which will accelerate product updates.

### Future Trends in Vacuum Cleaners

Nee predicts that while vacuum cleaners may not change drastically, they can evolve into various forms. "A vacuum cleaner is more versatile than a microwave," he says. "We have handhelds, uprights, and floor washers, each with unique applications." He envisions a future where the market will be dominated by three major brands.

### Product Development

Nee mentions that Kingclean plans to expand into smaller categories: "We'll develop both handheld models and uprights," he says. "For handhelds, we focus on reducing the strain on users' arms."

### The Role of Influencer Marketing

Regarding influencer marketing, Ni views it as a form of traffic-driven marketing rather than value-based marketing. "Influencers bring significant traffic but at high costs," he explains. "While they can boost sales in the short term, their impact is temporary and difficult to sustain."

This discussion highlights Kingclean's strategic approach to positioning its Mojie brand within the competitive vacuum cleaner market, emphasizing innovation and portability while exploring new product categories and marketing strategies.

## The Challenges and Opportunities in the Cleaning Robot Market

For instance, offering the lowest price online is a one-time or two-time deal that cannot be sustained. Once such products are launched, they can quickly become obsolete as star influencers take over, leading to diluted traffic. Over time, consumers may grow weary of low prices, making it difficult for this strategy to generate long-term value. These are impulse purchases, so high single-item prices do not work; the majority of sales now fall below $500.

Brand marketing fundamentally needs to focus on value creation, spreading the genuine benefits that products bring to users. Without this, it is merely a short-term strategy. We do not shy away from influencer collaborations but rely more on our own brand strength.

### The Evolution of Brushless Vacuum Cleaners

Currently, we are in an era dominated by brushless vacuum cleaners, with motor technology being the core component. While LEK has been at the forefront in this area, how can it maintain its leadership as other companies catch up?

Ning: Continuous innovation and pursuit of better performance and lighter weight are key to staying ahead. However, there comes a point where further breakthroughs become less significant.

I remember 20 years ago when air conditioners were competing on energy efficiency; today, no one does that anymore because the technology has reached its limit. The theoretical maximum is already set, so improvements are minimal and indiscernible to users. In the future, it won't be about who performs better in terms of performance since differences will be negligible. Users may not even notice these changes.

For instance, a motor weighing one kilogram was reduced to 300 grams, which was significant. Further reductions from 200 grams to 100 grams and then another 10 grams offer diminishing returns for the user experience. Therefore, while technological progress is possible, it has its limits when it comes to user perception.

### The Future of Vacuum Cleaner Motors

Dyson's early motor superiority may have given it an edge, but if that advantage diminishes, other brands will erode its market share. It’s not just about the motor; product flaws can naturally divert customers elsewhere. For example, Apple was once highly regarded, but users now find alternatives equally good, leading to a loss of customer base.

### The Limits of Motor Speed

Ning: Future development in motors is less about speed because higher speeds come with bearing issues. Why did ordinary motors go from 40,000 to 100,000 RPM? It was for smaller size and lighter weight; a difference between one kilogram and two kilograms was significant. But reducing by another 100 grams is not meaningful since it’s just two ounces.

Currently, the motor industry has reached a stage where further advancements are minor improvements that users cannot perceive. Therefore, creating value means generating benefits that users can actually recognize.

### Soft Pack Lithium Batteries

Ning: Soft pack lithium batteries do not offer significant advantages over 18750 and 21700 models in terms of volume-to-energy ratio. Core technology remains with manufacturers like Sony and Samsung. For our needs, we select the best available from these brands.

### The Impact of Trade Wars on Manufacturing

Ning: Trade wars are intermittent; due to them, we have set up factories in Thailand and Vietnam. With tariffs lifted, we bought land in Thailand but haven't built a factory yet. Production is ongoing in Vietnam, though at low volume since there has been no significant tax increase. Compared to China, costs remain lower.

If trade wars escalate with 25% taxes, we might move production overseas. Currently, we are prepared for both scenarios—increased tariffs mean more production in Vietnam; otherwise, focus on domestic operations.

### The Future of Trade Wars

Ning: Given America's efforts to curb China’s development, trade wars will likely continue. We need to build manufacturing bases abroad. American business accounts for over $100 million and 30% of our exports, so the impact is significant.

### Summary of LEK's Past and Future

In summary, LEK has relied on innovation-driven growth through technological advancements that create value for customers. This strategy will continue to drive our development in the future. We aim to leverage technology to enhance quality of life while building a lasting brand legacy.
