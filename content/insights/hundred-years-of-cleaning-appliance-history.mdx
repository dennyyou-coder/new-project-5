## History and Brand Evolution of Rowenta

In 1884, Robert Weintraud founded Robert Weintraud GmbH & Co. KG, using the letters from his name to form the brand Rowenta . Today, Rowenta is one of the most significant brands under the French SEB Group.

## 1899 and the Birth of Miele: Pioneers in Vacuum Cleaners

In 1899, the German company Miele & Cie was founded and released its first vacuum cleaner in 1927. This company, known as Maytag in Chinese, went on to become synonymous with high-end vacuum cleaners.

## 1906: The Birth of Nilfisk

In 1906, Peder Andersen Fisker and Hans Marius Nielsen co-founded Nifisk, a name derived from the first names of the two founders—Nielesen's "Nil" and Fisker's "Fisk." In mainland Europe, Nilfisk became the first company to sell vacuum cleaners.

## Early History of Vacuum Cleaners

In 1908, Henry Hoover purchased the patent from Spangler and founded Hoover Company, releasing the Model O vacuum cleaner. Today, the Hoover brand remains an important player in the North American market.

## 1919: Electrolux's Beginnings and Expansion

In 1919, Electrolux began producing vacuum cleaners under the name Lux AB. Through extensive acquisitions, Electrolux emerged as a leading brand in the European market for vacuum cleaners and eventually became a global household appliance giant through a series of mergers and acquisitions.

## Early Innovations in Cleaning Appliances

In 1954, engineers from Shanghai's Yongtaihe Electric Factory, including Shen Yanmin, developed the first domestically produced upright vacuum cleaners with a power rating of 200W and 400W. These machines were later enhanced to include a 200W direct current (DC) version in 1955, primarily for use on international trains traveling between China and the Soviet Union. A total of 372 vacuum cleaners were manufactured.

In 1956, Bissell introduced the manual carpet shampooer, significantly boosting the company's sales by a factor of five.

## Historical Background of Vacuum Cleaner Production in Shanghai

In 1966, the Dachua Hardware Factory was restructured into Shanghai Electrical Three Factory and began producing "Joyful Brand" vacuum cleaners. At that time, the factory employed 115 people and manufactured 2,627 units of vacuum cleaners in 1968.

## History of BSH Home Appliances

In 1967, Robert Bosch GmbH and Siemens AG jointly established BSH Home Appliances. Leveraging their combined strength, BSH grew to become the largest home appliance company in Europe. In 2014, Robert Bosch GmbH acquired a 50% stake in Siemens AG, transforming BSH into a wholly-owned subsidiary.

## 1977: Alan Brazier Founded VAX in the UK

In 1977, Alan Brazier established VAX in the United Kingdom. At one point, VAX was the most popular vacuum cleaner brand in Britain.

## Early Innovations in Cordless Cleaning

In 1979, Black & Decker introduced the Dustbuster, what is widely considered to be the world's first cordless handheld vacuum cleaner. The innovative device sold an impressive one million units that year. Interestingly, the Dustbuster was originally developed by Black & Decker for NASA’s Apollo moon landing mission, intended for use by astronauts in space.

![A Hundred Years of Cleaning Appliance History image 20](/images/insights/hundred-years-of-cleaning-appliance-history-image-020.jpg)

## 1979-1984: Dyson's Pioneering Journey

Between 1979 and 1984, Dyson spent four years experimenting with 5,127 prototypes before finally inventing the first cordless vacuum cleaner. He then took his invention to prominent vacuum cleaner manufacturers such as Black & Decker, Electrolux, and Hoover. At that time, these companies' products were all bagged models, with dust bags being a crucial source of profit. Consequently, they had no interest in distributing a product that would disrupt their business model.

![A Hundred Years of Cleaning Appliance History image 21](/images/insights/hundred-years-of-cleaning-appliance-history-image-021.jpg)

## 1981 Lisbon Exhibition: The Birth of the Smiling Henry

In 1981, at a trade show in Lisbon, Numatic showcased their latest canister vacuum cleaner. During a lull, one of their engineers amused themselves by drawing a smiley face on the machine. Little did they know that this simple addition would make quite an impact. The Henry vacuum cleaner, now adorned with a cheerful grin, became an instant hit due to its whimsical charm.

![A Hundred Years of Cleaning Appliance History image 22](/images/insights/hundred-years-of-cleaning-appliance-history-image-022.jpg)

In 1985, Japanese company Apex collaborated with James Dyson to improve the first-generation DC02 vacuum cleaner. This pink machine, priced at $2,000, was still highly popular and won numerous design awards despite its steep price tag.

## 1985: Hitachi's Pioneer in Indoor Cleaning Robots

In 1985, Hitachi showcased a prototype of an indoor cleaning robot named HCR-00 at the Domotechnica exhibition. This early model featured a gyroscope and ultrasonic radar for positioning.

## 1985: The Birth of Techtronic Industries (TTI)

In 1985, with an initial investment of two million US dollars, Techtronic Industries (TTI) was officially established. The name TTI was inspired by the old company name,ITT, by founder Kong Zhiping. Horst Pudwill was responsible for sales, while Kong Zhiping managed the factory operations.

## Early History of Household Cleaning Appliances in China

In 1986, the Suzhou Vacuum Cleaner Factory produced China's first generation of household vacuum cleaners, known as the Chunhua brand upright vacuum cleaner. Chunhua vacuum cleaners, alongside Great Wall fans, Xiangxuehai refrigerators, and Peacock televisions, became widely popular across the country. The people of Suzhou proudly referred to these products as "the Four Great Dan Acts."

In 1987, Ni Zuguang (later the CEO of Kingclean) transitioned from military service and was transferred to work at the Suzhou Vacuum Cleaner Factory by year-end. He held various positions including Motor Engineer, Deputy Plant Manager of the Motor Sub-plant, and Deputy General Manager of the main plant.

## 1988: The Birth of Chunhua Vacuum Cleaner Factory

In April 12, 1988, the Chunhua Vacuum Cleaner Factory was established. It comprised seven sub-factories specializing in motor manufacturing, injection molding, stamping processing, packaging materials, accessory assembly, and final product assembly. That year, the factory produced over 899,500 units of Chunhua vacuum cleaners. Chunhua Electrical Appliances is often referred to as the the industry training ground of the vacuum cleaner industry, with many prominent figures in the sector having direct ties to the company.

## Legacy and Influence

The legacy of Chunhua Vacuum Cleaner Factory extends far beyond its initial production achievements. It has served as a training ground for numerous industry leaders who have gone on to shape the landscape of home cleaning appliances. Companies like Roborock, Ecovacs, Dreame, Tineco, Narwal, Aiper Intelligent, Beatbot Innovation, WYBOT Technology, Ninebot, Wildland, Husqvarna, Positec, Mammotion, LD Robot, Kingclean, Deerma, Uwant, Puppy, iLife, Smartmi, Picea, 3i, Laifen, DJI, and Insta360 have all drawn inspiration from or directly competed with Chunhua in the realm of robotic cleaning solutions.

## Dyson's Pioneering Effort: DC06

In 2001, Dyson developed the DC06 robot vacuum, which featured an impressive array of technology including 70 sensors, 54 batteries, three processors, two large wheels, and a typical Dyson-style dustbin. Some described it as "95% robot, 5% vacuum cleaner." Despite its advanced features, the DC06 was too bulky and heavy for practical use, and faced significant production challenges. Additionally, with potential prices reaching up to $6,000, it never made it to market.


In 2001, LG released the Roboking robot vacuum, which in terms of size did not fall short of Dyson's DC06. This, however, was clearly not an optimal solution.

## 2010: The Introduction of Neato XV-11 and the Dawn of Planned Cleaning

In February 2010, the Neato XV-11 robot vacuum was launched at a price point of $399. This marked the first device to utilize a 360-degree rotating laser rangefinder for scanning its surroundings, enabling simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM). Based on this technology, it could plan cleaning routes more efficiently, ushering in an era where robot vacuums transitioned from random to planned cleaning. Additionally, Neato featured the capability of breakpoint recovery; when it automatically returned to charge due to low battery, it would resume cleaning from where it left off.

## Dyson's 2015 Lawsuit and the Evolution of Cordless Vacuum Cleaners

In 2015, Dyson filed a lawsuit against European giants Siemens and Bosch for alleged cheating in EU vacuum cleaner energy efficiency tests. According to Dyson, Siemens and Bosch used sensors to send signals to the motors of their vacuum cleaners, enabling them to achieve an AAAA rating in test environments with no dust. In real-world usage, these devices might only reach E or F ratings.

In 2015, Dyson launched the Dyson V6 cordless vacuum cleaner, marking a significant shift in the industry. The new model featured digital motors capable of reaching 120,000 RPM—far surpassing the previous standard of around 25,000 RPM for typical motors. This innovation spurred a significant growth in cordless vacuum cleaner products, with annual increases of 30-40%.

## 2015: iRobot's Roomba 980 Pioneers Advanced Navigation Technology

In September 2015, iRobot introduced the latest model of its robot vacuum, the Roomba 980 (priced at $899). This new product incorporated the company’s most advanced navigation technology to date: iAdapt 2.0. A key component of this innovation was VSLAM (Visual Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) combined with global planning capabilities.

Unlike previous models that relied on laser-based LiDAR for navigation, the Roomba 980 featured a camera mounted at approximately a 45-degree angle above its top surface. The images captured by this camera were processed through localization algorithms to identify key features within the environment. As the program ran, the robot simultaneously built an environmental map and estimated its position relative to that map. This allowed for precise cleaning paths and achieved high levels of cleaning coverage.
