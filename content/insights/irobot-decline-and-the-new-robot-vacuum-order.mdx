iRobot is a global benchmark in the consumer robotics sector, often referred to as the "pioneer" of robot vacuums. Its development and technological innovations have significantly shaped the landscape of smart home technology. Founded by American MIT robotics experts in 1990 in Delaware, USA, iRobot's headquarters are now located in Bedford, Massachusetts. The company went public on NASDAQ in 2005. Initially, iRobot focused on research and development of military and specialized robots. Its PackBot series was involved in major projects such as the exploration of the Great Pyramid of Giza and investigations into the Gulf oil spill, and it also performed bomb disposal tasks in Afghanistan, with over 5,000 military robots deployed.
The launch of the Roomba robot vacuum in 2002 marked a strategic shift for the company, entering the consumer robotics market and ushering in a new era of home cleaning robots. The global cumulative sales have exceeded 40 million units. In 2010, the introduction of the Braava mopping robot further expanded its product range, offering an integrated whole-home cleaning solution that combines sweeping and mopping. iRobot also developed derivative products like Scooba washing robots to form a comprehensive solution for floor cleaning.
According to data from the commercial institution Euromonitor, in 2012, iRobot held over 80% of the global market share and was the world's largest robot vacuum company. However, with the rise of domestic brands in China, its market share has been declining.

In recent years, with the rise of Chinese robot vacuums, their performance and user experience have surpassed iRobot's products. Under intense competition from Chinese companies, coupled with strategic errors, lack of innovation, and a narrow product line, iRobot has been on a declining trajectory since reaching its peak revenue of $1.565 billion in 2021. In 2022, the company faced operational difficulties due to an antitrust review, failed attempts to sell to Amazon, and directly reported a net loss of $286 million for the year. Since then, iRobot's performance has continued to decline, resulting in consecutive annual losses.
To address these challenges, iRobot has implemented several measures. From December 2023 to December 28, 2024, iRobot reduced its workforce by nearly 51%, with the total number of employees decreasing to 541 (as of June 28, 2025, the latest figure is 509). To further reduce costs and improve efficiency, iRobot has transferred its manufacturing facilities to Shenzhen Sanshan. This move could save iRobot between $80 million and $100 million in product costs and between $20 million and $40 million in research and marketing expenses.
To boost market confidence in the company, iRobot launched eight new Roomba robot vacuums in March 2025, which is the largest product launch in its 30-year history.

However, in its financial report, iRobot stated that "due to potential factors including but not limited to consumer demand, competition, macroeconomic conditions, and tariff policies, there is no guarantee of the success of new product launches." This information led to a significant drop of 36% in its stock price on the same day.
Four months later, in July, iRobot launched the Roomba® Max 705 combined robot + AutoWash™ base station, which is the company's most advanced two-in-one cleaning system.

According to the latest financial report from iRobot for Q2 of 2025, despite a year-over-year decline in revenue by 23.3% to $127.6 million, net loss decreased significantly from $70.6 million last year to $22.8 million. This improvement is mainly attributed to an increase in gross margin from 16.5% to 30.0%, driven by the sales of new products and a better cost structure. However, the company still faces severe liquidity challenges, with total inventory at the end of Q2 2025 amounting to $88.2 million, down 13% year-over-year.
Furthermore, according to IDC data, iRobot, the former market leader in global smart home cleaning robots, has fallen out of the top five in Q2 2025, being replaced by CloudWhin.

According to external reports, in mid-July, iRobot faced securities fraud charges. Investors sued Wall Street, and Levi & Korsinsky represented iRobot Corporation investors in a class-action lawsuit. These investors suffered losses between January 29, 2024, and March 11, 2025, due to alleged securities fraud by iRobot. The lawsuit alleges that the defendants made false statements or failed to disclose material facts: iRobot overstated the extent to which its restructuring plan helped maintain stability after Amazon's acquisition was terminated; concealed key information about iRobot's difficulty in achieving profitability as an independent company and doubts over its ongoing viability, leading to materially false and misleading public statements.

iRobot's stock price has fallen from a high of $197 to around $4, with a market value of only approximately $100 million.

▲ iRobot Stock Price Trend

Under pressure from performance and market skepticism, another report suggests that iRobot is set to announce its delisting. Post-delisting, it plans to merge with the pool cleaning robot giant Dolphin, aiming to create a super channel alliance covering "indoor cleaning + outdoor maintenance" and become the global leader. However, industry experts generally do not view this combination favorably, referring to it as the twilight of former dominance.

Earlier this year, CEO Gary Cohen stated that if conditions did not improve, iRobot could go bankrupt within 12 months.

Time is running out for iRobot.
